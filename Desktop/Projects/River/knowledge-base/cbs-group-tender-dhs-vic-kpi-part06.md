Jim Ellwood

Project Director & Strategic Asset Management Lead

Qualifications: BEng (Aerospace Hons), RMIT  |  MMaintRelEng, Monash University  |  MIEAust  |  Member, Asset Management Council

Professional Profile

Jim is a senior consulting engineer with over 30 years of experience in the analysis and management of complex, safety-related systems and projects across the defence, aerospace, utilities, infrastructure and transport industries. He has extensive expertise in reliability, availability, maintainability and safety (RAMS) analysis, asset management strategy and planning, and systems engineering.

Jim’s career spans whole-of-life asset management support, the establishment and management of reliability and maintenance programs, and the governance and delivery of large project portfolios. He has also led the development and implementation of new business strategies and operating models for organisations across multiple industries.

His direct experience with Transport for NSW on the Western Harbour Tunnel, Sydney Harbour Tunnel and M6 Tunnel asset management contracts provides a strong foundation for the maintenance model assessment, KPI framework development and engineering analysis requirements of this engagement.

Role on This Engagement

As the Project Director & Strategic Asset Management Lead, Jim will lead the technical assessment of maintenance models, systems and engineering practices across health service assets. He will provide specialist expertise in reliability and maintainability analysis, maintenance strategy development and KPI design, supporting the Phase 1 diagnostic, Phase 2 benchmarking and Phase 4 pilot evaluation.

Key responsibilities on this project include:

Leading the Phase 1 technical assessment of maintenance models, systems and processes across pilot health services

Developing the maintenance KPI benchmarking methodology and analytical framework for Phase 2

Providing specialist reliability and maintainability expertise to inform KPI design and performance targets

Supporting the development of tailored implementation pathways based on engineering assessment findings

Leading the Phase 4 pilot evaluation, including KPI data collection, performance monitoring and lessons-learned documentation

Contributing to the development of the Code of Maintenance Standards and engineering guidance materials
