ESS Side Deed Downer Comments (23 August 2022) – Sydney Trains Response

We set out in the table below our responses to Downer's comments on and requested amendments to the ESS Side Deed. We have not included the minor tidy up changes requested by Downer in the table below. These have been incorporated directly into the revised draft of the ESS Side Deed.

The clause references in the table below are to the clauses in the revised draft of the ESS Side Deed.

ESS Side Deed – Additional Amendments by Sydney Trains

In addition to the changes we have noted in the table above as being incorporated into the revised draft of the ESS Side Deed, we have also made the following amendments of note. We have not included minor tidy up changes (such as corrections to cross-references and definitions) in the table below. These have been incorporated directly into the revised draft of the ESS Side Deed.

|  | Definitions of 'ACP's Fee' and 'Net ESC Sale Proceeds' | For simplicity:
the 'Supplier's Fee' should embed the ACP's Fee and all other associated costs; and
the 'Net ESC Sale Proceeds' should be calculated as the ESC Sale Proceeds minus the Supplier's Fee (rather than the ACP's Fee). | The proposed amendments suggest that the ACP will pass through to Downer the total amount of ESC Sale Proceeds (without deduction for the ACP's Fee), with Downer to then repay the ACP for its costs.

|  | Cl 5.1(b)(ii)(A) | On advice of its ESS Consultant that this step is not required, Downer has deleted the requirement that NGE applies to the Scheme Administrator as soon as reasonably practicable after the Effective Date to become accredited as a certificate provider in relation to the implementation of the HVAC Software Update as a Recognised Energy Saving Activity. | Based on the current list of ACPs published on IPART's website, NGE is already registered as an ACP in respect of a number of RESAs. In any event, we have amended this step to say that to the extent that NGE is not already accredited in relation to the implementation of the HVAC Software Update as a RESA, NGE must become accredited for this activity. | Okay. Closed. |

|  | Cl 5.1(b)(ii)(B) | Downer must procure that NGE creates ESCs promptly after the energy savings occur and in any event within the timeframe prescribed by the Act, Regulation or Rule (as applicable).

Downer notes that the current program requires 12 months of data, plus modelling and auditor approval (approx. 12-18 months in total). | We have replaced 'promptly' with 'as soon as reasonably practicable', to account for the practical modelling and audit requirements.
For completeness, we note that the Act states that ESCs may be created by no later than 6 months after the end of the year in which the energy savings occur. | Okay. Closed 
DC - Okay |

|  | Cl 5.1(b)(ii)(D) | Downer has requested clarification of the requirement that NGE applies to the Scheme Administrator for registration of the creation of the ESCs, noting that the act of 'registering' is what creates the ESCs. | Our reading of the Act is that the act of 'creating' an ESC is separate from, and different to, the registration of that ESC. Specifically, s46 of Sch 4A to the Act states that:
an ESC has no force or effect until its creation is registered by the Scheme Administrator in the register of ESCs; and
an application for registration of the creation of an ESC may be made to the Scheme Administrator by an ACP.
There are also a number of provisions in the Act that refer to 'creation' only, without 'registration'. Our drafting was intended to reflect this distinction.
In any event, based on Downer's view that the act of registering the ESCs is what creates them, both obligations (to create and register) should be satisfied by a single act.
We have not made any changes to the revised draft of the Side Deed in this respect. | DC - We will need to confirm with NGE that they can comply with the requirements of clause 5.1 as per the latest amendments. This is in progress. |

DC – May be worth increasing this to 12 months (subject to CBS advice). We will need to confirm with NGE that they can comply with the requirements of clause 5.1 as per the latest amendments. This is in progress. |

|  | Cl 5.1(c) | Downer has requested clarification on the purpose of this clause, which relates to Downer providing information and reasonable access to the SGT Fleet to a representative of the ESS or a member of the ESS Audit Services Panel. | The template Nomination Form published on IPART's website provides that the original energy saver (TfNSW) agrees to provide information regarding the energy saving activities, and reasonable access to the implementation sites (SGT Fleet), to a representative of the ESS or a member of the ESS Audit Services Panel, upon their request. |  | Cl 5.4(e) and (f) | Firstly, in relation to TfNSW's right to revoke NGE's nomination as the ACP at any time, Downer has proposed that:

In relation to the second point, the template Nomination Form published on IPART's website states that the nomination entitles the ACP to create and own all energy savings in respect of the energy saving activities. The 'ownership' language was included for this reason, and we have therefore retained it. | Okay. Closed. |

|  | Parties, definition of 'Related Entity', cl 5.4(b) and 18 | Counterparty to Side Deed | We have made TfNSW the counterparty to the Side Deed, rather than Sydney Trains, to align with legislative requirements. The counterparty to the Side Deed should be the entity that is the 'Purchaser' and 'Energy Saver' under the ESS Rule. This is the entity that purchases the services from Downer that enable the relevant energy savings to be made, which in our view is TfNSW, as the counterparty to the SGT TLS Deed with Downer under which the variation order for the implementation of the HVAC Software Update has been issued.
