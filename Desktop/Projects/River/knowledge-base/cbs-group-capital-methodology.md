Summary of the project, product, framework 

A short paragraph or dot-point summary of the key aspects of the project or framework (maximum 100 words)

The CAPITAL (Commercial Asset Performance, Infrastructure Tailoring And Lifecycle) framework represents an innovative long-term asset management and commercial model designed for critical infrastructure. 

Initially implemented for the Western Harbour Tunnel and Sydney Harbour Tunnel, it establishes a 30-year, fixed-fee asset management, operations and maintenance framework that optimises asset performance while balancing risk allocation. 

CAPITAL incorporates formal collaboration mechanisms, and drives design simplifications in new and existing assets – ensuring clear traceability of customer needs and hazards to design features and value – formalising options and analysis and rewarding an innovative asset manager.

It ensures long-term community value is protected by; applying commercial levers like lane-rental fees to reflect the impact of maintenance, defining strict asset condition requirements as guardrails for asset value, and provisioning sophisticated lifecycle funding mechanisms.

Description of project 

addressing the assessment criteria (maximum 1000 words)

The nominated framework (CAPITAL) is the solution developed for the asset management, operation and maintenance of the Western Harbour Tunnel (WHT) and of the pre-existing Sydney Harbour Tunnel (SHT).

The solution was required to:

Meet Treasury Objectives: Ensure the NSW Treasury Asset Management Policy objectives were met - operations phase costs are known and fixed for the long term – noting that the WHT was at Reference Design only.

Operations Phase Voice: Establish an authoritative voice for the asset custodian during project design and delivery – ensure that operational cost and asset performance considerations retain priority in the context of larger project costs and looming deadlines.

Trade-off Mechanics: Provide a structured framework for the necessary project and operations phase trade-offs between capex, opex, and asset performance

Incentivise Strategic Asset Management: Provide market incentives to apply intelligent asset management techniques, while protecting asset condition, asset performance, and contract performance

Build and Sustain Capability: Apply and sustain practical asset management skills capability within the market

In this context, the CAPITAL framework was designed and deployed with these key features:

Community Asset Performance: The ‘community cost’ of motorway unavailability calculated and reflected in the lane rental fee charged whenever maintenance is conducted.  The mechanism ensures community impact has a monetary value that:

can be used to rationalise arguments for / against system resilience and redundancy.

is used to assess the merits of asset maintenance and replacement strategies

A Fixed Fees and Long-Term Incentives: A fixed asset management, operations, and maintenance (including lifecycle) fee over 30 years that provides for cost certainty for government, while allowing asset management innovation benefits (works analysis, asset retirement etc) to flow to the asset managers’ long term benefit. 

Asset Risk Allocation and Condition Assurance: Effective risk allocation is achieved by providing the asset management with asset performance risk, protecting the asset over the entire asset life with clear condition assurance measures, and state retention of high consequence risks beyond the control of the asset manager.

CAPITAL explicitly defines asset condition by asset class - allowing a pre-agreed return condition for the asset.

An asset lifecycle fund is established at contract award, establishing a lifecycle budget for the contract term – aligned with the agreed asset return conditions.

The lifecycle funds are fully redeemable by the Asset Manager:   

When asset return conditions are met, any remaining lifecycle funds are passed to the asset manager.

When asset return conditions are not met, the cost to 'make good' is met by the Asset Manager, who may access any funds remaining in the lifecycle fund.

Design and the Lifecycle Cost Model:  The development of a comprehensive lifecycle cost model encourages design for the best whole-of-life outcome and to manage the fixed fee risk exposure to the asset manager.   The model incorporates maintenance costs, lifecycle works, failure rates and other attributes and is then leveraged to:

Allow a fixed fee to be established for an asset very early in its design

Structure agreements with the asset manager as to the operational cost impacts of design changes

Weigh the merits of design alternates during the WHT design development

Collaboration: With a contract term of over 30 years, formal provisions establish enduring collaborative practices between Transport for NSW and the asset manager.   The ISO 44001 framework is integrated into CAPITAL to ensure both parties are committed to actively investing in well-established long-term collaboration practices.

Degree of originality and ingenuity of solution

The CAPITAL Framework is original, tightly integrating asset management principles with commercial structures to deliver best practice asset performance for the community, within a financial framework that represents un-rivalled value to the state.

Key original and ingenuous elements of the framework include:

Asset Management Engagement Before Main Works Contractor: The framework provides a formal commercial role for the asset manager in defining specifications and selecting the contractor and subsequent design.

Lane rental mechanism: The framework implemented a lane rental mechanism that created a measurable link between maintenance activities and community impact. This enabled the WHT designer to rationalise equipment redundancy based on community impact analysis, while providing maintenance contractors with defined financial incentives to minimise disruption. 

Incentivised Asset Management. The framework allows the asset manager to retain cost reductions realised through asset management innovations, provided they continued to meet safety, asset condition and performance requirements.

Lifecycle and Asset Condition Model.  The framework establishes a redeemable lifecycle fund linked to defined asset condition requirements for various asset classes, providing clear parameters for asset performance at contract completion.  The asset manager could access these funds at any time, while bearing the cost of any remedial works if conditions were not met. Furthermore, a Change of Use Sinking Fund was established to support changes is asset use that are unkown but will arise over the term.

Formalised Collaboration.  The CAPITAL Model is the first to fully integrate two international standards: ISO 55001 for asset management and ISO 44001 for collaborative relationships - providing formal provisions for sustained cooperation across the 30-year contract term.

The CAPITAL (Commercial Asset Performance, Infrastructure Tailoring And Lifecycle)
framework represents an innovative long-term asset management and commercial
model designed for critical infrastructure. Initially implemented for the Western
Harbour Tunnel and Sydney Harbour Tunnel, it establishes a 30-year, fixed-fee asset
management, operations and maintenance framework that optimizes asset
performance while balancing risk allocation.


CAPITAL incorporates formal collaboration mechanisms and drives design
simplifications in new and existing assets – ensuring clear traceability of customer
needs and hazards to design features and value. It ensures long-term community
value through commercial levers like lane-rental fees, strict asset condition
requirements, and sophisticated lifecycle funding mechanisms.

The CAPITAL framework was designed to meet multiple objectives: ensuring NSW Treasury Asset Management Policy compliance, establishing an authoritative voice for asset
custodians during design and delivery, providing structured frameworks for project trade-offs, incentivizing strategic asset management, and building sustainable asset management
capability within the market.

This innovative approach integrates ISO 55001 for asset management excellence with ISO 44001 for collaborative business relationships, creating a comprehensive system that
balances technical requirements with commercial incentives over a 30-year timeframe.

A cornerstone of the CAPITAL framework is its innovative approach to quantifying community impact. The 'community cost' of
motorway unavailability is calculated and reflected in lane rental fees charged whenever maintenance is conducted. This
mechanism ensures community impact has a monetary value that can rationalize arguments for system resilience and
redundancy, while also assessing the merits of various asset maintenance and replacement strategies.

By establishing this direct link between maintenance activities and community impact, the framework enables designers to
make informed decisions about equipment redundancy based on community impact analysis. It also provides maintenance
contractors with defined financial incentives to minimize disruption to the public, aligning commercial interests with
community benefit.

The CAPITAL framework establishes a fixed asset management, operations, and maintenance fee over 30 years, providing cost certainty for government while allowing
asset management innovation benefits to flow to the asset manager's long-term benefit. This structure has consistently resulted in fixed fees below industry
benchmarks and government estimates.

The framework incentivizes the asset manager to undertake self-funded analysis in return for cost-base reductions. For example, the asset manager led analysis of
ventilation requirements for the Sydney Harbour Tunnel and identified redundant equipment that could be more cost-effectively maintained or retired. Similarly, the
framework quantified benefits of coordinated road tunnel operations, allowing consolidation of facilities and staff between SHT and M6 Stage 1.

Effective risk allocation is achieved by providing the asset manager with asset performance risk, protecting the asset over its
entire life with clear condition assurance measures, and state retention of high-consequence risks beyond the asset manager's
control. This balanced approach ensures that risks are allocated to the party best positioned to manage them.

The framework also establishes a Change of Use Sinking Fund to support changes in asset use that may arise over the term but are
currently unknown. This forward-thinking provision acknowledges the inevitability of changing requirements over a 30-year
timeframe and provides a mechanism to address them without disrupting the core financial structure.

The development of a comprehensive lifecycle cost model encourages design for the best whole-of-life outcome and helps manage the fixed fee risk exposure to the
asset manager. This model becomes a powerful tool for making informed decisions throughout the project lifecycle, from initial design through decades of operation.



By establishing this model early in the process, the framework enables the asset manager to have meaningful input into design decisions that will affect long-term
operational costs. This early engagement of asset management expertise represents a significant departure from traditional approaches where operational
considerations often take a back seat to construction priorities.

With a contract term of over 30 years, formal provisions establish enduring collaborative practices between Transport for NSW and the asset manager. The ISO 44001 framework is
integrated into CAPITAL to ensure both parties are committed to actively investing in well-established long-term collaboration practices.

The governance structure ensures that project objectives remain true to the framework during inevitable changes to staff, leaders, and organizational priorities. Strategies to manage
organizational alignment gaps are purposefully set and actively reviewed over the contract term, with governance boards used to set shared objectives, promote collaborative
problem-solving, and reduce adversarial behaviors.
