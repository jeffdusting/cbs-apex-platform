Executive summary

Situation

Traditional consulting models incentivise activity over outcomes. Consultants are compensated for time expended and deliverables completed, regardless of whether their recommendations generate tangible benefits. This structural misalignment means the client bears all value risk—paying full fees even when initiatives fail to deliver anticipated improvements.

Complication

Infrastructure asset management encompasses diverse work types with varying risk profiles and value certainty. Some initiatives are exploratory, with outcomes that cannot be guaranteed but offer significant potential value. Others have well-defined scope where deliverables and effort can be reliably estimated. A single engagement model cannot optimally address both situations: applying shared-risk pricing to defined-scope work undervalues consultant expertise, while applying fixed fees to uncertain-outcome work places disproportionate risk on the client.

Question

How can TfNSW engage specialist consultants through a framework that matches engagement models to work characteristics—sharing risk where value is uncertain, while providing cost certainty for defined-scope assignments?

Answer

CBSE—a joint venture combining CBS Group's deep TfNSW integration and proven CAPITAL framework with Egis's global infrastructure expertise—proposes a dual-pathway engagement framework offering genuine flexibility.

The framework provides two complementary options:

Option A: Shared-Risk, Value-Based Partnership — Below-market retainers with value sharing where pre-agreed benefits are realised. Both parties agree on value potential before work commences, co-invest in pursuing that value, and share in the outcomes achieved.

Option B: Traditional Time-Based Engagement — Market rates for defined-scope work, with upper limiting fees agreed with TfNSW. This pathway provides cost certainty for assignments where scope is clear and outcomes are predictable.

This flexibility allows TfNSW to select the most appropriate model for each project based on its specific objectives and risk appetite. CBSE brings the analytical tools, proven methodologies, and global benchmarking capability to accept shared risk with confidence—while offering competitive traditional pricing where that model better serves TfNSW's needs.

We look forward to working through the our seed list of initiatives and the necessary commercial amendments to rapidly enable this model and start driving real value to support the Asset Financial Sustainability (AFS) targets set for the organisation.

Introduction and context

Purpose of this Alternative Proposal

This document outlines an alternative commercial framework to our base proposal of October 2025. In response to our discussions with Transport for NSW concerning innovative commercial models and value-based procurement, we have developed a dual-pathway framework. This framework is designed to cater to the varied requirements of TfNSW's infrastructure portfolio by offering greater flexibility and alignment of interests.

We note that CBS Group has also submitted an alternate proposal for Option A engagement supported by ST Engineering, which follows the same commercial framework presented here. This CBSE proposal offers the added benefit of a blended team combining CBS Group's local expertise with Egis's global engineering capabilities, providing TfNSW with enhanced technical depth and international best practices while maintaining the same innovative commercial structure.

Commercial Flexibility: offering two distinct engagement options that can be selected based on project characteristics

Choice of Model: allows TfNSW to choose between an innovative, value-based model and a traditional, time-based model, depending on the specific needs and risk profile of the engagement

Duel engagement pathway framework

Framework structure

This alternative framework is built on two distinct and complementary engagement pathways, allowing TfNSW to select the most suitable approach for any given initiative.

Option A: Shared-Risk, Value-Based Engagement

This pathway is founded on a partnership model. CBSE accepts a significantly reduced monthly retainer, representing a substantial investment from our side. In return, we participate in the value we help create through a pre-agreed sharing mechanism on validated benefits. This model ensures that both organisations share in the risks and rewards, creating a strong alignment of interests.

Option B: Traditional, Time-Based Engagement

This pathway follows a conventional consultancy model. Services are provided at market rates for a defined scope of work. This option is suitable for projects where the scope and deliverables are clearly defined from the outset and where a traditional risk allocation is preferred. Services are provided at market rates for a defined scope of work, with fixed price or time and materials options available. Work orders do not include value share provisions.

2.2 Selection Criteria

The choice between Option A and Option B would be guided by the nature of the project.

Option A is particularly well-suited for initiatives with the potential for significant value creation, such as long-term transformation programs or projects that require innovative solutions. It is most effective in situations where a deep, collaborative partnership and fully aligned incentives are critical to success.

Option B is the preferred choice for more straightforward engagements. This includes defined-scope studies, technical investigations, or when seeking short-term expert advice. It is also appropriate for engagements where a more conservative, traditional risk allocation is desired.

2.3 Flexibility and Combination

The framework is designed for flexibility, allowing TfNSW to:

Employ different options for different projects concurrently

Transition between the two options with an agreed-upon notice period

Combine both options within a single large-scale program

Initiate with a pilot of Option A before committing to a broader rollout

Option A - shared risk, value-based engagement

Retainer structure

The shared-risk model is founded on a retainer structure designed to create mutual commitment while providing certainty of minimum service levels. The base retainer for this model is set at a level significantly below the typical market rate for a comparable team, reflecting a joint investment in the success of the engagement.

Base Retainer Terms:

Fixed Monthly Amount: $10,000 per team (with a maximum of three specialists per team)

Payment Schedule: Monthly in advance, by the first business day of the month

Non-Refundable Nature: Payments are guaranteed to CBSE, irrespective of the value achieved, to ensure mutual commitment

Team Composition: A typical team includes a senior lead, a technical specialist, and an analyst

Availability Commitment: A minimum of 15 working days per month will be dedicated to TfNSW

Minimum Deliverables Within Retainer:

To provide certainty and demonstrate ongoing value, each monthly retainer includes:

Monthly performance dashboard and benefit tracking report

Quarterly opportunity assessment identifying new value creation possibilities

Access to CATALYST 7™ tools and templates for TfNSW use on engaged projects

Monthly stakeholder briefing and strategic planning session

Continuous monitoring of asset performance against established baselines

This retainer structure represents a significant investment from CBSE, as it forgoes a substantial portion of standard market revenue. This arrangement ensures that our focus is on generating value that will lead to a successful partnership, rather than on maximising billable hours.

Value Share Mechanism

Value share is calculated and paid on an initiative-by-initiative basis. For each value-generating opportunity, CBS Group and TfNSW will execute a Project Schedule that documents the specific commercial terms applicable to that initiative.

Project Schedule Requirements

Before work commences on any value-based initiative, both parties will agree and document the following in a Project Schedule (Appendix A):

Scope: The specific activities, deliverables and outcomes for the initiative

Baseline: The current state costs or performance metrics against which value will be measured

Measurement Method: The methodology for calculating value, aligned with the CATALYST 7™ framework

Duration: The start date, end date, and value measurement period

Team Composition: The number and composition of teams assigned to the initiative

Value Share: The percentage payable to CBS Group, including any applicable cap

Value Share Calculation

The value share payable for each initiative is calculated as follows:

Value Share = (Validated Benefits - Cumulative Retainer Paid) × Agreed Value Share Percentage

The value share is payable only after the measurement outcomes are confirmed through the validation process documented in the Project Schedule.

3.5 Initiative Selection and Formalisation Process

The process for progressing from opportunity identification to project commencement under Option A follows these steps:

Step 1: Opportunity Identification

Either party may identify a potential value-generating opportunity. CBSE will maintain an ongoing pipeline of opportunities through strategic analysis and engagement with TfNSW stakeholders.

Step 2: Joint Scoping

For each prioritised opportunity, CBSE and TfNSW will jointly:

Define the problem statement and anticipated outcome

Establish baseline costs or performance metrics

Agree the measurement methodology aligned with CATALYST 7™

Determine the number of teams and resource commitment required

Agree the start date, end date, and value measurement period

Negotiate the value share percentage and any applicable cap

Step 3: Project Schedule Execution

The agreed terms will be documented in a Project Schedule, which forms a binding appendix to the Master Agreement. Work on the initiative will not commence until both parties have executed the Project Schedule.

Step 4: Project Commencement

Upon execution of the Project Schedule:

Monthly retainers for the agreed number of teams commence

Baseline data is formally documented and certified

The project team is mobilised in accordance with the agreed composition

Step 5: Value Realisation and Payment

Upon completion of the initiative:

Value is measured in accordance with the agreed methodology

Benefits are validated through the applicable validation tier

Value share is calculated and paid as documented in the Project Schedule

Hybrid Program Example

5.1 Hybrid Program Example

For a major asset management transformation program, a hybrid approach employs both engagement pathways with clear administrative separation:

Option A Components (via Project Schedules):

Strategy development and value identification phases

Innovation initiatives with measurable value potential

Each component governed by its own Project Schedule with agreed baseline, measurement methodology, and value share terms

Technical investigations and feasibility studies

Each component governed by a traditional work order with fixed price or time and materials terms

Progressive Engagement Example

A progressive engagement model could be used to move from a traditional to a value-based model over time. A project could begin with a feasibility study conducted under Option B to define the scope and potential value. Based on the findings, the engagement could then transition to an Option A pilot project to test the value-based approach on a smaller scale. If successful, this could be scaled up to a full deployment under Option A.

Parallel Engagement Example

In situations where there are multiple, concurrent needs, the two options can be run in parallel. For example, Option A could be used for a tunnel operations project where the goal is to identify and implement cost savings, while Option B could be used for a bridge assessment that requires a standard technical review. This allows for the most appropriate commercial model to be applied to each individual stream of work.

TfNSW is granted no rights to use CBS Group's pre-existing IP except as specifically required for the purposes of the engagement under this agreement."

This clause establishes that CBS Group IP remains with CBS Group, ST Engineering IP remains with ST Engineering, TfNSW receives usage rights for NSW transport purposes, no transfer of IP occurs between parties without consent, and validation methodologies remain confidential.

Why choose this alternative framework 

Key Framework Enhancements

This alternative framework incorporates several enhancements designed to address common concerns with innovative commercial models while maintaining the benefits of a value-based approach:

Refined Intellectual Property Framework
We have structured IP arrangements to protect CBS Group's proprietary methodologies while providing TfNSW with perpetual rights to use all project-specific applications and deliverables for internal purposes. This balances protection of our core IP with TfNSW's need for ongoing access to project outputs.

Performance-Based Contract Term
The framework features a 2-year initial term with clear performance gates at 6, 12, and 18 months, allowing both parties to assess progress and value delivery before committing to extensions. This provides security while maintaining flexibility.

Enhanced Retainer Value
The monthly retainer includes defined minimum deliverables such as performance dashboards, opportunity assessments, and strategic planning sessions, providing certainty of service levels regardless of value share outcomes.

Streamlined Validation Process
Pre-agreed templates and expedited review processes for benefits under $250,000 reduce administrative burden while maintaining rigorous validation standards. Common benefit types will have standardised calculation methodologies agreed upfront.

Balanced Termination Provisions
A sliding scale approach to termination payments (6 months Year 1, 4 months Year 2, 2 months thereafter) fairly recognises the diminishing investment recovery need over time while protecting CBSE's initial investment.
