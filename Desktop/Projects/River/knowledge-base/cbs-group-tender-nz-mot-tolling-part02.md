| Client: Transport for NSW (TfNSW)
Period: 2020-2021
Role: Lead Transaction Advisors
Context:
Our team was engaged by Transport for NSW to lead a comprehensive Market Interactive Process (MIP) for the Western Harbour Tunnel project, a major toll road infrastructure initiative in Sydney. TfNSW needed to explore the viability and structure of potential concession arrangements while balancing public value and private sector interest.

Scope:
We designed and executed a structured market sounding process that engaged over 30 market participants including major concessionaires (Transurban, Fulton Hogan/Egis), financiers, toll road operators, and maintenance contractors. Our engagement tested various concession models, risk allocations, payment mechanisms, and commercial structures.

Approach:

Strategic Planning Phase & Engagement
Developed an MIP strategy, information memorandum, and tailored questionnaires, leveraging CBS Group’s tolling expertise.
Developed a detailed market sounding methodology and communication strategy
Created a comprehensive information memorandum outlining the project scope, objectives, and potential concession opportunities
Designed bespoke questionnaires addressing key commercial and technical considerations
Established a robust probity framework to ensure transparency and fairness
Market Engagement Phase:
Facilitated workshops in Sydney, synthesizing feedback from diverse stakeholders into a cohesive framework.
Coordinated and facilitated three rounds of interactive sessions with participants
Conducted one-on-one confidential discussions focused on commercial structures, risk appetite, and deal parameters
Maintained detailed records of all interactions while preserving commercial confidentiality
Managed data room containing critical project information for market assessment
Analysis and Recommendation Phase:
Delivered a report recommending a hybrid concession model balancing upfront capital generation with long-term asset performance
Synthesized market feedback on optimal concession structures, contracting models, and risk allocations
Analyzed commercial viability of different packaging approaches (bundled vs. unbundled concessions)
Evaluated market views on optimal concession length and payment mechanisms
Tested innovative commercial structures including asset manager models and hybrid concession arrangements

Key Outcomes:
Informed TfNSW’s procurement strategy, achieving a structure that maximized value while ensuring integration with adjacent assets. The MIP report was praised for its clarity and actionable insights.
Identified an optimal risk allocation framework that maximized value for money while maintaining strong market interest
Demonstrated potential for 15-20% reduction in lifecycle costs through early operator engagement in design
Provided clear evidence that bundling lifecycle and maintenance responsibilities delivers better value through synergies
Uncovered and addressed critical "deal breaker" issues early in the process, avoiding procurement failures
Developed a comprehensive commercial framework incorporating market feedback on payment structures, contract length, and risk transfer
Delivered detailed report with specific recommendations on concession structuring, packaging, and market approach

Value Delivered:
The MIP informed TfNSW's procurement strategy for the Western Harbour Tunnel, leading to a commercial structure that balanced government objectives for upfront capital generation with long-term asset performance. Our approach identified significant opportunities to enhance value for money while maintaining strong market interest from leading toll road operators and financiers.
