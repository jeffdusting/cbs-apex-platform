On behalf of CBS Group, I am pleased to submit our alternate offer for the provision of Asset Management services to Transport for NSW. This proposal responds to our discussions regarding access to specialist products and is designed to operate in parallel with either our conforming base offer or Alternate 1 offer, providing a supplemental engagement mechanism to meet TfNSW's asset management and value creation needs over the term.

The offer provides TfNSW with access to a suite of CBS Group specialist products developed through our extensive experience delivering asset management outcomes for major infrastructure. These products represent proven methodologies, analytical tools and frameworks that have contributed to more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years.

Product Licensing

Each product may be acquired as a perpetual licence, providing TfNSW with ongoing access and the ability to deploy the product independently across the organisation. This pathway suits products where TfNSW seeks long-term capability uplift and internal ownership.

Product as a Service

Alternatively, products may be accessed on a service basis comprising an implementation fee and monthly usage charges. This pathway provides flexibility for time-limited initiatives, pilot programs or where TfNSW prefers operational expenditure over capital investment.

These amendments address intellectual property provisions, licensing arrangements and service-based commercial structures not contemplated in the standard terms. Acceptance of these amendments enables the product framework to operate effectively alongside the primary engagement.

This framework provides TfNSW with the flexibility to select the most appropriate commercial approach for each product, enabling rapid deployment of proven tools to support asset management objectives.

We have enclosed a detailed proposal outlining a selection of current products, licensing options, proposed contract amendments, and the value each product is designed to create.  Thank you for the opportunity to present this alternate offer. We look forward to discussing it with you further.

Executive summary

Situation: Delivering effective asset management for complex infrastructure requires sophisticated tools, methodologies and analytical frameworks. Developing these capabilities internally demands significant investment in time, expertise and iterative refinement. Traditional consultant engagements provide access to such tools on a project-by-project basis, but the intellectual property remains with the consultant—requiring repeated engagement to access the same capabilities.

Complication: TfNSW faces growing pressure to deliver asset management outcomes efficiently while building sustainable internal capability. Reliance on consultant-held tools creates dependency and limits knowledge transfer. Procuring or developing equivalent products independently would require substantial investment with uncertain outcomes. Standard contract terms do not contemplate product licensing or service-based access models, creating a structural barrier to accessing proven tools through mechanisms suited to their nature.

Response: CBS Group proposes an alternate engagement model designed to operate in parallel with either the conforming base offer or Alternate 1 offer. This model provides access to our suite of specialist products through a flexible dual-pathway commercial framework with three complementary mechanisms.

Product Licensing provides perpetual access to CBS Group products, enabling TfNSW to deploy proven tools independently across the organisation. This pathway supports long-term capability uplift and internal ownership of critical asset management methodologies.

Product as a Service provides access through an implementation fee and monthly usage charges. This pathway offers flexibility for time-limited initiatives, pilot programs or where operational expenditure is preferred over capital investment. It includes ongoing support and updates for the duration of the service arrangement.

Contract Amendments for Product Access details a set of proposed amendments to accommodate the licensing and usage mechanisms required. These amendments address intellectual property provisions, licensing arrangements and service-based commercial structures not contemplated in the standard terms, enabling the product framework to operate effectively alongside the primary engagement.

We bring a suite of proven asset management products, methodologies and analytical tools that have delivered more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years. It is with this track record that we offer these products with confidence in the value they will deliver.
