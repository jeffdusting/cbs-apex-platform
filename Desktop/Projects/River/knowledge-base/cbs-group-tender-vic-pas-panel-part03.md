## PAS SPC Refresh RFT 2022 - Part D.DOCX

PARTIES

BACKGROUND

The State wishes to appoint the Supplier as one of its strategic suppliers, and put in place a Master Supply Agreement, for the supply of certain Services and / or Deliverables.

This agreement sets out the terms and conditions: 

that govern the general relationship between the State and the Supplier; and

under which State Purchasing Entities can order Services and / or Deliverables from the Supplier.

MASTER SUPPLY AGREEMENT STRUCTURE

EXECUTED AS AN AGREEMENT

Strategic procurement

Strategic supplier

The State appoints the Supplier, as one of its strategic suppliers, to work closely and effectively with the State and State Purchasing Entities to ensure the supply of Services and Deliverables contributes to  best practice outcomes, consistent with the strategic procurement objectives set out in clause 1.2.

The Supplier acknowledges that a strategic supply arrangement requires:

a focus on compliance with the terms and conditions set out in the Agreement and an open, communicative and constructive relationship between the parties;

overarching strategic procurement objectives, which guide performance under the Agreement and the interpretation of its terms and conditions; and 

arrangements that support and encourage open communication and an engagement that meets the strategic procurement objectives.

Strategic procurement objectives

The strategic procurement objectives are as follows:

to ensure the supply of the Services and / or Deliverables are in full compliance with the terms and conditions of the Agreement, and meet or exceed the Service Levels;

to minimise the State’s costs and maximise the State’s return on its procurement investment;

to ensure the supply is market competitive and represents value for money for the State;

to ensure the Supplier fully understands the State’s commercial, legal and supply outcome requirements;

to constantly improve the State’s quality and efficiency, so that the State can operate as a best-in-class Government;

to identify and provide the State best-in-class innovation solutions and enable the State to continuously improve and accelerate the adoption of innovative solutions;

to ensure the Supplier is continuously improving and adopting innovative solutions beneficial to the State as part of the supply;  

to constantly improve the State’s ability to respond to the needs of its citizens, residents and visitors;

to provide the State with full transparency, so that it is aware and informed of aspects impacting performance under the Agreement; and

to ensure that Supplier takes a proactive role in relation to, and co-operates with, other suppliers to meet the strategic these procurement objectives. 

The Supplier must supply the Services and / or Deliverables in a way that is consistent with, and assists the State with meeting the strategic procurement objectives.

Legal relationships 

The Supplier is engaged as an independent contractor only and is not (and must not represent itself as) engaged in any other capacity including as a partner, employee, agent or joint venturer of the State or any State Purchasing Entity.

The Supplier’s engagement is non-exclusive, and the Supplier does not have the authority to bind, incur any Liability or waive any obligation, for, or on behalf of, the State or any State Purchasing Entity.

State Purchasing Entities

The State enters into the Agreement for itself and for the benefit of each State Purchasing Entity.

The Supplier agrees to make the Services and / or Deliverables available for supply to each State Purchasing Entity.

A State Purchasing Entity can acquire Services and / or Deliverables by issuing a Purchase Order, in accordance with the terms of the Agreement, at which time a Purchase Order Contract is formed between the State Purchasing Entity and the Supplier. 

The Supplier acknowledges that: 

no State Purchasing Entity will be liable to the Supplier for any acts or omissions of the State or any other State Purchasing Entity; and

the State may enforce any right afforded to a State Purchasing Entity under a Purchase Order Contract, for, and on behalf of, that State Purchasing Entity. 

The State may amend the list of State Purchasing Entities from time to time by giving notice to the Supplier. 

The Supplier warrants that it will not enter into any other agreement with any State Purchasing Entity in relation to the supply of services and / or deliverables which are the same or similar to the Services and / or Deliverables, without first obtaining the State’s consent.

Relationship Management

Relationship Managers

The Supplier and the State must nominate a Relationship Manager responsible for the overall management and administration of the relationship between the Supplier, the State and all State Purchasing Entities.

The Supplier and each State Purchasing Entity must nominate a Relationship Manager (with this nomination notified to the State) responsible for the management of the relationship between the Supplier and that State Purchasing Entity.

The Supplier, the State and each State Purchasing Entity may nominate a replacement Relationship Manager for the purposes of clause 4.1(a) or 4.1(b) (with any replacement of the Supplier under clause 4.1(b) notified to the State).

If the State:

objects to the Supplier’s nominated Relationship Manager(s) under clauses 4.1(a), 4.1(b) or 4.1(c); or

requests that the Supplier replaces its nominated Relationship Manager(s), 

then, the Supplier must:

nominate an alternative Relationship Manager acceptable to the State; and

use all reasonable endeavours to provide a seamless transition between its Relationship Manager(s) and their replacement(s).

State Purchasing Entity review meetings

The Supplier and each State Purchasing Entity must ensure that their Relationship Managers meet in accordance with the Agreement Details, to review matters relating to:

ongoing working arrangements between the parties; 

the supply of the Services and / or Deliverables; and

any other matters described in the Agreement Details. 

The Supplier must provide the State with at least 5 Business Days’ notice of any such meeting and the State’s Relationship Manager may attend that meeting.

State review meetings

The Supplier and the State must ensure that their Relationship Managers meet in accordance with the Agreement Details, to review matters relating to: 

the relationship between the parties and whether there are any issues impacting the Supplier’s ability to assist the State or State Purchasing Entities in meeting the strategic procurement objectives; 

the Supplier’s performance, including in relation to its assistance with achievement of the strategic procurement objectives; 

whether any amendments are required to the Agreement to ensure the Supplier is meeting the strategic procurement objectives; and

any other matters described in the Agreement Details. 

The Supplier acknowledges, as part of this review, that the State may require the Supplier to take remedial action in order to ensure supply is consistent with and contributing to the strategic procurement objectives. The Supplier must comply with those requirements.

To the extent the parties agree to change the Agreement as part of this review, the parties will document those changes in accordance with the variation requirements under the Agreement.

Ordinary course interactions

Nothing in the Agreement limits communications between the parties in the ordinary course and, to this end, Relationship Managers must be available at all times during Business Hours and on 48 hours’ notice outside of Business Hours.

The Supplier must provide any additional reports or information reasonably requested by the State and / or the State Purchasing Entity in order to enable them to review and ensure compliance with the Agreement.

Ordering and supply
