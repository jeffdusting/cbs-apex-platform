 Briefing: Deputy Secretary Planning Integration and Passenger, Coordinator General,
 Deputy Secretary Road Maintenance and Resilience
 FOR APPROVAL by 3/07/2025
 Urgent
 Revised Harbour Crossing Operations & Maintenance Strategy
 Purpose: To seek joint Deputy Secretary and Coordinator General approvals of the revised Harbour
 Crossing Operations and Maintenance Strategy (the Strategy), which defines the updated
 principles for the operation, maintenance and Whole-of-Life (WoL) management of the Harbour
 Crossing motorways and road tunnels.

 Analysis: The Harbour Crossing motorways and tunnels comprise the Gore Hill Freeway, Warringah
 Freeway, Sydney Harbour Tunnel (SHT), Sydney Harbour Bridge (SHB), Domain Tunnel, Cahill
 Expressway, Western Distributor, City West Link and the Western Harbour Tunnel (WHT). This is a
 complex corridor of inter-dependent motorways and tunnels that must be closely managed and
 governed as an integrated set of assets for effective transport outcomes.
 This revision to the Strategy incorporates the principles adopted in the previously approved
 Western Harbour Tunnel and Beaches Link (WHTBL) Operations Strategy (rev F5 dated Oct 2020)
 and WHTBL Maintenance and Whole-of-Life Strategy (rev B8, Nov 2020).
 While the principles are designed with strategic long-term performance of the harbour crossings
 in mind, immediate adoption of these principles allows a strategic intent to be applied to several
 pending high value asset decisions.
 Immediate actions for the implementation of the Strategy have been identified in the supporting
 analysis. Implementation of the Strategy will be led by Asset Management Branch (AMB) in PIP.
 Accountability assignments in the Strategy are subject to finalisation of the Transport for NSW
 (Transport) organisation restructure. The NSW Motorways Entity will be the owner of some of the
 Harbour Crossing assets and as such will be included within the Harbour Crossing governance
 arrangements to be established. The Tolling Branch in PIP will represent the NSW Motorways Entity
 until this entity is established.
 Any impact upon current in-progress projects will be assessed on a case-by-case basis, assessing
 the value to network performance, program and totex costs, in consultation and agreement with
 IPE prior to application. In assessing application of the Strategy to any inflight projects post Gate
 2 investment decision, any changes must be cost neutral or funded, and not adversely impact
 program unless delay costs are also funded.
 Reason for deadline: There are a number of high value asset decisions that risk being made without
 strategic context. Approval within the target timeline will ensure these investments are made
 within this strategic framework.

Key issues
The Strategy provides detailed substantiation for the adoption of six key strategic principles to ensure
the effective governance and asset management of the critical assets that comprise the harbour
crossings. These principles (and the Strategy) are intended to inform the management of the harbour
crossings for 10+ years. To ensure continued relevance through this period, the Strategy and its
principles may be updated upon agreement from all stakeholders through the governance
framework to be established by this Strategy.
To avoid duplication, readers are directed to the Strategy (Attachment A) to review the
substantiation for the adoption of the following six strategic principles (as copied from Attachment
A).
    1.      Route Prioritisation: Under normal operations, the following journey routes will be
            prioritised: SHT will carry traffic destined for the Eastern suburbs; the WHT will carry
            traffic destined for the airport, and West and South of the city; and the SHB will be for
            access to the city. The preferred harbour crossing route for freight will be the WHT
            (within the Dangerous Goods restrictions). (Note: The impact of the possible tolling
            review outcomes, and the Military Road access restriction to WHT, will be considered
            prior to the implementation the Strategy and the route prioritisation through the
            Harbour Crossing. The required review of routes and signage will ensure tolling and
            Military Road corridor impacts are assessed, and recommended routes and signage will
            be presented to the proposed Harbour Crossing governance forum, Principle #6, for
            approval prior to implementation.)
    2.      Centralised Transport Operations within the TMC: TfNSW will be accountable and
            responsible for all corridor motorway and tunnel operations, including incident response
            services and customer communication.
    3.      Performance Metrics: To ensure consistent options analysis and decision making that
            reflects the value of public transport on the corridor, Harbour Crossing performance will
            be measured using an agreed set of metrics based upon public safety, efficiency of
            movement, WoL Cost (Totex), and other key metrics as agreed.
    4.      Operator Workload Reduction: Adopt control system strategies to reduce operator
            workload and the risk of error, while retaining a competitive market for system supply
            and support.
            a. Response Automation: To allow improved productivity and reduction in operational
               interfaces across the corridor, a tiered approach to incident management response
               automation is to be adopted as standard.
            b. Common Operator Interface: Fund the development of a transition strategy to a
               standard user interface, supported by standardised field device inputs/outputs, that
               can be applied to any control system upgrade to enable progressive transition to a
               common user interface while retaining market competition.

   5.      Asset Management: Establish long term accountability for ITS and controls systems asset

           ensuring corridor performance.

Immediate actions to implement Strategy
Immediate actions required to begin implementation of the Strategy are:
   1. Confirm the prioritised routes, by origin-destination, through the Harbour Crossing, and review
      signage and wayfinding designs to support these route priorities (for Principle 1). Reviews are
      in progress for this purpose.
   2. Continue with the transition of operations to the TMC (for Principle 2). Transition program in
      progress.
   3. Define and establish the Harbour Crossing governance framework, incorporating existing
      forums as appropriate (for Principle 6). The proposed governance framework and draft terms
      of reference are to be established by 1 July 2025, with the first meeting to be conducted by 31
      July 2025.
   4. Define and establish the performance framework and metrics for the Harbour Crossing,
      establishing a stakeholder working group to do so (for Principle 3).
   5. Fund and initiate the development of a technical strategy, roadmap and business case for
      transitioning to a common operator interface (for Principle 4).
   6. Develop transition options and recommendations for consolidating the asset management and
      maintenance of control systems and roadside ITS, assessing impacts on existing arrangements
      (for Principle 5).
In response to the approval of this Strategy, a draft plan outlining the immediate implementation
activities is to be developed by 1 July 2025, for review at the first Harbour Crossing governance
meeting.
PIP, as the Custodian of the Harbour Crossing assets, is to lead these actions. Governance of these
initial actions, and the implementation of the outcomes, to be transitioned to the Harbour Crossing
governance framework to be established.

Financial impact

immediate actions, which will identify any initial funding requirements. Where funding is required,
separate briefing note(s) will be developed to authorise funding. Funding requirements have not

horizon.

business cases.

benefits and impacts to project costs, budgets and schedules, and upon consultation and agreement
from IPE. Any changes to inflight projects (post Gate 2 investment decision) must be cost neutral or
funded, and not adversely impact program unless delay costs are also funded.

       adopted as standard.

       interface, supported by standardised field device inputs/outputs, that can be applied to any
       control system upgrade to enable progressive transition to a common user interface while
       retaining market competition.

       the sustainment of critical internal strategic and support capabilities.

1.           Introduction
1.1          Context

             This document updates the WHTBL Operations Strategy (rev F5, Oct 2020), Ref 3, and the
             WHTBL Maintenance and Whole-of-Life Strategy (rev B8, Nov 2020), Ref 4.

             In updating these baseline documents, a decision was made to increase the scope to include all
             motorways leading to the harbour crossings, remove references to the Beaches Link, and
             combine these strategies into a single Harbour Crossing Operations and Maintenance (O&M)
             Strategy (Strategy). This Strategy is also consistent with the Transport Road Corridor
             Operations Strategy (Jun 2023), Ref 5, which consolidates operational control staff and facilities
             to the Transport Management Centre (TMC).

             The Harbour Crossing Corridor is to be built, operated, maintained and managed consistent
             with the principles described by this Strategy.

             The Strategy will be used to guide future project and design decisions and may also be applied
             retrospectively to in-flight projects and service contracts on a Value for Money basis.


1.2          Purpose

             This document provides a set of key principles that are to be consistently applied by projects,
             operations teams, and asset custodians and stewards in management, change and operation of
             the Harbour Crossing Corridor (Corridor).

             The Strategy provides specific application of the Transport Road Corridor Operations Strategy
             to the Harbour Crossings.

             Building upon the key principles, this document:

             •   provides the framework for, and informs, the development of the Concepts of Operations
                 (ConOps) and operational needs for various motorways and tunnels within the Harbour
                 Crossing;
             •   provides the principles and guidance for consistent, integrated decision making across the
                 harbour crossing projects and assets;
             •   provides the framework for decisions made to change the operations and maintenance of
                 the Harbour Crossing assets.

2.           Harbour Crossing Description
2.1          Current Situation

             The Harbour Crossing is:

                   •   A critical interchange for Sydney and its productivity, with delays and unavailability
                       having a high cost to the community.

                   •   Subject to several significant, in-progress projects (WHT, WFU, WDSM, SHBC1),
                       delivering independently and at different times, requiring integration between the
                       motorway interfaces, that will significantly change operations and support arrangements.

                   •   Using multiple control systems for the various motorways and tunnels, requiring
                       integration to shared ITS devices, within a complex operations systems architecture.

             Furthermore, the current WHT, Warringah Freeway Upgrade (WFU) and Western Distributor
             Smart Motorway (WDSM) projects aim to uplift the performance of the Harbour Crossing by
             improving safety, journey times and reliability for active transport, public transport, freight and
             private vehicles.

             An Over Height Vehicle (OHV) management program has also been initiated to minimise the
             network impacts of OHV upon the Harbour Crossing Corridor.

             The Beaches Link project has been cancelled, with some gaps in understanding as to the
             impacts of this change upon traffic flows on the approach to and along the Harbour Crossings.

             The TfNSW Toll review is also in progress, and the impact of the selected toll regime for the
             crossings may have an impact upon a future version of this strategy.

       2.2.5      ITS & Control Systems:

       Safe, efficient operation of the Harbour Crossing requires integrated ITS and control systems for
       coordinated traffic and incident response management. The current control systems
       implemented on the Harbour Crossing are summarised in section 2.3.

       The multiple, separate control and reporting systems currently in use across the Harbour
       Crossing assets is putting undue pressure on the operators. This situation is being addressed
       through the current review of the future operating system architecture, such as the Operational
       Technology Ecosystem Strategy (OTECS) program.

       2.2.6      Warringah Freeway (WF) + Gore Hill Freeway (GHF)

       WF (and GHF) is a critical section of the network, being the one interchange north of the
       harbour crossing and a key decision point for road users. It supports access to a variety of
       connecting motorways and arterial roads, and its coordination is critical to the effective
       operation of the harbour crossings.

       The in-progress WFU Project is upgrading the 4 km WF corridor between the northern end of
       SHB and Willoughby Road in Naremburn, allowing new connections to and from WHT, and
       reconfiguring the existing corridor to optimise the performance and safety (e.g. minimising traffic
       weaves) of the SHT, SHB and the WHT for the future.

       WF (and GHF) is within the maintenance area of the Sydney Roads Asset Performance (SRAP)
       Contract with Connect Sydney. During the current WFU project, WF maintenance is the
       responsibility of the WFU Contractor.

       Arrangements for maintenance and asset management following the completion of the WFU
       project are currently being determined.

       2.2.7      Western Harbour Tunnel (WHT)

       A 6.5 km cross-harbour motorway tunnel from the Rozelle Interchange to the Warringah
       Freeway (WF) near North Sydney. Linking directly to the WestConnex M4-M5 Link, WHT will
       form a new, motorway-standard western bypass of the Harbour CBD and improve the
       performance and function of the heavily congested Anzac Bridge, Western Distributor and
       Sydney Harbour Bridge (SHB) corridor.

       The WHT is due to be operated by Ventia as part of the WHT+SHT Asset Manager contract;
       however, operations will transition to TfNSW TMC under the strategy to centralise operations.
