# Competitor Profile — Mott MacDonald

## Overview

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Company | Mott MacDonald Group Limited (private, employee-owned) |
| Headquarters | Croydon, UK (AU: Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane) |
| Size | ~18,000 employees globally, ~800 in Australia/NZ |
| Key Sectors | Rail, tunnels, transport, PPP advisory, international development |
| Geographic Coverage | National (AU offices), UK, Middle East, Asia, Americas |

## Capabilities

- Rail infrastructure advisory and design (including Inland Rail advisory roles)
- Tunnel ventilation, safety, and operations
- PPP advisory and transaction support
- Transport planning and demand forecasting
- International infrastructure development advisory
- Asset management for rail and road

## Strengths

- Strong rail expertise — advisory roles on Inland Rail programme
- International tunnel and rail experience (UK, Middle East)
- PPP advisory capability (financial, legal, technical)
- Employee-owned structure — long-term client relationship focus
- Smaller Australian office enables specialist, committed teams

## Weaknesses / CBS Advantage

- Smaller Australian presence — may struggle to resource large concurrent engagements
- Less established in NSW road/motorway AM — CBS has deep TfNSW relationships
- Mott MacDonald's AM is secondary to their engineering/design core — CBS is AM-specialist
- International focus can mean less Australian regulatory/procurement knowledge than local specialist

## Win Themes Against Mott MacDonald

1. "Deep Australian transport AM experience — CBS's established TfNSW, SHT, WHT, M6 relationships vs Mott MacDonald's primarily international track record"
2. "CAPITAL framework delivers quantified outcomes in Australian context — not adapted from international methodologies"
3. "Full-time Australian-based senior team — CBS personnel are based where the projects are, not fly-in from UK"
