Speaker Notes

Slide One

Pakenham site exists within several important Federal and State Policy and Infrastructure contexts:

Policy: REZ, M1 Strategic Corridor Plan, Victorian Hydrogen Plan and State has announced that Capacity Market will use only Renewables.

Infrastructure: 

M1: 60m from Melbourne CBD, Adjacent to heavily trafficked M1 – Section 6 corridor: Nar Nar Goon to Traralgon with key towns of 10k Pop along the route. (Morewell, Moe, Warragul, Drouin,  

Access to stable capacity HV lines (Traralgon to CBD), Adjacent to major gas pipelines, 56km to Port of Hastings via South Gippsland Hwy, Access to Desalination at Wonthaggi.

Our approach: evaluate the opportunity for additional PV power generation and expanded use of power behind and beyond the meter.

Doubling the current solar and investigating wind generation shows the potential for the site to be a Large-Scale Energy provider. 

Consider the use of PV power and site water (and/or desalination water) to establish a Green Hydrogen plant: Energy outputs may include Hydrogen pumped directly to Gas Pipeline, Capacity Power Market participation via the existing HV transmission lines, Hydrogen fuel (M1) and Hydrogen export (Port of Hastings)

Slide Two:

Political Context. Victoria is the at the forefront of Treaty discussions with Indigenous groups. 

Historical Context Pakenham/Nar Nar Goon is low lying swamp lands that provided a rich fishing and hunting ground for the tribes and clans of the Kulin Nation for millennia. Today, the protected fauna and flora recall this history.

Our approach: Respect and re-integrate the cultural history of the site into carbon plots that become eligible for Gold Standard ™ certification. Gold Standard projects layer additional value by contributing to the achievement of UD Sustainability Development Goals in addition to decarbonisation.  The nearby Cardinia Environmental Coalition is the largest native plant nursery in Victoria. Early feasibility shows the potential for 50 Hectares to be regenerated using Native or Mallee Planting (FullCAM method) in accordance with the requirements of the Emissions Reductions Fund.

Site Climate and Weather – the site is well suited to wind generation either rooftop turbines or freestanding as (in contrast to solar) the cut in of 10mph for turbines is available more often than the sunlight hours required to generate solar. 

The site is relatively suitable for large scale battery storage (requires  design for optimal operating environment as 40% loss in cold weather.

The wind patterns are also suited to the passive air flow requirements for Mechanical Trees.

Slide Three: Options Review

Additional Solar

Context: current solar provides @30% of the 1000 MGh used annually. Majority of power usage is at night. 

Hypothesis: we can ‘close the gap’ and generate an additional 700MWh per annum by doubling the existing rooftop array and providing battery storage capacity (15 + Tesla Powerwall2’s)

We could also expand PV and storage to create surplus power for use behind the meter (EV charging, Green Hydrogen) or beyond the meter (Green Hydrogen or a Virtual Energy Network)

Notes/Issues

Cost to double PV and 100% battery storage Circa 800k. Eligibility for Large Scale Energy Certificates (paid retrospectively) could contribute circa 250k.

Wind Turbines

Context: The site is better suited to wind generation than solar – see climate notes – however there has been strong community opposition to prior wind projects so stakeholder consultation is critical.

Hypothesis: Roof mounted or freestanding turbines to be investigated

Notes/Issues

Price circa 1m – payback period estimated at 9 – 12months

Battery Storage

Context: No battery storage on site

Hypothesis: Site energy needs require storage to service night and peak demands. (TBC). Current options are from China or Tesla. (Circa 15k/unit x 15 – 20 units)

Notes/Issues Battery install will need to be designed to avoid frosts and temps below 10 degrees.

EV Charging

Context: M1 adjacency

Hypothesis: May work with a Downer Fleet strategy or for a Downer Client, or General Public. (GP)

Notes: established technology, there is also a Shell service station nearby that could be used for GP delivery.

Reafforestation, Future Carbon Plots, Gold Standard Credits

Context:

Site is eligible for Native Planting or Mallee and consequent ACCU’s. Estimated yields are 290tCO2e/Ha and 7000 ACCU’s. 

Carbon heat map shown using FullCAM methodology

Future Carbon Plots – are 1 Hectare plots planted to mimic the future climate. They assist in preserving fauna who use these plots to begin adaptation to future conditions.

Gold Standard certified projects contribute to the achievement of UD Sustainability Development Goals in addition to decarbonisation.  

Hypothesis: develop and certify a Gold Standard project that creates higher value credits, a Target price of $65 – 70 v. ACCU of $38 – 50. This can be achieved using indigenous land care practices and in consultation with the community.

Note that the Kulin Nation Seasonal Calendar has three seasons related to activity of the Pakenham site: Kangaroo Apple Season (December), Dry Season (Jan-Feb), Eel Season (March), Wombat Season (April-August), Orchid Season (September), Tadpole Season (October), Grass-flowering Season (November).
