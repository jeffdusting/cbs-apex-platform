Countrywide Renewable Hydrogen, in cooperation with the Melbourne Market Authority (MMA), are investigating the possibility of creating a renewable hydrogen hub co-located with the MMA facilities at Epping in Melbourne’s northern suburbs. 
The site is strategically placed in the northern Melbourne transport corridor, opening opportunities to supply renewable hydrogen to a fleet of fuel cell buses, with up to 90 transit buses to be introduced onto Melbourne’s roads progressively from the end of 2022. 
The MMA operates 1,100 forklifts powered by LPG, opening opportunities to transition the fleet to renewable hydrogen. 
The project is also assessing options for blending hydrogen into local gas networks. The project is examining the installation of a 10 MW PEM electrolyser, with an output of around 4.5 tonnes of hydrogen per day. 
The MMA infrastructure offers ‘behind-the meter’ solar power from proposed rooftop arrays with a capacity of up to 25 MW. Excess electricity would be available for hydrogen production. Water would be available from on-site storage that holds 40 million litres of rainwater.

Pakenham Site Insights
A review of the 167hectare Pakenham was undertaken to establish options to provide an additional 700 MGh per annum from renewable energy while generating 300+ tonnes of carbon offsets per annum. 
The review identified:
Potential for additional Solar and Wind generation, with Battery storage, to meet the estimated 1000 MWh annual requirements via renewables. (2,3,4)
Potential for Pakenham to generate in excess of its requirements and feed a Virtual Power Network that provides renewable energy to Newport or other Downer nominated projects. (10)
Potential for Pakenham to leverage its proximity to the M1 corridor to develop a Green hydrogen plant (mobility, power use, or addition to adjacent gas mains) similar to the Melbourne Hydrogen Hub at Epping currently under development by Countrywide Renewable Hydrogen. (8)
Potential for @ 60hectare reafforestation program in accordance with ERF’s Reforestation by Environmental or Mallee Plantings FullCAM Methodology to generate carbon units (ACCU’s). (6)
Potential for development of Gold Standard ™  projects onsite including, defined heritage protection activities and Carbon Future plots for threatened species and flora. (11)
Potential for site to be used as a test, initial ANZ licenser, of Mechanical Tree ™ technology – to be achieved in conjunction with Federal Department of Trade and Industry. (9)

Option Seven: Mechanical Trees

### Slide 33
They're tall vertical columns of discs coated with a chemical resin, about five feet in diameter, with the discs about two inches apart, like a stack of records. As the air blows through, the surfaces of the discs absorb CO2.
The Mechanical Tree farm was developed by ASU professor Klaus Lackner and Silicon Kingdom Holdings, a company in Dublin, the farm will act as a passive CO2 capture device.
Once the carbon is collected, it is permanently buried under the ground in a process called sequestration. The carbon dioxide itself can also be commercialized. CO2 captured by MechanicalTreesTM can be safely and permanently sequestered in underground geological formations, such as saline aquifers or depleted and end-of life oil wells, leading to a material reduction of CO2 in Earth’s atmosphere.
Two hundred and fifty large scale Mechanical Tree™ farms have the capacity to capture one gigaton (A gigaton equals one billion metric tons.) of CO2.
