PIAM&V Method


             Purpose of this template
The Energy Savings Scheme Rule of 2009 (ESS Rule) requires that Measurement and
Verification (M&V) Professionals be used to confirm the validity of energy models used by
Accredited Certificate Providers (ACPs) when implementing activities that use the Project
Impact Assessment with Measurement and Verification (PIAM&V) method.

ACPs must ensure that an approved M&V Professional deems appropriate the various
aspects of their measurement procedures, energy models and parameters in accordance
with the requirements of clause 7A of the ESS Rule and PIAM&V Method Requirements
published by IPART.

M&V Professionals must provide their validation of the baseline measurement procedures
and their written explanatory reasoning using this template.



             Instructions for using this template
To use this template, you must complete every section. The completed report will be
reviewed by PIAM&V auditors and must include sufficient reasoning to justify your opinion of
the various aspects of the measurement procedures relating to the baseline energy model.

Please note that you must validate the measurement procedures in relation to the baseline
energy model and complete the Preliminary M&V Professional Report with your written
explanatory reasoning prior to the implementation date.

Reports without sufficient explanatory reasoning may be assessed as not meeting the
requirements of the ESS Rule and/or PIAM&V Method Requirements published by IPART,
and may impact the validity of the energy savings certificates for the relevant
implementation.

Measurement Procedures – Baseline Energy Model
    Explain how the Measurement Procedures for measuring electricity/Gas consumption, Independent
    Variables, Site Constants and other parameters are appropriate for the Baseline Energy Model (in
    accordance with clause 7A.5 of the ESS Rule).
    The Measurement Procedures must:
         o      be based on a baseline Measurement Period that meets the requirements of clause 7A.5(a)-(b)1
         o      include any time periods in which Independent Variables may reasonably be expected to lead to the
                Implementation increasing electricity consumption or Gas consumption or both (clause 7A.5(f1))
         o      cover all relevant Independent Variables that impact the energy consumption of the End-User
                Equipment
         o      have sufficient frequency of measurements over the baseline Measurement Period to establish
                normal operating conditions (consider frequency of measurements in respect of power draw,
                availability of metered energy data and other relevant factors)
         o      define which items of End-User Equipment will have their electricity consumption, Gas
                consumption, or both, measured (the measurement boundary)
         o      specify measurement equipment (meters) or other sources of measurements
         o      define the calibration procedures, accuracy and precision of such measurement methods, and
         o      record and exclude any Non-Routine Events that occurred during the baseline Measurement
                Period, ensuring that the percentage of time excluded is less than 20% of the baseline
                Measurement Period

The measurement procedures used for measuring Electricity Consumption and monitoring Site
Constants are considered appropriate for the project and are in accordance with Clause 7A.5 of the
ESS Rule. This has been demonstrated by the ACP as follows:

7A.5(a)
The measurement period for the Baseline Model covers the period from 1/10/2020 to 30/9/2021, with
relevant data consolidated into daily totals. The daily totals for the HVAC electricity consumption will
be derived 10 second interval data logged at the individual HVAC system meters installed on each
train set (of 8 carriages).

7A.5(b) (i)
The Baseline Period is prior to the project implementation date which is scheduled for October 2021.

7A.5(b) (ii)
N/A

7A.5(b) (iii)
N/A

7A.5(b) (iv)
N/A

7A.5(c)
The measurement frequency for the RESA has been set at daily intervals, creating 365 data points for
the Baseline Model. There are not expected to be any non-routine events during the Baseline Period.
Where some interval data used to accumulate the daily totals is missing for some reason, the ACP
has decided to eliminate daily data if more than 20% of the interval data is missing.

The daily data used in the modelling is being consolidated from measured real time data of the
independent variables available from calibrated and/or reliable independent data sources such as the
BOM climate data. A large number of possible independent variables have been identified and these
are being monitored during the baseline period prior to modelling commencing.



7A.5(d)
The End-User Equipment being measured in association with the RESA consists of the following;
    - The HVAC systems of each train set sampled.
        and
    - The 8 carriage train set as a whole (for its operational status)

The baseline regression model proposed will be developed to take into account the interrelationship
between the electricity consumption of the train set HVAC systems under the current control software
modes.



7A.5(e)
The measurement equipment that has been employed in the development of the Baseline Model is as
follows:
     - On board electricity meters associated with each HVAC unit on each train set.
     - BOM climate data
     - Train set run time data provided by Sydney Trains for each train set.



7A.5(f)
The electricity meters on each HVAC unit are identified by the ACP as being accurate to +/- 0.3%.
BOM data is considered to be accurate. The ACP has not specified the nominal accuracy at this stage
but this is not considered to be an issued for the modelling.

7A.5(f1)
The Baseline Measurement Period (365 days) is sufficient to capture the full range of normal
operating conditions likely to occur in a normal year.
7A.5(g)
No data points have been excluded from the Baseline Measurement period as of the time of writing
this preliminary report.

7A.5(h)
Following my review of the above items relating to clause 7A.5 of the ESS Rule, I am satisfied that the
Measurement Procedures adopted by the ACP so far for this RESA are appropriate to meet the
requirements of the ESS Rule.
