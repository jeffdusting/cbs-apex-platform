Executive summary
Provide an overview of the Offer.

The Challenge
The recontracting of the 2028 Metropolitan Bus Services Contracts (MBSC), a generational transformation of
the metropolitan bus network, represents one of the most complex public transport procurement
programmes currently underway in Australia. Approximately 40% of Melbourne's metropolitan bus network
must be recontracted while simultaneously embedding the operational, commercial, and infrastructure
requirements of Victoria's Zero Emission Bus (ZEB) transition. The current contracts, based on negotiated
total costs with fixed margins, contain no ZEB transition commercials. The State will gain access to fleets,
staff, and the majority of transferring depots in June 2028, but most depots within the scope do not currently
have charging infrastructure, and some will not transfer to State control at all. DTP must design a
procurement framework that achieves commercial consistency with the recent MZF tenders while
accommodating unresolved questions of asset ownership, energy cost risk allocation, grid augmentation
timelines, and the pace of technology change in an asset class with limited Australian operational history.
Why CBS Group
CBS Group brings a combination of capabilities to this engagement that is directly shaped by the practical
demands of ZEB transition advisory. Our team has already navigated the technical, commercial, and
contractual complexity of ZEB transition in the most directly comparable Australian programme, and we
combine that frontline ZEB experience with deep whole-of-life asset management, systems interface, and
operational integration capability developed across more than two decades of major transport infrastructure
advisory work.
1. Direct experience in developing the asset class strategy for the NSW ZEB transition. CBS Group's
Gethin Perry developed the asset class strategy for the Transport for NSW (TfNSW) Greater Sydney Bus
Contracts (GSBC) ZEB transition — a ZEB transition programme directly comparable to the MBSCs. That
work established the strategic framework for the NSW ZEB rollout across the GSBC footprint, addressing
fleet transition sequencing, depot electrification prioritisation, charging infrastructure configuration, and the
operational requirements for transitioning from diesel to zero emission bus services. The NSW programme
involves both operator-led and State-led depot conversions, wholly brownfield contract variation pathways,
and the use of NFI arrangements — giving CBS Group direct, current experience across the full range of
delivery models that DTP is now considering for the MBSC 2028 recontracting.
2. Translating service outcomes into commercial models within existing contract constraints. A
defining requirement of this engagement is embedding ZEB operations and service delivery specifications
within a recontracting framework that must work within the architecture of existing and evolving franchise
agreements. CBS Group's approach is grounded in the principle that commercial models must be anchored
to service outcomes — fleet availability, route coverage, energy efficiency, depot uptime — rather than to
abstract cost structures. In the NSW GSBC programme, CBS Group's partner Firecone Ventures (Chad
Pappas) designed the financial templates for collecting operator pricing submissions, developed energy cost
risk sharing mechanisms that accommodate the transition from diesel to ZEB steady-state operations, and
compiled revised contract payment schedules, budgets, and KPIs — all within the constraints of eight
existing contracts with different commercial pathways. This same discipline of translating service delivery
objectives into workable commercial terms within real contractual constraints will be applied to the MBSC
2028 recontracting.
3. Cross-jurisdictional financial modelling and commercial advisory depth. CBS Group's partnership
with Firecone Ventures provides access to Chad Pappas, a senior commercial and financial advisor with
over 25 years of experience across bus, heavy rail, light rail, ferry, and road transport service contracts in
Australia. In addition to his current role as commercial and financial advisor to TfNSW on the GSBC ZEB
implementation, Chad has led or contributed to the Melbourne Tram and Train Franchising (MR5)
programme for DTP, the Adelaide Public Transport InHouse Program and Adelaide Metro Bus Retendering
for DIT South Australia, and the Sydney Roads Asset Performance Contracts. This breadth across bus
programmes in New South Wales, South Australia, and Victoria provides CBS Group with comparative

reliability and operational efficiency rather than simply replicate diesel-era fleet structures with electric

coordination of multiple operational systems, contractual interfaces, and stakeholder requirements was
central to the advisory scope. For this engagement, CBS Group will apply that discipline to the interfaces
between ZEB operational requirements and the commercial framework, between depot electrification
proposals and DNSP augmentation programmes, and between DTP's programme team and the broader
advisory panel.
7. Operational integration and transition capability targeted at service and asset outcomes. The
programme timeline requires the transition from incumbent operators to new franchise holders while
simultaneously commissioning ZEB infrastructure and fleet across a mix of greenfield and brownfield sites.
CBS Group has directly managed transition and mobilisation advisory on long-duration transport contracts,
ensuring that commitments made during procurement are translated into operationally verifiable transition
milestones targeted at best asset and service delivery outcomes. Our operational integration capability
ensures that ZEB transition planning addresses dual-capability requirements during ramp-up, depot
commissioning sequencing, fleet delivery scheduling against supply chain realities, and the handover of
State-owned assets — all coordinated to minimise service disruption and to position DTP for sustained
operational performance from franchise commencement.

Approach Overview

CBS Group will deliver ZEB Operations and Service Delivery advisory services through a phase-aligned
methodology that tracks DTP's programme milestones from commencement (1 April 2026) through to

with how CBS Group has delivered comparable advisory engagements on major transport programmes.

The four principles are as follows.

    •    First, embed within the DTP team from day one — advisory value is maximised when the adviser
         understands the day-to-day context, the internal constraints, the decision points as they arise, and
         the governance processes to be applied. CBS Group will work on-site at DTP as directed,
         particularly during critical programme milestones.
    •    Second, maintain outcome-focussed, whole-of-life thinking across every advisory output — the 2028
         MBSC contracts will define bus operations in Melbourne for the next decade or more, and
         specification decisions will have lifecycle implications that must be surfaced, assessed within the
         strategic context and quantified for DTP's consideration.
    •    Third, translate operational complexity into procurement-ready outputs — DTP needs advice that
         can be converted directly into specifications, procurement documents, and evaluation criteria, all of
         which need to be managed across the contract and stakeholder interfaces. Our advisory outputs
         will always be structured for decision-making and documentation purposes, not for abstract analysis.
    •    Fourth, manage advisory risk through structured escalation and peer review — all CBS Group
         advisory outputs are subject to internal technical review and senior quality assurance review before
         delivery to DTP.
