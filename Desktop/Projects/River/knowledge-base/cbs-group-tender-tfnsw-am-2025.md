JourneyIQ: MASCOT TUNNEL DEMONSTRATION

Subject: A value-based proposal to prove the suitability of CBS JourneyIQ for analysing the local area queuing impacts of removing the contraflow lane on General Holmes Drive and Mascot Tunnel eastbound.

Date: 16 November 2025

1. Executive Summary

This proposal outlines a highly focused, low-risk approach will demonstrate the suitability of the CBS JourneyIQ solution for modelling the specific queuing impacts of removing the contraflow lane on General Holmes Drive eastbound, approaching and through the Mascot Tunnel. 

The primary deliverable will be a clear, graphical analysis of predicted queue lengths by time of day for three distinct reference weeks, providing a robust, evidence-based insight into this critical operational question.

To demonstrate our confidence in the JourneyIQ product and deliver exceptional value, we propose a commercial model based on success:

Mobilisation Fee: An initial commitment of $5,000 (ex. GST) to configure and apply JourneyIQ to this specific problem.

Success Fee: A retained fee of $24,500 (ex. GST), payable only upon successful demonstration of the product's suitability and acceptance against clear, pre-defined criteria.

This demonstration offers TfNSW a low-risk opportunity to validate a powerful new analysis capability and gain specific, actionable insights into the queuing impacts of a key operational change, with the majority of the fee contingent on a successful outcome.

2. Introduction: Proving the JourneyIQ Product

The operational efficiency of the Mascot Tunnel and its approaches is critical to the performance of Sydney’s road network. The eastbound contraflow lane on General Holmes Drive is a key component of this operation. Any consideration of its removal requires a detailed understanding of the potential impacts on traffic flow, particularly the formation and extent of queuing.

CBS Group has developed JourneyIQ, a proprietary, AI-augmented traffic analysis product that translates real-world travel time data into robust, evidence-based insights. This demonstration is designed to prove the suitability of JourneyIQ for TfNSW's specific needs by applying it to this complex operational question.

Instead of a broad, theoretical analysis, we will deliver a tangible demonstration of the product's capabilities, aligning with the CAPITAL framework's focus on delivering targeted, evidence-based outcomes.

3. The JourneyIQ Methodology

Our established JourneyIQ methodology is designed to translate real-world travel time data into a robust analysis of traffic queues.

Historical Traffic Pattern Extraction: JourneyIQ utilises the Google Maps Routes API to build a comprehensive and statistically robust picture of typical travel times for different times of day and days of the week, based on billions of real-world data points.

Localised Model Development and Calibration: The extracted travel time data is used to calibrate a localised, microscopic traffic simulation model in Eclipse SUMO. The model is specifically calibrated to ensure that the simulated travel times across different segments of the corridor accurately reflect the patterns observed in the Google Maps data. This is the critical step that allows travel time data to be used as a proxy for real-world traffic conditions.

Queue Estimation and Analysis: The calibrated model is used to simulate the removal of the contraflow lane. By analysing the simulation outputs, we can directly measure the formation, propagation, and dissipation of traffic queues. The model will report queue lengths in metres and the number of vehicles queued at regular intervals.

Temporal Analysis: This analysis will be conducted for three distinct reference weeks to provide a comprehensive understanding of queuing behaviour under different demand conditions:

A typical school term week.

A NSW school holiday week.

A week with a known major event impacting Sydney Airport or the CBD.

4. Illustrative Deliverables: Local Area Impact Analysis

The following illustrative maps show what the final JourneyIQ analysis will look like, with a specific focus on queue spillback impact on the local residential area (Kyeemagh). These schematic diagrams illustrate how removing the contraflow lane could affect local residents trying to access General Holmes Drive from Bestic Street.

Scenario 1: Baseline (Current - 3 Lanes with Contraflow)

This schematic shows the current PM peak conditions. The moderate queue (~250m) does not block the Bestic Street intersection, allowing local residents to access General Holmes Drive.

Key Metrics (Baseline):

Configuration: 3 lanes eastbound (with contraflow)

Congestion Level: Moderate (Orange)

Local Access: ✓ Unimpeded

Scenario 2: Proposed (Contraflow Removed - 2 Lanes)

This schematic shows the predicted PM peak conditions after removing the contraflow lane. The heavy queue (~620m) spills back and blocks the Bestic Street intersection, preventing local residents from accessing General Holmes Drive.

Key Metrics (Proposed):

Configuration: 2 lanes eastbound (contraflow removed)

Congestion Level: Heavy (Red)

Local Access: ✗ Blocked

Impact Summary

The JourneyIQ analysis will provide a clear, quantitative comparison of these two scenarios, with a specific focus on local area impacts. The final report will include detailed charts and tables quantifying the impacts on queue length, travel time, delay, and access for feeder traffic from Bestic Street.

Note: The queue lengths and local impacts shown here are illustrative. The final JourneyIQ analysis will provide precise, calibrated results based on SUMO traffic simulation.

5. Scope of Services

The scope of services is tightly focused on demonstrating the JourneyIQ product's capability:

Data Extraction and Processing: Automated extraction of historical travel time patterns from the Google Maps Routes API for the General Holmes Drive corridor for the three specified reference weeks.

Model Development and Calibration: Construction and calibration of a microscopic traffic simulation model in SUMO, validated against the extracted Google Maps data.

Scenario Modelling: Simulation of the baseline (with contraflow) and the lane reduction (without contraflow) scenarios.

Queuing Analysis and Reporting: A detailed analysis of the simulation outputs to determine queue lengths, and the preparation of a final report focused on this specific impact.

Presentation of results and executive presentations are limited to 3 sessions, with subsequent support provided on an as needs basis at $390/hr plus GST.

6. Deliverables

Upon completion of this analysis, our primary deliverable will be a demonstration of Queuing Impact Report. This report will provide:

Graphical Analysis of Queue Lengths: A series of charts illustrating the predicted eastbound queue lengths (in metres) on General Holmes Drive at 15-minute intervals throughout the day (06:00 - 20:00).

Temporal Comparison: This graphical analysis will be provided for each of the three reference weeks, allowing for a clear comparison of queuing behaviour under different network conditions.

Summary of Findings: A concise summary of the key findings, highlighting the predicted increase in queue length and duration resulting from the removal of the contraflow lane.

Methodology Overview: A brief overview of the JourneyIQ methodology used to generate the analysis.

In addition, we will provide:

The calibrated SUMO traffic model, allowing for further analysis by TfNSW.

A presentation of the key findings to relevant stakeholders.

2. Introduction: Proving the JourneyIQ Product
The operational efficiency of the Mascot Tunnel and its approaches is critical to the performance of Sydney’s
road network. The eastbound contraflow lane on General Holmes Drive is a key component of this operation.
Any consideration of its removal requires a detailed understanding of the potential impacts on traffic flow,
particularly the formation and extent of queuing.

CBS Group has developed JourneyIQ, a proprietary, AI-augmented traffic analysis product that translates real-
world travel time data into robust, evidence-based insights. This demonstration is designed to prove the
suitability of JourneyIQ for TfNSW's specific needs by applying it to this complex operational question.
Instead of a broad, theoretical analysis, we will deliver a tangible demonstration of the product's capabilities,
aligning with the CAPITAL framework's focus on delivering targeted, evidence-based outcomes.

3. The JourneyIQ Methodology
Our established JourneyIQ methodology is designed to translate real-world travel time data into a robust
analysis of traffic queues.

    1.   Historical Traffic Pattern Extraction: JourneyIQ utilises the Google Maps Routes API to build a
         comprehensive and statistically robust picture of typical travel times for different times of day and days
         of the week, based on billions of real-world data points.

    2.   Localised Model Development and Calibration: The extracted travel time data is used to calibrate a
         localised, microscopic traffic simulation model in Eclipse SUMO. The model is specifically calibrated to
         ensure that the simulated travel times across different segments of the corridor accurately reflect the

         patterns observed in the Google Maps data. This is the critical step that allows travel time data to be
         used as a proxy for real-world traffic conditions.

    3.   Queue Estimation and Analysis: The calibrated model is used to simulate the removal of the contraflow
         lane. By analysing the simulation outputs, we can directly measure the formation, propagation, and
         dissipation of traffic queues. The model will report queue lengths in metres and the number of vehicles
         queued at regular intervals.

    4.   Temporal Analysis: This analysis will be conducted for three distinct reference weeks to provide a
         comprehensive understanding of queuing behaviour under different demand conditions:

             o   A typical school term week.

             o   A NSW school holiday week.

             o   A week with a known major event impacting Sydney Airport or the CBD.
