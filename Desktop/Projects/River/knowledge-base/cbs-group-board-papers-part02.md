PART 2 — SHARE CAPITAL

4.	F Shares

4.1	Voting. Each F Share confers on its holder the right to one vote on a poll at a general meeting of the Company or on any written resolution of Members.

4.2	Dividends. F Shares rank equally with Common Shares for dividends, with each F Share entitled to participate pro rata in any dividend or distribution declared by the Company on the same terms as each Common Share.

4.3	Capital. On a winding up or return of capital, F Shares rank equally with Common Shares, with each F Share entitled to participate pro rata in the distribution of surplus assets on the same basis as each Common Share, subject to the prior rights (if any) of Series A Preferred Shares as determined at the time of the Qualifying Financing.

4.4	Conversion. Each F Share will automatically convert into one Common Share, on a one-for-one basis, upon the earlier of:

(a)	completion of a Qualifying Financing; or

(b)	a written election by the F Share Majority to convert all (but not some only) F Shares to Common Shares.

4.5	Upon conversion under clause 4.4, the Company must update the Register to reflect the conversion. No additional consideration is payable. Conversion does not affect the number of Shares held, only the class designation. Following conversion, the converted Shares carry the rights of Common Shares.

4.6	Class rights. The rights attaching to F Shares are class rights for the purposes of section 246B of the Corporations Act and may only be varied or cancelled with the written consent of the F Share Majority.

5.	Common Shares

5.1	Voting. Each Common Share confers on its holder the right to one vote on a poll at a general meeting of the Company or on any written resolution of Members.

5.2	Dividends. Common Shares rank equally with F Shares for dividends, with each Common Share entitled to participate pro rata in any dividend or distribution declared by the Company on the same terms as each F Share.

5.3	Capital. On a winding up or return of capital, Common Shares rank equally with F Shares, with each Common Share entitled to participate pro rata in the distribution of surplus assets on the same basis as each F Share, subject to the prior rights (if any) of Series A Preferred Shares as determined at the time of the Qualifying Financing.

5.4	Class rights. The rights attaching to Common Shares are class rights for the purposes of section 246B of the Corporations Act and may only be varied or cancelled with the written consent of holders of more than 75% of the Common Shares on issue.

6.	Series A Preferred Shares

6.1	The Company is authorised to create and issue Series A Preferred Shares on completion of a Qualifying Financing. The rights, privileges, restrictions, and conditions attaching to Series A Preferred Shares will be determined by the Board and the F Share Majority, and will be set out in:

(a)	an amendment to this Constitution adopted by special resolution in connection with the Qualifying Financing; and/or

(b)	the SHA.

6.2	To the extent of any inconsistency between this Constitution and the SHA in relation to the rights attaching to Series A Preferred Shares, the SHA prevails (clause 22).

6.3	Until Series A Preferred Shares are created and issued, the provisions of this Constitution relating to Series A Preferred Shares, Investor Majority, Investor Director, and Investor Reserved Matters are dormant and have no effect.

7.	Issue of Shares

7.1	Subject to the Corporations Act, this Constitution, and the Founder Protective Provisions (clause 20), the Board may issue, or grant options over, Shares on such terms as the Board determines.

7.2	The Board must not issue Shares, or grant options over Shares, without the prior written approval of the F Share Majority, except:

(a)	Shares issued pursuant to the Qualifying Financing on terms approved by the F Share Majority;

(b)	Shares or options issued under the ESOP within the 10% pool authorised by clause 26; and

7.3	All Shares must be fully paid on issue.

8.	Pre-emptive Rights

8.1	Subject to clauses 8.3 and 8.4, before issuing any new Shares (other than on conversion of existing securities), the Company must first offer those Shares to the existing Members pro rata to their existing holdings (calculated on a Fully Diluted Basis), on the same terms and at the same price as the proposed issue.

PART 3 — TRANSFER OF SHARES

9.	Transfer Restrictions

9.1	A Member must not transfer any Shares except in accordance with this Part 3.

9.2	The Board may, in its absolute discretion, refuse to register a transfer of Shares. The Board is not required to give reasons for any refusal.

9.3	No transfer of Shares may be registered unless:

(a)	the transfer instrument is in a form approved by the Board and is delivered to the Company, together with any certificate for the Shares and such other evidence of title as the Board may require;

(b)	the right of first refusal process in clause 10 has been completed or waived; and

(c)	any applicable restrictions in the SHA have been satisfied.

9.4	Permitted transfers. A Member may transfer Shares without complying with the right of first refusal in clause 10 to:

(a)	a body corporate controlled by that Member (within the meaning of section 50AA of the Corporations Act);

(b)	a trustee of a trust of which that Member (or, in the case of an individual, that individual or their spouse) is a beneficiary;

(c)	in the case of an individual Member, the Member’s spouse, de facto partner, or immediate family member; or

(d)	any person approved in writing by the F Share Majority and (if Series A Preferred Shares are on issue) the Investor Majority,

provided the transferee agrees in writing to be bound by this Constitution and the SHA (if any).

10.	Right of First Refusal

10.1	A Member who wishes to transfer Shares (Selling Member) must, before offering the Shares to any third party, give written notice to the Company (Transfer Notice) specifying the number and class of Shares proposed to be transferred, the proposed price per Share, and the name of the proposed transferee (if known).

10.2	Within 5 Business Days of receiving the Transfer Notice, the Company must give a copy of the Transfer Notice to each other Member.

10.3	Each other Member may, within 20 Business Days of receiving the Transfer Notice (Acceptance Period), give written notice to the Company that it wishes to purchase some or all of the Shares specified in the Transfer Notice, at the price and on the terms specified in the Transfer Notice, pro rata to its existing holding (calculated on a Fully Diluted Basis).

10.4	If the other Members in aggregate elect to purchase all of the Shares specified in the Transfer Notice, the Selling Member must transfer those Shares to the purchasing Members within 10 Business Days of the end of the Acceptance Period.

10.5	If the other Members do not in aggregate elect to purchase all of the Shares specified in the Transfer Notice, the Selling Member may, within 60 Business Days after the end of the Acceptance Period, transfer the unsold Shares to the proposed transferee (or any other bona fide third party) at a price not less than, and on terms no more favourable than, those specified in the Transfer Notice.

10.6	If the Selling Member does not complete the transfer within the period specified in clause 10.5, the Selling Member must recommence the right of first refusal process before making any further transfer.

11.	Drag-Along Rights

11.1	If the F Share Majority resolves to accept a bona fide offer from an arm’s length third party to acquire all of the issued Shares in the Company (Sale Offer), and the Sale Offer has been approved by:

(a)	the F Share Majority; and

(b)	if Series A Preferred Shares are on issue, the Investor Majority,

then each other Member (Dragged Member) must transfer its Shares to the acquirer on the same terms and at the same price per Share (adjusted for class rights) as the F Share Majority and Investor Majority (if applicable) have agreed.

11.2	The Company must give each Dragged Member at least 20 Business Days’ written notice of the Sale Offer, including full details of the price, terms, and identity of the acquirer.

11.3	The price payable to each Dragged Member must be:

(a)	not less than the price per Share payable to the F Share Majority (adjusted for any liquidation preference or priority applicable to the Dragged Member’s class of Shares); and

(b)	determined on a basis that is fair and equitable to all Members, having regard to the rights attaching to each class of Shares.

11.4	No Member may be required to give warranties, indemnities, or representations in connection with a drag-along transfer other than: (a) as to title and authority to transfer; and (b) a several (not joint) pro rata share of any general warranty cap agreed by the selling Members.

12.	Tag-Along Rights

12.1	If any Member or group of Members holding in aggregate 50% or more of the issued Shares (Majority Seller) proposes to transfer Shares to a third party (other than a Permitted Transfer under clause 9.4), each other Member (Tagging Member) has the right to participate in the transfer on the same terms and at the same price per Share (adjusted for class rights).

12.2	The Majority Seller must give each Tagging Member at least 20 Business Days’ written notice of the proposed transfer, including the price, terms, and identity of the proposed acquirer.

12.3	Each Tagging Member may, within 15 Business Days of receiving notice under clause 12.2, give written notice to the Majority Seller that it wishes to include some or all of its Shares in the transfer.

12.4	If the proposed acquirer is unwilling to acquire all of the Shares that Tagging Members wish to include, the number of Shares to be sold by the Majority Seller and each Tagging Member must be reduced pro rata to their respective holdings.

12.5	If the Majority Seller does not comply with this clause 12, the Company must refuse to register the transfer.

13.	Transmission of Shares

13.1	If a Member who is an individual dies, the Company must recognise the Member’s personal representative as having the rights of the deceased Member in relation to the Shares, subject to production of such evidence of title as the Board may require.

13.2	A person who becomes entitled to a Share by reason of the death, bankruptcy, or mental incapacity of a Member may elect to be registered as the holder of the Share or to have some other person registered, subject to clause 9.

14.1	General meetings of the Company must be convened and held in accordance with Part 2G.2 of the Corporations Act.

15.2	If a quorum is not present within 30 minutes of the time appointed for a meeting, the meeting is adjourned to the same day in the following week at the same time and place. If a quorum is not present within 30 minutes at the adjourned meeting, the Members present constitute a quorum.

(a)	all interested Directors must disclose their interests in accordance with clause 18.1;

(b)	the interested Directors may be present and vote on the resolution in reliance on section 194 of the Corporations Act (the Company being a proprietary company);

(c)	the Board must record in the minutes: the nature and extent of each Director’s interest, the disclosure made, the reliance on section 194, and the commercial rationale for the transaction; and

(d)	the Board must consider whether the transaction is on arm’s length terms and, if not, whether it is reasonable and in the best interests of the Company.

Where Chapter 2E requires member approval for a related party benefit, the Company must obtain that approval unless an exception in sections 210–216 applies.

18.6	Record keeping. The Company must maintain a register of all Related Party Transactions approved under this clause 18, including the date, parties, terms, disclosures made, and the basis of approval. This register must be available for inspection by any Director or Member on reasonable notice.

20.	Founder Protective Provisions

20.1	This clause 20 applies while the Founders (directly or through CobaltBlu or any controlled entity) hold Shares representing at least 30% of all issued Shares (on a Fully Diluted Basis).

20.2	The Company must not, and the Board must not resolve to, take any of the following actions without the prior written consent of the F Share Majority:

(b)	create any new class of Shares, or vary or abrogate the rights attaching to any existing class of Shares;

(c)	issue any Shares or securities convertible into Shares, or grant any options, warrants, or other rights to acquire Shares, other than: (i) pursuant to a Qualifying Financing approved by the F Share Majority; or (ii) under the ESOP within the limits of clause 26;

(g)	enter into any merger, consolidation, scheme of arrangement, or other business combination;

(h)	sell, transfer, or dispose of all or substantially all of the Company’s assets, or any asset or group of related assets with a value exceeding A$1,000,000;

(i)	voluntarily wind up the Company, appoint an administrator, or pass a resolution for the Company to enter into any form of external administration;

(o)	adopt or materially amend the Company’s annual budget or business plan;

(p)	enter into any agreement that restricts the Company’s ability to conduct its business in any material respect; and

(q)	approve any transaction or series of related transactions outside the ordinary course of business with a value exceeding A$500,000.

CONFLICT OF INTEREST POLICY

1.  Purpose

This policy establishes the framework by which directors and officers of WaterRoads Pty Ltd (the Company) identify, disclose, and manage conflicts of interest and material personal interests in connection with the affairs of the Company. It is designed to:

(a) ensure compliance with sections 191, 192, 194, and 195 of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) (the Act) and Chapter 2E (Related Party Transactions);

(b) satisfy the disinterested director and related-party provisions of the Company’s Constitution (including Clause 48.1);

(c) provide a clear, auditable governance trail for investors, regulators, and future board members;

(d) meet the governance standards expected by institutional investors, including private equity and venture capital funds undertaking due diligence; and

(e) establish practical procedures that reflect the Company’s current board composition and transition to independent board oversight following the Series A investment.

2.  Scope

This policy applies to all directors of the Company, including any alternate directors, and to any officer or senior manager who participates in board-level decision-making or has authority to bind the Company.

Series A Transition: Upon appointment of an independent director (targeted at or within 60 days of the Series A close), this policy continues to apply to all directors, and the enhanced procedures in Section 7 (Independent Director Oversight) will activate automatically.

Post-Series A Alignment: Within 30 days of Series A close, this policy will be reviewed to ensure alignment with the shareholders’ agreement, reserved matters, investor consent rights, and board composition requirements.

5.  Disclosure Obligations

5.1  Statutory Disclosure (s191/s192)

A director who has a material personal interest in a matter relating to the affairs of the Company must, as soon as practicable after becoming aware of the interest, disclose the nature and extent of that interest to the other directors. Disclosure may be given:

(a) orally at a board meeting, to be recorded in the minutes of that meeting (s191); or

(b) by Standing Notice in writing, tabled at the next board meeting (s192).

Penalty for non-disclosure: Section 191(1) creates a strict liability offence carrying a penalty of up to 50 penalty units (currently A$22,200) per director per undisclosed matter.

5.2  When Disclosure Is Required

Disclosure is required whenever a director has, or may reasonably be perceived to have, a material personal interest in a matter before the Board. This includes, without limitation:

(a) any transaction, arrangement, or dealing between the Company and CobaltBlu, CBS Group, or any entity in which a director holds an interest;

(b) any matter that confers or may confer a financial benefit on a related party (as those terms are defined in sections 228–229 of the Act);

(c) any matter in which a director’s spouse, de facto partner, or close family member has a material interest;

(d) any matter in which a director has a conflicting duty to another entity (including a directorship obligation to CobaltBlu or CBS Group);

(e) approval of the Company’s remuneration, equity grants, or benefits to directors or their associates;

(f) any contract or proposed contract in which a director has a direct or indirect pecuniary interest; and

(g) any matter where the director’s judgment may reasonably be perceived as influenced by interests other than the Company’s interests.

5.3  Standing Notices

Where a director’s interest in a matter is of an ongoing nature (for example, a directorship of another entity, a shareholding, or a spousal relationship), the director should give a Standing Notice under section 192. A Standing Notice must:

(a) be in writing;

(b) set out the nature and extent of the interest;

(c) include explicit acknowledgment of the director’s obligations under this Policy;

(d) include a statement that the notice extends to any matter in which the interests may reasonably be perceived to influence the director’s judgment;

(e) be tabled at the next board meeting after it is given; and

(f) be updated whenever the nature or extent of the interest materially changes.

A Standing Notice has ongoing effect and covers all matters within its stated scope. It does not need to be re-given at each meeting, but the Chair should note at the commencement of each meeting whether any Standing Notices are in effect and whether any updates are required.

5.4  Timing and Form

On appointment: Each new director must complete a disclosure form and provide a Standing Notice (if applicable) before attending their first board meeting.

Annually: Each director must confirm annually that their Standing Notice remains current or provide an updated notice.

As conflicts arise: Directors must disclose new conflicts immediately upon becoming aware of them.

At meetings: The Chair will ask for conflict declarations at the start of each meeting for matters on the agenda.

6.  Procedures for Managing Conflicts

6.1  General Procedure

When a conflict of interest or material personal interest is identified in relation to a matter before the Board, the following procedure applies:

Step 1 — Disclosure. The conflicted director discloses the interest (if not already covered by a Standing Notice). The disclosure is recorded in the minutes.

Step 2 — Assessment. The Board (or, where available, the disinterested directors) assesses the nature and severity of the conflict. The assessment considers:

• whether the interest is direct or indirect;

• whether it is financial or non-financial;

• the materiality of the interest and the transaction;

• whether it could objectively influence the director’s judgment;

• whether there is a reasonable perception of conflict; and

• whether Independent Advice or other safeguards are required.

Step 3 — Management. Based on the assessment, the Board applies one or more of the following management strategies:

Step 4 — Record. The minutes of the meeting must record the conflict management approach taken, including:

• which strategy was applied and why;

• if s194 reliance, the commercial rationale for the decision;

• if Independent Advice obtained, the conclusions and where it is filed;

• cross-reference to Register of Interests entry (Appendix B); and

• for Material Transactions, confirmation of arm’s length terms or member approval obtained.

Step 5 — Register Update. The Company Secretary (or, upon activation of Section 11.1, the Governance Custodian) updates the Register of Interests (Appendix B) and Related Party Transaction Register (Appendix A).

The following standing protocols apply to known, recurring conflicts:

6.2.1  CBS Group Matters

6.2.2  CobaltBlu Matters

(a) both directors disclose their interests (covered by Standing Notices);

(d) for Material Transactions, the Board obtains Independent Advice (legal, financial, or valuation) to confirm arm’s length terms; and

(e) the rationale emphasises that the transaction serves the Company’s commercial interests and strategic objectives.

Rationale: With both directors equally interested, recusal would leave no quorum. Section 194 participation with full disclosure is the only available mechanism until an independent director is appointed. Independent Advice provides external validation of commercial reasonableness.

6.2.3  Director Remuneration and Benefits

Decisions on director remuneration, equity grants, expense reimbursement, or other benefits to directors or their associates require:

(a) disclosure of the interest by the relevant director(s);

(b) where an independent director has been appointed, approval by the independent director(s);

(c) where no independent director is available, both directors vote under s194 with the decision benchmarked against market comparables (including comparable roles at similar-stage companies, industry surveys, or adviser recommendations) and documented in the minutes;

(d) consideration of whether member approval is required under Chapter 2E (s208); and

(e) for Material Transactions or non-standard arrangements, Independent Advice on market terms.

6.2.4  Materiality Guidance

To assist in applying the conflict management framework, the Board has adopted the following materiality guidance:

Minor conflicts (typically managed via monitoring):

• Transactions under A$10,000 on arm’s length terms

• Standard commercial hospitality or gifts of trivial value (under A$250)

• Matters where the director’s interest is remote or purely theoretical

Material conflicts (require recusal, Independent Advice, or heightened safeguards):

• Transactions exceeding A$50,000

• Any transaction involving non-standard or preferential terms

• Any transaction where the director has competing fiduciary duties

• Director remuneration or equity grants

• Enforcement, waiver, or variation of existing related party contracts

When in doubt, directors should err on the side of disclosure and seek guidance from the Chair or Company Secretary.
