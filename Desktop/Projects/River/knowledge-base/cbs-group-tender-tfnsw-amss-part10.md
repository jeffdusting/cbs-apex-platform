Executive summary

Situation: Traditional models of consultant engagement leave all value risk with the client. Consultants are paid for their time and deliverables regardless of whether their recommendations generate tangible benefits. This creates a fundamental misalignment of incentives—the consultant's commercial success is disconnected from the client's actual outcomes. Similarly, standard contract terms allocate risks to the consultant that come at a cost yet provide no corresponding benefit to the client when those risks do not materialise.

Complication: Value in complex infrastructure engagements is inherently uncertain. Even well-conceived initiatives may not deliver anticipated benefits due to implementation challenges, changing circumstances or unforeseen constraints. Under traditional arrangements, TfNSW bears this uncertainty entirely while consultants face no consequence for recommendations that fail to materialise as value. Meanwhile, contract terms that transfer risk to consultants simply result in higher fees to cover contingencies that may never eventuate—cost without benefit.

Response: CBS Group proposes an alternate engagement model built on two complementary mechanisms.

Traditional Engagement on Collaborative Contract Terms: This mechanism provides for engagement of our core and supplementary teams on amended commercial terms. TfNSW acceptance of these terms allows a reduction in CBS Group costs and risk premium - enabling reduced fees. This creates a direct exchange: TfNSW accepts manageable risks in return for tangible cost savings

Value Based Price (VBP) Method: Value Based Pricing structures our compensation around shared risk and aligned returns, operating on three foundational principles: shared conviction (both parties agree there is genuine possibility of value before work commences), co-investment (CBS Group assigns appropriate specialists on a fixed retainer basis, with value share agreed initiative-by-initiative), and aligned returns (if value is delivered, we share in it; if not, our returns are limited accordingly). 

We bring a suite of proven asset management products, methodologies and analytical tools that have delivered more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years. It is with this capability and track record that we ca accept shared risk with confidence.

Mechanism 1 is detailed at section 4 of this Alternate Proposal.  The remainder of this document has been drafted to explain the value and operation of Mechanism 2 (VBP).

We look forward to working through the our seed list of initiatives and the necessary commercial amendments to rapidly enable this model and start driving real value to support the Asset Financial Sustainability (AFS) targets set for the organisation.

This alternative framework is built on two distinct and complementary engagement pathways, allowing TfNSW to select the most suitable approach for any given initiative.

This pathway follows a conventional consultancy model. Services are provided at market rates for a defined scope of work. This option is suitable for projects where the scope and deliverables are clearly defined from the outset and where a traditional risk allocation is preferred. Services are provided at market rates for a defined scope of work. Work orders do not include value share provisions.

Method 2: Shared-Risk, Value-Based Engagement

This pathway is founded on a partnership model. CBSE accepts a significantly reduced monthly retainer, representing a substantial investment from our side. In return, we participate in the value we help create through a pre-agreed sharing mechanism on validated benefits. This model ensures that both organisations share in the risks and rewards, creating a strong alignment of interests.

Selection Criteria

The choice between Option A and Option B would be guided by the nature of the project.

Method 1 is the preferred choice for more straightforward engagements. This includes defined-scope studies, technical investigations, or when seeking short-term expert advice. It is also appropriate for engagements where a more conservative, traditional risk allocation is desired

Method 2 is particularly well-suited for initiatives with the potential for significant value creation, such as long-term transformation programs or projects that require innovative solutions. It is most effective in situations where a deep, collaborative partnership and fully aligned incentives are critical to success.

The Problem with Traditional Consulting

The conventional consulting model contains a structural flaw that limits its effectiveness for infrastructure agencies. Under traditional fee-for-service arrangements, consultants are compensated for effort expended rather than value delivered.

This misalignment creates predictable consequences:

Infrastructure asset management initiatives involve inherent uncertainty. Even expertly designed interventions may not achieve intended outcomes due to organisational readiness, competing priorities, market conditions, or technical constraints that emerge during implementation. This uncertainty is not a failure of planning—it is a reality of complex systems.

The question is not whether to accept this uncertainty, but how to allocate it fairly between client and consultant. Under traditional models, TfNSW accepts all of this uncertainty while consultants face no consequence for recommendations that fail to materialise as value.

The Value-Based Engagement Model

Core Principles

The value-based engagement model fundamentally restructures the consultant-client relationship around three principles that address the limitations of traditional arrangements:

Principle 1: Shared Conviction

Before any initiative commences, both TfNSW and CBS Group must agree that a genuine possibility of value exists. This is not a formality—it requires both parties to review the proposed intervention, examine the evidence base, and commit resources only where there is mutual confidence in the potential for measurable improvement.

Principle 2: Co-Investment

CBS Group assigns a team of appropriate specialists to agreed initiatives on a retainer basis. The value target, task duration and value share percentage are agreed on an initiative-by-initiative basis. CBS Group's initial fees are set at 40-50% of market rates, representing our investment in the partnership. This reduced fee is not a discount—it reflects our willingness to accept a lower certain return in exchange for participation in value upside.

Principle 3: Aligned Returns

If value is delivered and independently validated, CBS Group shares in that value through benefit-sharing payments. If value does not materialise, our returns are limited to the reduced retainer. This structure ensures that CBS Group's commercial success depends directly on TfNSW's outcomes.

CBS Group Principles for Using CATALYST 7™ and Applying Value-Based Pricing

Overview

CATALYST 7™ is CBS Group's proprietary framework for delivering and measuring value across infrastructure advisory engagements. Developed through over a decade of application and more than $1 billion in documented savings for Transport for NSW and other major infrastructure clients, CATALYST 7™ provides a comprehensive methodology that moves beyond traditional time-and-materials consulting to create strategic partnerships where compensation is directly tied to measurable value creation.

The framework recognises that infrastructure value extends far beyond financial returns to encompass seven interconnected capital dimensions, enabling CBS Group to identify, quantify, and deliver holistic value that benefits all stakeholders.

The Seven Capitals Framework

CATALYST 7™ measures and optimises value across seven interconnected capital dimensions:

Value Multiplication Principles

CATALYST 7™ recognises that the seven capitals form an interconnected system where interventions in one dimension create ripple effects across others. CBS Group actively designs interventions to maximise synergistic effects through three mechanisms:

Cross-Capital Synergies: Interventions in one capital dimension enhance value creation in another (e.g., Digital Capital investments enhance Manufactured Capital performance while improving Natural Capital outcomes).

Cascading Value Effects: Value created in one dimension triggers sequential value creation in others through mapped pathways.

Reinforcing Feedback Loops: Value creation across multiple dimensions establishes self-reinforcing cycles that compound over time.

Typical value multipliers range from 1.8x–2.7x for two-dimensional interactions, 2.5x–3.8x for three-dimensional synergies, and 3.2x–4.7x for comprehensive multi-dimensional integration.

Value-Based Pricing Commercial Model

Dual-Component Structure

CBS Group's VBP model comprises two components designed to balance budget predictability with strong performance incentives:

Strategic Partnership Retainer: Fixed monthly fee covering strategic advisory, methodology development, knowledge transfer, governance support, and continuous improvement activities.

Value-Based Success Fees: Calculated as a percentage of independently validated value creation across the seven capitals, typically representing 60–80% of total CBS compensation in successful engagements.

Measurement and Validation

CATALYST 7™ applies rigorous measurement protocols to ensure credible value creation claims:

Baseline Rigor: Comprehensive baseline assessment incorporating 3–5 years of historical performance data, industry benchmarks, and explicit counterfactual analysis.

Conservative Estimation: Lower-bound estimates, time-value discounting, and exclusion of weakly attributed value categories.

Independent Validation: All material value claims (>$1M) undergo third-party validation by qualified assessors, with full methodology and data transparency.

Performance Guarantees

CBS Group typically guarantees minimum value creation of 3x–5x total retainer fees over the engagement period, demonstrating confidence in the CATALYST 7™ methodology. Remediation mechanisms include success fee waivers, partial retainer refunds, and extended engagement commitments if guaranteed minimums are not achieved.

Perfect Incentive Alignment

CATALYST 7™ VBP fundamentally restructures the consultant-client relationship:

Outcome-Based Compensation: CBS Group's compensation is calculated as a percentage of documented value creation, ensuring CBS earns more when clients achieve more value.

Risk-Sharing: CBS Group bears significant performance risk—if interventions fail to create value, CBS receives only the base retainer without success fees.

Long-Term Partnership: Engagements typically span 3–5 years with phased success fee payments tied to sustained value creation.

Capability Transfer: Structured knowledge transfer builds client capabilities over time, enabling increasingly independent value creation.

Summary

CATALYST 7™ represents a fundamental shift from traditional consulting to genuine strategic partnership. By measuring and optimising value across seven interconnected capitals, applying rigorous baseline and validation protocols, and structuring commercial arrangements around shared risk and reward, CBS Group delivers transformative outcomes where consultant success depends entirely on delivering measurable, sustainable client value.

Appendix F: VBP Standards Rationalisation Approach

Executive Summary

This appendix describes a value-based initiative for systematic review and rationalisation of TfNSW Standards, operating within the VBP framework established in the main proposal. The initiative addresses three objectives: reducing the compliance burden on projects, reducing standards maintenance costs for TfNSW, and applying a risk-based methodology to enable innovation.

The approach involves identifying a target asset class, mapping TfNSW standards against existing Australian and international standards to reduce TfNSW-unique content to genuinely unique requirements, then identifying opportunities to explicitly allow creative design solutions that implement whole-of-life innovations.

The intended outcomes are less unique standards volume to manage (with associated internal cost reduction), reduced compliance and project costs, and greater opportunity for innovation with reduced lock-in to past precedents that may no longer serve TfNSW's interests.

1.1 Integration with Method 2 Engagement

As with all VBP initiatives, this programme follows the established process comprising identification and scoping, agreement of terms and execution of a Project Schedule, delivery, and value realisation.

The three foundational principles that govern all Method 2 engagements apply equally to this initiative:

Shared Conviction: Both TfNSW and CBS Group must agree that genuine value creation potential exists in standards rationalisation before work commences.

Co-Investment: CBS Group undertakes analysis and development work at reduced retainer rates, reflecting our investment in the partnership.

Aligned Returns: If value is delivered and independently validated, CBS Group shares in that value. If value does not materialise, our returns are limited to the reduced retainer.

2. Objectives

The Standards Rationalisation initiative addresses three distinct but related objectives:

Reduce Compliance Burden: Simplify standards to reduce the compliance effort required on projects, lowering project delivery costs and timelines.

Reduce Standards Maintenance Cost: Decrease the volume of TfNSW-unique standards requiring ongoing maintenance, freeing internal resources currently dedicated to standards administration.

Enable Innovation: Apply a risk-based methodology to remove unnecessary prescription and explicitly allow creative design solutions that deliver whole-of-life value.

2.1 The Problem

TfNSW maintains a substantial portfolio of transport standards, many of which duplicate or exceed requirements already established in Australian or international standards. This creates several challenges:

Unnecessary Uniqueness: Standards that replicate AS/ISO requirements with minor TfNSW variations create compliance complexity without commensurate benefit.

Maintenance Burden: Each unique standard requires ongoing review, update, and stakeholder management—consuming internal resources.

Innovation Barriers: Prescriptive specifications prevent adoption of proven technologies and lock projects into past precedents that may no longer represent best practice.

Project Cost Impact: Compliance with duplicative or overly prescriptive standards adds cost to projects without delivering proportionate value.

3. Approach

The initiative follows a two-stage approach, commencing with a target asset class to focus the rationalisation effort.

3.1 Target Asset Class Selection

The initiative commences with selection of a target asset class to focus the rationalisation effort. Selection criteria include volume of applicable standards, potential for cost reduction, alignment with TfNSW strategic priorities, and readiness for implementation. The recommended starting point would be agreed with TfNSW during Project Schedule development.

3.2 Stage 1: Standards Mapping and Reduction

The first stage maps TfNSW standards against existing Australian (AS) and international (ISO, EN) standards to identify where TfNSW requirements duplicate or marginally vary from established frameworks. The objective is to reduce TfNSW-unique content to genuinely unique requirements—those elements where TfNSW's operating context necessitates departure from industry norms.

This stage involves the following activities:

Map each TfNSW standard against corresponding AS/ISO/EN frameworks

Identify clauses that duplicate or marginally vary from referenced standards

Assess whether TfNSW-specific variations are justified by genuine operational requirements

Recommend consolidation to reference external standards where appropriate

Retain only genuinely unique TfNSW requirements within rationalised standards

3.3 Stage 2: Enabling Innovation

The second stage identifies opportunities within rationalised standards to explicitly allow creative design solutions that implement whole-of-life (WoL) innovations. This involves shifting from prescriptive specifications (which lock in past precedents) to performance-based requirements (which define outcomes and allow flexibility in how those outcomes are achieved).

This stage involves the following activities:

Identify prescriptive clauses that constrain innovation without commensurate risk mitigation

Apply risk-based assessment to determine where flexibility can be safely introduced

Draft performance-based alternatives that define required outcomes rather than mandated methods

Create pathways for adoption of proven technologies (e.g., sustainable materials, smart systems)

Validate proposed changes through stakeholder engagement and pilot implementation

3.4 Stage 3

Work through the formal standards change and concession processes ensuring technical operational and custodian input is sought and accommodated and that the value is realised.

Communicate the change to impacted projects and asset stewards to ensure that the value is realised in project delivery and operations phases.
