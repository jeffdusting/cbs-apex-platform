This section will overview common technology choices and some guidance in the factors that will factor into STC’s decision on selecting one solution while assessing tolling solution proposals.

STC has some constraints that need to be noted in choices that can be made for regarding technology, specifically the location of data and services that STC utilise which is particularly relevant when assessing cloud and outsourced solutions.

STC data and systems will need to be hosted only within Australia and have no aspect of the service that is homed in alternate geographies.

STC call centre operations and the supporting services, including technologies, are to be based in Victoria.

Factors to be Assessed for Cloud or On-Premise Solutions

The factors to be considered when assessing cloud on premise solutions are:-

Funding Model

Cloud services have less upfront capital costs (depending on financial standards utilised by the company even initial costs may be determined as Operational Expenditure), however require monthly license fees to maintain, so the underlying compute capability needs to be budgeted for in operational budgets

Traditional data centre hosted hardware and software requires a large up front and periodic capital expenditure however the ongoing run costs will be substantially lower as software licensing and the hosting costs will be the only costs to be  by operational budgets. Due to depreciation and tax treatments this form of funding can be more efficient than the cloud model.

The monthly hosting cost of cloud computing can be calculated upfront, however . These offerings sometimes include the hosting cost in the charge, however as STC has limitations on additional charges can be incurred in housing the service in Australia (implementation cost) and ongoing additional monthly charges to manage the separated service.

Operational Complexity

Cloud services remove a large amount of operational complexity and cost from technology operations as the data centre hosting and compute layers are all already established and are managed for the service to utilise. This reduces the upfront delivery risk in establishing these capabilities and removes the need to maintain these skills and teams within the technology operations (whether internally sourced or outsourced).

The cloud service provider will also maintain the service currency, including patching, failover testing for infrastructure business continuity and general maintenance of the service to ensure the applications are available and performant

An on premise hosted service, requires infrastructure to be procured, teams to be setup to build and maintain (either in house or outsourced) the Data Centre, Compute, Storage and Connectivity to allow the solution to be operational and these teams will need to manage the security and feature patching, ensure that the servers and systems are operating as expected and secure on a daily basis.

Security and Compliance

Cloud services provide security capabilities such as Intrusion Detection and Prevention, DDoS, Virus and Firewall as part of their services, however the security is offered as part of securing the tenancy and usually is not specifically tailored for specific tenant requirements.

In terms of compliance, there are established standards for cloud applications that have allowed for cloud services to meet rigorous compliance requirements, however these requirements can add additional expense onto the base cloud hosting costs depending on the contract

On premise systems can be setup from the start to meet the security requirements to comply with regulation and contractual obligations. By nature on premise hosted services are also more secure as the networking between the data centre and offices can be isolated and secured as part of design.

Lifecycle Management

On premise hosting requires periodic investment in renewing software and hardware as STC would be required to manage the lifecycle of the hardware required to host the service as well as the software utilised by the service as well. These changes can also be disruptive to business operations and have opportunity cost in preventing other projects and initiatives from being executed while these changes are implemented.

Cloud services remove a large amount of lifecycle management from the maintenance of the solution, leaving only lifecycle changes that entail the solution itself, greatly reducing the level of disruption and management needed to maintain the solution.

Different Styles of Cloud Solution

These three terms are mentioned a lot in the last few years  service offerings and solutions. They may be applicable to the solutions that STC considers for  Tolling Solution.

These different terms will be utilised, sometimes as options within the pitch for a service. I have included information of what these terms mean and how they may impact STC and the Tolling Solution

Public Cloud

Public cloud is a cloud deployment model where computing resources are owned and operated by a provider and shared across multiple tenants via the Internet. Typically these clouds are maintained by the large cloud providers, such as AWS and Microsoft Azure. These deployment types do not preclude geographical locking, to keep data and services within Australia. However Public services do not usually allow for specific requirements around security and isolation of data, so can be difficult to achieve specific compliance outcomes if required.

Private Cloud

A private cloud is a service that is completely controlled by a single organization and not shared with others. This service could be a hosted data centre that has been setup to resemble a typical cloud providers environment or, more commonly, it is a specific cloud instance that is only accessible by the specific tenant. These environments are typically setup to meet specific security and compliance requirements for clients. The disadvantage of these setups is the cost of the environment is significantly higher than a public cloud as the maintenance cost cannot be shared across the tenant base. Also there can be a risk that currency of software is not maintained if the environment is not being managed well.

Hybrid Cloud

Hybrid cloud is the combination of Public and Private clouds, usually done to orchestrate a single service between the two environments. This can be done for resilience and business continuity purposes or as a way to transition existing on premise environments into a cloud service. These hybrid clouds do offer the advantage of achieving multi cloud vendor independence with the operational cost and complexity overhead of having to maintain the multiple environments.

System Integration & Management

The Tolling Solution will need to interact with systems within STC, such as financial systems as well as integrate with foreign toll operators to . To achieve this the Tolling system will need to be integrated with these services, and may also require integration the Tolling Solution depending on how it is procured and built.

There are three “styles” of integration and the associated Integration management that can be selected to manage the integration of the Tolling Solution: Point to Point Integration, Hub and Spoke and Enterprise Service Bus (ESB). There are factors that lead to selecting the integration solution and then the level of integration management as part of the solution.

Point to Point Integration

Simplified Example of Point to Point Integration

Point to Point integration is the simplest, most direct form of system integration in that each system integrates directly with the components it needs to orchestrate the service. These direct connections can be a variety of technical integration types, API or Application Programming Interface, Data Integration or web interface integration.

Advantages

The advantage of Point to Point integration is that the integrations can be built and tested fairly quickly and comparatively cheaply. The custom code will be built specifically for the interface and can be customised to meet organisational requirements specifically as part of the build with a minimum of constraints.

Disadvantages

The disadvantages of Point to Point integration can be:

Scalability – Point to Point integrations are designed to be single function integrations that meet a specific need. As a business operation scales out, for each additional function a new integration pattern will be required to be developed.

Interdependence – The systems that are integrated in this manner functionally and operationally are interdependent and any changes or errors in one system will impact the integrated system. This can lead to simple enhancements and changes having widespread impacts and operational issues cascading through the broader solution

Operational Complexity – Without the implementation of a tool to monitor and manage the integration of the systems, there is no way for an operator to hold flow of integrations while investigating issues, view integration errors and logs in a centralised view. 

Knowledge Management – As the point to point integrations are built as part of each application for a specific purpose, it can be difficult to maintain documentation and common understanding of the interfaces, as well dependence on specific members of application teams who built the integrations

Security – As there are a number of individual interfaces between applications and no centralised pattern securing the interfaces and ensuring the security of the interfaces and reviewing the traffic for malicious activities is more difficult raising the risk level to the solution.

Integration Management and Operations

A integration management system can be put in place to manage point to point integration, with the integrations being built in the integration platform and connected to each system or application requiring integration. The addition of this software alleviates some of the disadvantages of Point to Point integration, such as visibility and management of the integration patterns and operations

However the implementation of the integration management platform to reduce the risk to operations does reduce the advantage of point to point integration in that the cost to build and maintain will increase. Also the other disadvantages of dependence because of tight coupling of the system and risks with knowledge management still remain.

Hub and Spoke Integration

Simplified Example of Hub and Spoke Integration

Hub and Spoke Integration represents the evolution of Technology, trying to alleviate the drawbacks of point to point integration through the implementation of a Central Integration Broker (Hub) with Integration to applications via Adaptors (the spokes). The Integration Hub is a centralised piece of software that orchestrates the passing of messages between the applications. The hub performs all the translation and routing of messages between the applications. A form of the hub model of integration is the API Gateway which is most often used to describe an integration management point that sits between web applications and internal systems, but can also be utilised in a similar manner to an integration hub.

Advantages

The advantages of this model the improvement in operational management of integration and the solution overall and the standardisation of integration across the solution improving maintainability. The hub maintains the format and function of the interfaces, which centralises the capability to integrate the system and manages the impact of change and enhancement across the solution.

Disadvantages

The disadvantages of this approach can be

Single Point of Failure: Once the Integration Hub is established the hub becomes critical to the end to end function of the integration solution, so if the hub has issues then integration between the applications is impacted or halted.

Scalability: The integration hub as a single system manages all the traffic between the systems which can form a challenge to scaling due to performance and compute capacity limitations unless carefully managed.

Operational Complexity: The integration hub, while resolving some operational concerns, does add some operational complexity in case of failure of the hub and recovery as the hub itself and all impacted traffic would need to be recovered after a failure.

Cascading Impacts: Due to the centralised nature of the hub, the impacts of changes (anticipated or unanticipated) and of incidents can have unexpected consequences throughout the integrated solution. 

Integration Management and Operations

With the hub and spoke model of integration it is fundamentally easier to add operational management and control to the  to day operations of the integration hub. This can be achieved either through the implementation of a packaged integration management solution or the connection of a bespoke built integration hub with an operational reporting and dashboarding solution

Enterprise Integration (Enterprise Service Bus)

Simplified Example of Enterprise Service Bus Integration

The Enterprise Service Bus integration method evolves the hub and spoke integration method to decouple the applications through the implementation of an intermediary messaging system (the bus) that maintains a separate standard messaging form without the systems needing to understand anything about the other systems reachable on the bus. There is an adapter for each application that translates the application into the messaging format of the bus, which is standardised across the solution and translated for each system within the solution. While this is superficially similar to the hub and spoke method of integration the function and processing of transformation of messages between applications does not reside centrally but is located in each of the application adapters, distributing the load.

Advantages

The ESB style of integration  decouples and segments the components of solution which allows for changes and enhancements to be more effectively isolated within each component of the solution. This style of integration allows for more efficient scaling as each interfacing component is added to the solution, and adapter is built for the component and the additional processing is managed by the adapter and not weighing on a centralised system. This horizontal allows for the addition of additional functions and capabilities to the overall solution without needing to add additional capacity or functions to a central integration management hub.

Disadvantages

The main disadvantages of ESB integration can be:

Performance: Due to the constant translation of data and messages from source applications to ESB messages and then back to target application format, ESB’s can cause performance issues if the translations are not well managed.

Resiliency: While the bus architecture decouples the applications and the method of translation will limit the impact of in flight transactions, the bus architecture is still open to single points of failure in the message management and distribution layer which would impact the delivery of integration between applications and impede solution function.

Integration Management and Operations

This form of integration will require the implementation of a Middleware Integration Platform to manage the bus distribution layer of the integration. The advantage of this is that the platforms have a universal operational view of the integration layer and tools to manage integration between applications (assuming a reasonably mature integration platform). The negatives to this can be that some of these platforms are complex and require specialist and specific product knowledge and skills to operate and maintain.

System Operations Management

Regardless of the operational monitoring solution that is selected it is important that the operational capabilities of the system are developed in line with the functional capabilities. A full set of capabilities supporting operations will enable STC to simplify operating the Tolling Solution and enable the operations to be more easily orchestrated between internal and external service providers.

The ability to monitor and understand end to end solution health at any point in time as well as predict system health issues to proactively maintain the solution end to end will improve the operations teams Mean Time to Recovery as well as increase the Mean Time between Failures. This will drive the outcome of ensuring that the Tolling solution is available and performing as expected, but also more accurately achieving the correct Toll Collection outcomes.

The development of operational dashboards that cover technology solution status as well as tolling outcomes and process views is key in enabling informed operational decisions that resolve issues with the minimum of impact to Tolling operations.

The core of these capabilities is the ability to trace the outcome of Trips from passage detection to settlement, Detection to Collection, on an ongoing live basis. This capability enables true end to end understanding of Tolling outcomes and informs analysis and tuning of tolling processes as well as detecting underlying or developing issues from the outcomes of processing logic.

System Health Monitoring

The System Health Monitoring capability provides the core of the Operational Management capabilities. Being fed the logs of all systems and devices, allowing the aggregation of events and trends across the organisation with the capability to analyse this data to determine both issues in progress as well as predict issues that are developing. This capability provides alerts to operational teams to ensure critical issues are managed with priority. 

The predictive algorithm will develop over time from observing the system as the monitoring solution and the operational team get experience in the solution end to end. Once tuned this function will analyse the logs provided to match previous issues or notices trends in reduced performance or patterns of certain types of outcomes, which may be due to system change or a degradation in devices or systems.

Operational Dashboards and Reporting

This capability provides continual and easy to digest information on the current state of the system as well as the ability to produce reports for in depth analysis.

The key for the operational reporting and insights is that business process outcomes and system states are tied together to make actionable insights from the data. The development of the dashboard and reporting needs to prioritise showing both the system health and outcomes in views that available to operational teams to ensure informed decisions are made when rectifying system issues.

Tolling Outcome Traceability

The key to the operational capabilities is building into the Tolling Solutions the capability to easily trace passage and trip events through to settlement with full traceability through all stages of Toll Collection.

This information will enable up to the minute analysis and understanding of financial outcomes of the Tolling process and enable adjustments to system and processes that enhance the collection of Tolls from tolling events.

As the selected solution will orchestrate the messaging within the Tolling Solution, the integration pattern for any remaining interfaces with systems outside the Toling Back Office should be developed as Point-to-Point Integration. If the solution selected is a cloud hosted solution, very likely to be a Software as a Service solution. These solutions as a baseline are by default hosted on Public Cloud solutions. The role of System Integrator during the development stage of the solution development will need to be resolved, with the SI having the responsibility for delivery of the integrated solution and ensuring the end-to-end outcomes meet STC’s requirements.

Hosting Choice

Due to the mix of solutions and products, STC will need to develop the hosting environment for the Tolling solution. This option may also introduce some additional risk during the establishment stage in correctly sizing the environment before the performance benchmarks have been established.

Finally a multi-cloud/multi-hosting solution would match each component to that component’s ideal hosting environment. This approach will introduce added complexity into STC’s operating environment and likely additional cost in establishing and in operational costs.

In a Best of Breed approach the operational management requirements become critical to the success of the platform and the ability of STC to effectively operate the solution once live. The need to understand the System Health of each component is of course necessary, but managing flow between components becomes critical to ensure smooth Tolling Operations which requires detailed understanding of system state.

The need to trace Tolling Outcomes throughout the lifecycle of tolling becomes critical in this sourcing choice and will need to built into the reporting and system status logging in every component in a unified manner to ensure that this capability is developed and usable by the operations team.

Operational Scenarios and Impacts on Solution

Operational Scenarios

There are three broad roles that STC can adopt as Tolling operator that has some influence over the Tolling Solution domains and capabilities that need to be developed.

These three positions are anchor points along a spectrum of possibilities and it is possible that STC adopts a position that is more nuanced than the archetypal positions depicted below

The three broad positions are:-

Contract Manager (Full Outsourced) – in this model STC does not maintain any direct control over the Tolling Solution and manage the providers to deliver the service promised and direct the providers to deliver tolling outcomes

Tolling Operator with Selective Sourcing – in this model STC outsources select parts of the Tolling ecosystem (such as the call centre or roadside device maintenance) while maintaining internal teams operating the tolling process and solution

Full Internal Operations – STC take responsibility for operating all aspects of the Tolling process and underlying solution

In the sections below this document will outline areas that are of particular importance in each of these scenarios, with the second and third roles combined into one section, that needs to be borne in mind when choosing an operational position. These call outs are not meant to minimise the remaining requirements and domains but are critical to alleviating potential weaknesses inherent in the scenario.

Contract Manager Role

In this role due to the indirect control of the solution and the tolling process, it is imperative that the Tolling domains that provide information on the solution and the tolling outcomes are fully developed and functional. In addition to this it will be incumbent on STC to develop strong knowledge management processes and a knowledgeable management team in the mechanics of the processing of the system to ensure STC is successful in this role.

Tolling Operational Visibility

The full development of the Operational Management capabilities and STC maintaining a team capable of understanding the operational outcomes presented in the dashboard will enable an active management role in tolling operations. To achieve this requires strong requirements and investment in Operational Readiness during the build phase of the tolling solution will ensure these capabilities and teams are developed and ready for go live.

Reporting and Insights

The companion capability to the operational and system visibility and management, in this operational scenario is a full featured reporting and insights capability with full feed of all tolling data. This will allow STC to analyse trends and provide direction on enhancements or process changes that improve tolling outcomes as well as direct improvements in service.

Knowledge Management

A full and well executed knowledge management process and function will be critical for STC to retain provider independence and control of the tolling process. Investment in this process as part of Operational Readiness will set a standard for knowledge retention through the build phase that will need to be maintained throughout STC’s operational management of the system as well as ensuring that operational information is provided from the outsourced segments of the process.

Tolling Operator Role

In this role STC either fully operate or selectively source part of the tolling function for areas of the process that make sense, but on the whole STC remain as the orchestrator of the service and the outcome of the tolling process. Critical to success in this role will be a well established and mature integration management function that allows for seamless orchestration of tolling outcomes.

Integration Management

Well developed Integration capabilities and standards will be necessary to ensure that STC achieve the service outcomes that would be envisioned in agreeing to the service outsource. Ensuring this would mean that the interface provides all the information that the service provider requires to orchestrate their service, the interface includes full auditing information and there are appropriate controls on flow of data and state of data.

Operational Visibility

Ensuring that the provides operational data feeds for STC data as part of the service will ensure that STC is able to effectively manage provider service levels and potentially improve overall tolling outcomes. To achieve this it will be necessary to build into the integration, interfaces and contracts with service providers that obligation to provide operational insights into their processing and accurate status and updates on tolling events to ensure that STC retains full traceability on tolling events to measure effectiveness of the process.

Depth of Customer Relationship

STC has a decision to make on the level of relationship that STC wishes to maintain with their road users and potentially customers.

Within the Tolling Agreement STC needs to provide the capability for road users to purchase casual products to allow travel on NEL on an ad hoc basis. STC has a spectrum of choices that it could make to try and service that requirement

The choices that STC makes regarding the depth of  customer relationship will have direct impacts on the tolling solution and complexity of some of the specific tolling domains.

The 4 broad positions that STC can adopt are:

Link to External Pass Service – reach an agreement with an existing toll operator for that operator to provide a new or existing pass product to meet the requirement

Pay as You Go Toll Collector – develop a simplified offering or capability where road users can search for or be alerted to when they travel and allow them to pay for those trips post travel.

Casual Retailer – develop retailer capabilities and a product offering that meets the requirements for a casual product

Full Retail Offering – develop the capabilities to operate as a Tolling Retailer with full range of product offerings and customer servicing

Each of these positions has implications on the tolling service and solution and level of complexity that will need to be catered for.

External Pass Service

From a tolling solution and technology point of view this is the simplest of the 4 positions. This customer offering would assume an agreement has been struck with a Tolling Operator to offer a casual product on STCs behalf with requisite agreement on roaming fee and requirements. There would be some implications for the Tolling Solution and domains  Customer Interfaces such as the Website and Call Centre 

Web Customer Interface

The STC website would need to link to the customer sign up portal for the Toll Operators Casual product and link to the product definition and agreements. The link to the Toll Operators sign up could be performed within the STC branding frame if that was agreed as part of the casual pass arrangement.

Call Centre Interfaces

 It may be required to offer IVR support and provide call routing capabilities within the STC call centre operation that allows for STC to route calls and webchat enquiries to the Toll Operator who supports the product.

Toll Collector

In this position, STC will offer the ability for road users to search for travel they have made, or potentially setup alerts for travel that they undertake using their license plate number, and once travel has been identified, allow the road user to pay for that trip. This would require the ability for road users to search for travel and pay using a portal as well as customer support for these arrangements via call centre operations.

Travel and Road User Portal

A web portal will need to be developed allowing for searching for travel utilising registration number and potentially other details of the vehicle. Once found the road user would also be able to then select the travel and pay for those trips via the portal. Dependant on privacy assessments full characteristics of the trip may be available to road users including video as additional customer service.

A proactive style of this service could also be enacted by allowing road users to setup SMS or email alerts when their vehicle has been identified as having completed a trip, this would improve collection rates for casual travellers.

Customer Communication

Customer communication channels would need to be assessed as part of this scenario with potentially phone, email/electronic messaging or even potentially paper-based communications as part of this collection service. As there would be a thin layer of customer information, it is unlikely STC will collect customer preferences for communications so may need to select a cost-efficient form of communication based on expected response.

Call Centre and Customer Interface

The Call Centre will need to provide support to road users in finding and selecting their trips for payment, IVR and webchat self service formats would need to be considered for simple enquiries while call centre agents would be available to aid road users in finding and paying for their trips.

Enforcement Process Impacts

Consideration would need to be given in how this service interacts with Enforcement processes and whether road users would still be able to find their trips in the portal if the trip has been included in a Toll Notice. Enabling this service would improve collection rates and increase customer experience but assessment would need to be made regarding the impacts on overall enforcement and collection of this scenario of interaction.

Customer Dispute

Dispute processes would need to be developed that manages the problems with identity that this form product will involve, as the person who creates an arrangement for the vehicle or who rejects the travel may not be provable as having relationship with the vehicle during the dispute process. This may cause complication through the process in managing providing of information and resolving the case.

Casual Retailer

In this scenario, STC offers a casual pass product for travel on NEL (and potentially other roads via interoperability if desired). The exact product details would need to be developed but would require management of a relationship with a customer through a time limited lifecycle. This would require support for customer sign up and potentially a basic customer portal, support for customer queries as well as customer collections and debt processes.

Customer Sign Up and Customer Portal

STC will need to support the collection of essential customer details to sign up for a casual arrangement and develop the capability for customer to self service altering or updating their details. This portal may require management of customer identity, identity verification and passwords, to enable a full featured customer experience. If that isn’t maintained thn these updates may need to be made via call centre servicing and utilising a call based Identify and Verify process.

Customer Lifecycle and Case Management

Casual products require management of a customer relationship through sign up and several stages of relationship, including product activation, active arrangement, trip acceptance and then account closure. At each of these stages there may be multiple points at which customer may need to be contacted or payments will need to be managed. As such there will also be a need for Case Management capability to manage the contacts and responses.

Billing and Invoicing

Due to the different usage and payments points through the lifecycle of a casual product, a billing and invoicing process will need to be developed and maintained that takes payments from provided authorised payment methods (determined by the customer during sign up or a later interactions). 

Full Tolling Retail Offering

This will necessitate a full featured Customer Relationship Management system, Billing and Invoicing and Customer Communication Management capabilities, as well as supporting customer servicing services such as Tag warehousing and supply, a fully featured customer portal and potentially key account servicing options.

Customer Relationship Management

A fully featured CRM system will enable the management of small and large customers through providing a single view of a customer and their details such as contact and contact detail updates, vehicle updates, tag status and management, payment history and payment authorisations, customer communication preferences as well as customer reporting and information support to enable self-servicing. This single view of customer will enable accurate understanding of customer status and a seamless customer experience throughout the different customer interfaces.

Product Management

With a number of products under management, a capability that is tightly aligned to the CRM and Billing systems will need to be developed to manage the features and fees of the products being offered and aligning charges across all the active customers. 

Customer Interfaces

The complexity of customer interfaces in this scenario increases markedly as the various interfaces have a number of products and scenarios of usage to cater for to provide a full featured customer experience. This could include a full Customer Portal with customer identity managed through a customer login, IVR and Webchat integration allowing for self-servicing of customer enquiries and potentially a mobile application to provide customer support on devices.

Customer Communication Management

A full retail offering will require a fully featured approach to customer communications management due to the number of templated messages and variations on various communications for each product as well as catering for customer contact preferences that could outline not only how each customer will be communicated with but also potentially what times of the day. This capability will need to be linked closely with the mailhouse capability and CRM to ensure communications are sent accurately and linked to the customer log in case of further customer contact.

Supporting Technology Systems

This section will outline reliance the Tolling solution has on supporting corporate technology capabilities and the consideration that these solutions must take into account to make sure they are fit for purpose in performing tolling activities	

These systems and capabilities represent underlying technology enabling solutions such as networking, system resiliency and business continuity, compliance solutions required to meet security requirements and corporate systems such as financial systems.

Network and Security

Network Segmentation

A key part in creating an effective operating environment for the Tolling solution will be to ensure that each service and operator only have access to the data and areas of the solution that they require to perform their role, this is a security principle least access.

As part of achieving that goal, the network will need to be “segmented” into separate operational areas of the Tolling solution into logical functional which confines risk to systems and data from malicious actions to limited areas of the solution.

Each of these segments contain only the systems that are needed to perform specific functions, should be access controlled through Network Firewalls that only allow connections from specified user network addresses and external system locations via specific methods of connection (ie. Only allowing users from internal to STC to access Image Viewing via the application designed for that function)

The exact network segments will need to be determined during detailed solution network and security design taking into account the selected tolling solutions and functions, however a sample of the network segments that might be required can be found below:-

STC Internal User Network (by location if multiples) – This network will contain all the internal users within STC, their laptops and any user functional devices such as printers and conference facilities

Corporate Applications (may be further segmented) – This network segment houses all the STC corporate applications such as the financial and HR systems as well as  and STC windows domain services

Roadside Devices and Control – This will house the network connected devices along the NEL roadway including Tolling Devices

Tolling Applications – Should contain the Roadside Interface, Trip Processing and Toll Routing applications and data

Tolling DMZ – Separate network segment to contain the Image Viewing and Exception processing applications, allowing for external connection to these applications without exposing the tolling systems and data themselves

Toll Collection – The Toll Collection systems reside in this  this is separated from the remainder of the tolling systems and internal systems to limit the PCI DSS scope to a minimal footprint of systems that perform payments

Customer Support DMZ – These applications provide interfaces and applications utilised by the call centre to support customer servicing, this segment will also contain services that will be utilised by Customer interfaces for self servicing

Customer Interface DMZ – These are the websites and mobile applications utilised by customers to access their accounts (if necessary) and make payments

These different segments and applications do not all have to be hosted in the same way or location which could also drive some of the separation in the network

Role Based Security

The operators of the Tolling solution as well customer service team should only have access to the systems and functions required to perform their roles. To do this the tolling solution needs to be designed and constructed with the concept of role based security.

Role based security means to design access to the system to suit roles within STC’s operations, for example an Image Viewer will need access to the Tolling solutions Image Viewing and Manual Adjudication functions, but does not need access to the Toll Collection areas of the solution as her role does not require it.

The roles should be designed to meet the security principle of least  to ensure that each defined role only has access to the data and systems it needs to function effectively.

Perimeter Security

Customer interfaces will need to be secured and monitored as these interfaces are the most likely places where malicious attacks will arise.

All customer portals and websites that collect customer details and payments will need to be protected by SSL security certificates. This protects customers data from being intercepted as well as being mandatory to meet banking requirements in PCI DSS and social license expectations of the community.

In addition these interfaces will need to be via perimeter security devices like firewalls and traffic analysers such as Intrusion Protection Systems and denial of service detection and prevention solutions.

These protections will help ensure that these services remain safe and available for STC’s .

PCI DSS Requirements

No matter how STC chooses to accept customer payments, whether the payment is outsourced to a 3rd party payment processor, STC utilises a credit card tokenisation solution that means credit cards are not stored by STC or any other abstraction, the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standards will need to be assessed and designed for in building the Tolling solution.

The PCI DSS standards apply to any device, system or network that has access to or processes payments. These standards can be costly to maintain control of and are audited as part of banking agreements on an annual basis (the level of audit is  on the number of payments being processed).

Controlling the scope of these audits and the systems these standards apply to is one of the most critical controls to be implemented. This can be achieved through strict Network Segmentation, creating a PCI DSS only environment that isolates the systems that process payments from the remainder of the STC environment, including the rest of the tolling solution.

The storage of credit card details also adds a level of complexity to remaining PCI DSS compliant. STC could look at tokenisation solutions that mean that customer payment details are stored by the bank or 3rd party payments provider and only a reference number is received by STC. This limits to exposure over time to PCI DSS compliance and reduces the number of system controls that need to be implemented.

Call Centre and Customer Servicing staff will be part of the PCI DSS scope, and their processes will need to be assessed and audited for compliance, as well as their laptops and telephony systems will need to be isolated from the general STC environment to limit PCI DSS scope to only those operators that have access to or process customer payments. If the call centre operations are outsourced the call centre should be certified for PCI DSS and provide period evidence of  compliance and audits.

Additional Systems

Financial and Invoicing System

The Financial system that supports STC will have be interfaced with the Tolling solution to receive updates on revenue that has been collected or is for collection as a result of passages that have been processed. This will require period updates from the tolling system updating the general ledger with updates at minimum once per day from the Toll Routing updating the Trips sent to each Toll Collector, updates on Rejected Trips for each Toll Collector and Trips that have been sent for Enforcement Collection as well as payments received.

The finance system will also need to support Interoperability by enabling daily interoperability settlement with foreign toll operators through balancing the daily interoperability traffic and issuing an invoice for payment to the Foreign Toll Operator,

Accounts Payable operations and a B2B treasury and payments channel will need to be supported by the finance system, not only for the payment of these Daily settlement payment but also general support of payments to and from STC to other entities as part of general financial operations.

Court Evidence Document Management

As part of process of processing trips and enforcement cases, it may be necessary as part of a dispute to freeze evidence for presentation in a court case. The storage of digital assets as evidence requires not only freezing the transactional information so processing does not continue but also establishing a chain of custody that proves that the information has not been altered and potentially also prevent alteration of the records.

These capabilities can be sourced through specific technology solutions that support these processes and can be integrated with the tolling solutions so that the status of the data is frozen in system alongside the maintenance of the evidence.

Telephony and Omnichannel

Omnichannel engagement systems to support call centre operations are essential to modern customer serviceability. Omnichannel solutions link the traditional call  customer interactions, such as IVR and call handling with digital customer channels such as webchat and even conversational AI in some instances. These systems link all of these channels together and enabling "seamless" servicing of customers across channels.

This Omnichannel system will be intrinsically linked to the Telephony system as it will need to receive information from the IVR about incoming calls, as well as integrate into digital engagement channels.

The Telephony and Omnichannel systems will be tightly linked to the sourcing of the call capability.  If the call  service is outsourced then the sourcing arrangement is likely to include these systems and maintenance as part of the service provision. Integration services to integrate Tolling solution applications (as required) into the call  systems will also need to be considered.

Telephony services for corporate and maintenance offices as well as Business Continuity telephony services for the call  will need to be established separately to call  telephony, and the service should be distinct separate solution but of the same provider.

Batch File Operations and Management

With the number of files needed for operating in an Interoperability environment, a structured approach to Batch File management is necessary. A repository for storing the files will need to be maintained with links to individual files kept with a record of the  or export of the data.

The batch file management capability will need to integrate with a full featured scheduler that enables the orchestration of the interoperability batches, as well as management of the execution run in case of errors as well as alerting and monitoring integration to the operations management and dashboard to enable effective support response.

The Batch File Management capability will also need to manage status of all the files and be able to isolate files with errors until the errors are cleared through manual intervention. At successful conclusion of a file process, the results and number of records processed needs to be recorded against the file by update type to allow for full audit of outcomes.

System Resiliency and Business Continuity

System Resiliency

System Resiliency is the concept that the Tolling solution needs to be resilient to failure or degradation. This can include high availability ideas of making sure the application does not fail if a single server is not available but also can include the idea that the solution is segmented to allow for processing to continue while maintenance activities are being conducted.

The Tolling solution should be designed to enable for disruption to occur to segments of the system without requiring a full halting of operations. For example it should be possible for Trip Processing to undergo maintenance operations while Image Viewing staff continue to manually adjudicate passages. 

To achieve this level of system resiliency will require designing functional flow controls to ensure processes can be isolated within the system, but also segmentation of application and data to allow continued operation of applications while one section of the solution is offline.

Business Continuity

Business Continuity is  operational plan for a company to ensure  critical functions continue to operate during and after an emergency situation. 

A portion of this process is the IT Disaster Recovery capabilities, which allows systems to be available in case of a critical technical or hosting failure such as the loss of a data center. 

The larger part of this process is the planning and capabilities required to ensure that operations can continue in the event of a loss of office or unavailability of staff to travel to work. These plans need to be completed by each function of the business and the systems and capabilities of the systems need to be able to support them.

Keys to enabling a broad range of business continuity situations is to have distinct operating segments within the solution, network controls and segmentation that allow for control of user/office access and geographic dispersal of the technology systems, so a single location failure does not shut down operations.

These capabilities will also need to include customer servicing in scope, so that customers can contact STC and make payments and provide detail updates as necessary. This could mean that services such as Telephony and IVR systems need to be made resilient and able to  from multiple locations.

These capabilities do not need to be automated to be effective as these situations do not happen very often, if at all.

Tolling Domains

The domain performs the function of converting detected road events into billable units (known as Trips). These functions use the data detected by roadside devices and provided to the Trip Processing system as passages allowing for the system to  identity to construct detections of the vehicle into a Trip. The trip is then priced based on the characteristics of the trip

Trip Construction

Description

This process builds trips from identified passage detections and enables the Trip for charging. The trip will be constructed based on the tolling topology for NEL, which is Distance based tolling, with 2 virtual gantries to be used in Trip construction extrapolation. The trips created will be validated to ensure the trips are valid, and any anomalies raised the exception management for manual intervention.

Benefits

This capability is essential to accurately realise revenue and bill road users

Capability Priority

Mandatory

Considerations

This capability needs to be built to be extensible to allow for additional gantries in the future to support roadway extensions and the interfaces need to be designed generically to ensure that different Trip Construction engines can be utilised with the remainder to the Trip Processing capabilities to enable support for other tolling topologies.

Trip Rating

Description

This process assigns the price to the constructed Trips, including the flag fall and adjustments for peak/off-peak and other factors that may be required in the future.

Benefits

This capability is essential to accurately realise revenue and charge road users

Capability Priority

Mandatory

Considerations

Update of pricing of trips should be managed through workflow that is automated, that requires the alteration to be approved systemically by one or more approvers. The prices to be utilised should be controlled by effective date and time enabling for trips that traverse the time period to be managed without interruption.

Tolling Exception Management

Description

This capability allows for tolling operators to manually adjudicate on trips that have raised an as an exception through the validation process in Trip Processing. The sorts of issues that will require manual override from a trained operator will be Duplicate trips (where the vehicle has potentially been detected twice travelling through a toll point) or an Illogical Trip (where the vehicle has potentially been detected in rapid succession in two or more toll points in a sequence that doesn’t make sense). Exact exceptions and details of manual resolution expected will need to be developed and documented during detailed analysis.

Benefits

This capability will enable more accurate toll collection through the elimination of erroneous detections

Capability Priority

Mandatory

Considerations

The manual override screen may be similar enough to Manual Identification to enable reuse of the screen/application capability reducing the number of components being maintained and training users to utilise.

Court Evidence Process

Description

This capability “freezes” the status of Tolls and associated system evidence when a road user challenges a Toll Notice or Customer relationship legally and the systemic evidence needs to be maintained in a “locked” state till the matter is resolved.

Benefits

The maintenance of evidence supports STC’s efforts in responding to these incidents.

Capability Priority

Mandatory

Considerations

This capability can be maintained off system – such as in a document/data store style solution with managed time stamping and versioning to function of proof against tampering. The Tolling and Toll Collection data could then be set to a status that ensures the tolls are not progressed through a lifecycle.

The Operational and Systems Management domain includes the capabilities that enable STC to monitor and manage the tolling solution. The key to these capabilities is making sure that not just reactive but proactive management is enabled. Proactive maintenance and management will ensure that failure rates and service failure rates are greatly reduced and the outcomes of tolling processes are improved.

Operational Management

Description

This capability enables the collection of operational data for a variety of tolling systems and devices, the data will be aggregated and predictive and reactive monitoring and alerting for the tolling solution. The operations management capability will also enable the presenting of operational data in dashboards and operational data in reporting through Reporting and Insights systems.

Benefits

This capability improves the tolling outcomes achieved by the tolling solution and process by ensuring the tolling solution is available and performing as expected, reducing adverse outcomes by reducing errors and issues with the system

Capability Priority

Mandatory

Considerations

The operational management platform needs to be able to ingest all types of operational data streams, including device data to ensure full value of correlation of events is achieved. End to end data coverage will enable full trend analysis of tolling outcomes and predict events that require maintenance, avoiding issues across the platform.

Integration Management

Description

This capability represents a central integration function that enables the operational team to view integration logs and events, resolve integration issues and manage integration flow controls to aid diagnosis or to perform maintenance on a segment of the solution.

Benefits

Strong integration management capabilities will ensure that end to end process flows do not produce adverse outcomes when maintenance is in progress and allows operations engineers to resolve underlying issues with integrated processes and systems,

Capability Priority

Considerations

The exact style and choice of integration management and interface will be tightly related to the amount and style of integration to be found across the solution.

Operational Dashboard and Status Management

Description

This capability is the ability to display operational information in an easily accessible format that enables operational users and management to visually see the status and performance of the tolling solution and outcomes of the tolling process and enable them to take action as necessary.

Benefits

A good operational dashboarding capability summarises operational data into easily digested format allowing prompt and knowing action to be taken.

Capability Priority

Considerations

The visualisation of the dashboard should be in an easily maintained and updated format to ensure that the dashboard can be maintained and changed to meet specific needs of operational staff

Operational System Interface/Road Status Management

Description

This capability is the interface with the Operations Management and Control systems to allow for road data, particularly lane/road open and closed information to be ingested by the operations management system.

Benefits

This interface will allow the operations management systems and operations team to view and manage operational events and correlate them with system events.

Highly Desirable

Price Updates and Management

Pricing management is an important part of managing the tolling system and this capability allows for the tolling operations team to initiate and manage the process of altering pricing for trips and fees, including the ability for supervisors and managers to review the prices and approve the application of the changes.

Benefits

An automated managed process ensures that tolling prices are accurate and improves customer experience.

Mandatory

Considerations

This capability needs to interface with the Trip Rating process and should enable and disable prices with an effective date and time to ensure that the changes can be made effective with a minimum of disruption to tolling processes.

Virtual Asset and Test Harness

Description

The capability of Virtual Assets and Test Harness will enable the tolling system to be tested before the roadway is ready through a digital replica of the road, including traffic patterns and expected rates of trips of different classes, video and tag trips as well as the ability to generate exception scenarios.

Benefits

This capability enables early and comprehensive testing of the tolling solution end to end utilising a realistic level of traffic and scenarios that will occur on road, improving the solutions readiness for go live and improving the quality of testing post go live.

Capability Priority

Desirable

Considerations

This capability needs to be maintained alongside the rest of the solution to ensure it stays up to date and usable to replicate production road interactions.

Test and Operational Automation

Description

The test and operational automation enable effective management of the tolling solution in production as well as improve the quality of enhancements and changes prior to implementation. Automated operational processes includes automated recovery of services and integration processes as well as automated deployment and release scripting to limit the amount and impacts of manual errors. Automated testing should be executed continuously against the tolling system as changes are developed to limit the number of regression errors are introduced as new capabilities are added to the solution.

Benefits

Test and Operational automation enables scalability of operations while ensuring that the quality of the outcomes are high by limiting manual errors and ensuring corrective actions are taken quickly.

Capability Priority

Desirable

Considerations

Automation needs to be invested in continually as enhancements and changes are made to ensure that they are kept current with the system. This ensures that the automated testing and operational procedures are still effective and achieving outcomes desired.
