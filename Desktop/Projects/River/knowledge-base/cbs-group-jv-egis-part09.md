3.   BACKGROUND
     As a priority transport infrastructure investment by the State of Victoria (the State), North East Link (NEL)
     will provide a safe and efficient freeway connection by improving traffic flow, reducing travel times,
     removing non local traffic from local roads and increasing road use reliability for up to 135,000 vehicles a
     day. NEL will take 15,000 trucks off busy residential roads in the north east of greater Melbourne each
     day and reduce traffic on the arterial road network crossing the Yarra River. NEL will enhance access to
     major suburban business and employment centres, improve orbital road connectivity acro ss Melbourne
     and boost the capacity of the city’s freight network.
     North East Link will include the North East Link tollway (NEL Tollway), with the North East Link State Tolling
     Corporation (STC) retaining the entitlement to collect toll revenue.
     STC is a public entity body corporate established under the North East Link Act 2020 (Vic) (NEL Act). Its
     purpose is to:
         •    operate, maintain and otherwise manage the North East Link road, including managing and
              controlling the use of and access to the North East Link road;
         •    procure, deliver and operate the NEL tolling system;
         •    fix and collect tolls for use of the NEL Tollway; and
         •    optimise the value of, or return from, STC’s infrastructure and other assets.
     In October 2021, STC and the State executed the NEL Tolling Agreement which sets out the terms on which
     STC may fix and collect tolls and fees for the use of the NEL Tollway (or part of it) for the passage of
     vehicles. STC is continuing to establish its Toll Collection Capability (TCC) for the NEL Tollway, which forms
     part of the North East Link Program.
     STC has proposed to develop an end to end solution for the TCC that incorporates:
         •    the delivery and operation of a roadside system (RSS);
         •    the delivery, support, and maintenance of a back office system (BOS); and
         •    outcomes of the TCC Information Technology (TCC IT) Project, including the provision of
              supporting communications networks, IT infrastructure and related services.
     STC is procuring these elements of the TCC through separate, staggered approaches to market. To support
     STC in the delivery of these packages, STC will engage an independent verifier.


4.   PURPOSE
     The purpose of the TCC Independent Verifier (IV) Services is to provide STC with an independent expert
     opinion that the specified technical and business requirements have been met as per the TCC Contracts.
     The TCC Contracts include the Scope of Works and Technical Requirements.
     The TCC IV Services will include design and document reviews, witnessing of tests and demonstrations to
     ensure that the requirements verification traceability matrix is complete, accurate, and records sufficient
     evidence to verify that the requirements within the TCC Contracts have been met.
     The role will not have a commercial or financial element i.e. there is no payment certification role and
     there is no need to issue formal certificates to verify payment milestones.

5.   REQUIREMENTS AND DELIVERABLES
     The TCC IV will be expected to use its expert technical, assurance and contractual expertise to assist
     STC in monitoring and reporting on the level of compliance that the TCC Contractors (the RSS and BOS
     contractors) demonstrate in accordance with the TCC Contracts and the documented management plans.

     The TCC IV will provide expert advice and report on a monthly basis to the tolling director and will be
     expected to brief other nominated stakeholders on the review services as may be required.

     The TCC IV Services (the Services) are to be provided through the delivery stages of the TCC Project –
     including the implementation and operational readiness phases due to complete by December 2028 and
     then up to the first 3 months of operations (subject to the extent of defects identified during the initial
     operations period).
     The TCC IV will be responsible for delivering the following Services:

     (a) Development and submission of an Independent Verifier Plan (within 20 business days of
         commencement) detailing the methodology, process and approach of performing the Services
         including the skills, expertise and experience required of the resources and the estimated resource
         levels required for each component of the Services. This Independent Verifier Plan is to be agreed
         with STC.
     (b) Familiarisation with documentation that forms the relevant TCC Contracts (including TCC Contractor
         management plans) and the North East Link Act 2020.
     (c) Attendance at meetings with both STC and the TCC Contractors where clarifications and
         understanding of the requirements will be further explored.
     (d) Reviewing and evaluating (by general overview and reasonable checking (GORC)) or as directed by
         the tolling director, the TCC Contractor’s performance against the obligations imposed in the relevant
         TCC Contract.
     (e) Expressing an informed opinion on the TTC Contract deliverables including the achievement of formal
         entry and exit criteria detailed in the TCC Contracts for each deliverable or project milestone/phase,
         and whether that deliverable is suitable for endorsement or acceptance under the terms of the TCC
         Contracts.
     (f) Reviewing evidence submitted by the TCC Contractor in order to provide an opinion on whether the
         system complies with the TCC project SWTRs for RSS and BOS including witnessing of tests and
         demonstrations provided as evidence to support the achievement of the requirements contained in
         the TCC Contracts.
     (g) Recommend a program of TCC Contractor verification audits relevant to the performance of the TCC
         Contractor’s obligations under the TCC Contracts and where requested by STC conduct or assist
         STC in the conduct of the audits and report to STC on its findings and any recommendations arising
         from the audit.
     (h) Providing progress reports to the Project Control Group (PCG) in accordance with an agreed
         timetable monthly based on:
              i. A statement of the IV activities performed during the period;
              ii. A forecast of the IV activities to be conducted within the next period;
              iii. regular observation of the status of the TCC Contractor’s progress against the schedule;
              iv. witnessing formal test sessions and internal audits;
              v. reviewing progress reports;
              vi. conducting meetings with key personnel within the TCC Project Team; and
              vii. Identifying any major risks or concerns that need to be raised to the PCG in relation to the IV
                   observations.
     (i) The option for STC to elect an independent review be conducted by the Independent Verifier, of the
         readiness of the systems, processes and people required to enable STC to commence the tolling
         operations for North East Link. This review will include:
              i. Ensuring the contract documents are in place and up to date;
              ii. System testing is completed successfully and there are no impediments to operations
                   commencing;
              iii. Users are ready to operate the systems and the business is ready to implement the changes;
              iv. STC supporting systems are in place and ready to go; and
              v. Support and maintenance arrangements are in place and have been validated.

    Overview
    The Fair Jobs Code (FJC) aims to improve employment outcomes for persons employed by suppliers and
    service providers to the Victorian Government. It is implemented by Victorian Government departments,
    agencies and public bodies, such as STC, to support and promote fair labour standards and ensure
    compliance with employment law. It applies to the purchase of goods and/or services regardless of the
    method of procurement.

    A recent policy update means that from 1 September 2024 the FJC applies to threshold procurement
    contracts with a value of $1 million or more exclusive of GST. Should the Supplier’s total fee exceed this
    threshold value, the Supplier must complete Section 3, Item 5 to demonstrate compliance to the FJC.


    To comply with the FJC, the Supplier must:

         1.    hold a valid Fair Jobs Code Pre-Assessment Certificate, available at buyingfor.vic.gov.au/fair-
               jobs-code-departments-and-agencies. A copy of the Supplier’s Pre-Assessment Certificate
               number must be included in a response to this RFP.
               Suppliers that do not hold a valid Pre-Assessment Certificate may not be considered for this
               procurement. If a Supplier is unable to secure a valid Pre-Assessment Certificate, it must
               notify STC as soon as possible and prior to submitting its bid.
               STC may use its discretion to enter into a contract with a Supplier that does not have a valid
               Pre-Assessment Certificate subject to sections 4.3 or 4.4 of the FJC.
         2.    if successful, maintain a valid Pre-Assessment Certificate throughout any agreement entered
               into between the Supplier and STC. That agreement will also include the FJC Model Clauses.
         3.    if the Supplier’s Pre-Assessment Certificate is revoked or lapses after a response is submitted,
               but before contracts are awarded, notify STC as soon as practicable but not later than ten (10)
               days after the revocation or expiry date.
