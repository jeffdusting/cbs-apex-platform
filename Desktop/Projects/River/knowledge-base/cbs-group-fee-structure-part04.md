### 5.7 Infrastructure Transformation Case Study

**The Challenge: Whole-of-State Infrastructure Optimisation**

A state government managing $45 billion in infrastructure assets faced fragmented governance, duplicated capabilities, and missed synergies across transport, water, and energy sectors. Traditional sector-based approaches were creating inefficiencies worth $2 billion annually. The government sought an integrated transformation framework that could work across all infrastructure types.

**CBS Group's Integrated Framework**

CBS Group deployed our Infrastructure Transformation Framework, treating all state infrastructure as an interconnected system. We applied systems thinking to identify cross-sector synergies, such as transport corridors doubling as renewable energy generators and water infrastructure providing grid storage. Our methodology integrated commercial, technical, and collaborative elements across all sectors simultaneously.

**The Transformation Process**

Year 1 focused on assessment and strategy, mapping all infrastructure interdependencies and identifying quick wins worth $300M. Year 2 built foundations through unified governance structures and capability development programs reaching 500 leaders. Year 3 implemented integrated solutions including shared control centres, combined maintenance contracts, and cross-sector data platforms. Year 4 optimised and scaled successful initiatives statewide.

**Portfolio-Wide Impact**

- Efficiency gains: $2.3B annual savings through integration
- Service improvement: 30% reduction in infrastructure failures
- Sustainability: 45% reduction in carbon emissions
- Innovation: 15 cross-sector initiatives generating new value
- Capability: Unified competency framework for 5,000 staff
- Resilience: Integrated response to climate events

This transformation demonstrated that treating infrastructure as an integrated portfolio, rather than isolated assets, unlocks exponential value through synergies, shared capabilities, and systems-level optimisation.
