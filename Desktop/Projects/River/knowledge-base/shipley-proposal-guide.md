# Shipley Proposal Guide — CBS Application Reference

## Overview

This guide synthesises the 5 domains of the Shipley proposal methodology for use by CBS Group tender agents. Each domain contains practical rules and techniques for producing winning tender responses.

## Domain 1: Document Design (19 Techniques)

### Executive Summary (Most Important)

Under Shipley, the executive summary is the most important section. Rules:
- Write it LAST, after all other sections are complete
- Distil the best content from the full document
- Structure as a standalone Four Box document
- Address the evaluator who may only read this section
- Thread all win themes into a coherent narrative

### Customer Focus

Every sentence should be framed from the client's perspective:
- Use "you" and the client's name more than "we" and "CBS"
- Reference the client's specific challenges, not generic industry problems
- Use the client's own terminology and document formatting where possible
- Intelligence about the client's actual vision should drive the response structure

### Active Voice

Use active voice for persuasive writing:
- Wrong: "The asset management framework was developed by CBS Group"
- Right: "CBS Group developed the asset management framework"
- Active voice assigns responsibility, creates urgency, and reads more confidently

### Graphics

Graphics convey information and elicit persuasive impact:
- Every major section should include at least one graphic
- Graphics should have action captions that state the benefit, not just describe the image
- Types: process flows for methodology, comparison tables for competitive positioning, timelines for project approach, org charts for team structure
- Caption format: "[Benefit statement]. [Figure description]."

### Headings

Use informative headings that tell the evaluator what to expect:
- Wrong: "Section 3: Methodology"
- Right: "CBS's CAPITAL Framework Delivers Measurable Lifecycle Savings"
- Headings should function as a summary of the document when read in sequence

### Choosing Correct Words

- Avoid cliches: "best practice", "world-class", "cutting-edge", "holistic"
- Use specific, measurable language: "$180M savings" not "significant savings"
- Use the client's terminology, not CBS internal jargon
- Risk management: avoid absolute guarantees, use "demonstrated", "validated", "proven"

## Domain 2: Proposal Management (Key Techniques)

### Theme Statements

Differentiation through consistent win themes threaded throughout. See the Win Themes document for the full development process.

Key rule: brainstorm win themes BEFORE writing begins, then apply consistently across all sections.

### Features, Advantages, and Benefits

Always lead with benefits, not features:
- **Feature:** What it IS (CAPITAL has 7 core capabilities)
- **Advantage:** What it DOES better than alternatives (integrates commercial and technical)
- **Benefit:** What it MEANS for the client (delivers minimum 3-5× return on advisory fees)

Emphasise benefits throughout. Quantify benefits wherever possible.

### Discriminators

Features of your solution that differ from competitors and are important to the customer:
- Identify CBS discriminators for each tender (CAPITAL, named personnel, client relationships)
- Position to downplay areas where competitors have discriminators CBS lacks
- Requires competitive intelligence and document review (see competitor-analysis skill)

### Compliance and Responsiveness

- Create a compliance matrix before writing begins
- Map every mandatory criterion to the response section that addresses it
- Compliance is binary — you either meet it or you don't
- Responsiveness goes beyond compliance — demonstrate you understand WHY the criterion exists

### Storyboards and Mock-ups

Before writing, create a storyboard for each section:
- What is the key message? (Win theme)
- What evidence supports it? (KB sources)
- What graphic will illustrate it?
- How does it link to the evaluation criteria?

Agents should produce section outlines (Bronze phase) before full drafts (Silver phase).

### Resumes/Personnel

Resumes are a key discriminator:
- Format: relevant experience first, generic qualifications second
- Each resume should be tailored to the specific role in this tender
- Reference specific project outcomes, not just project names
- Query KB for personnel CVs: `category: ip`, search by name

## Domain 3: Pricing

### Presenting Cost and Price Data

- Use graphics for pricing data — tables alone don't engage senior evaluators
- Quantify value alongside price: "investment of $X delivers $Y in savings"
- Common mistakes: hiding pricing, over-complicating fee structures, not linking price to value
- CBS approach: always present a conforming offer AND a value-based alternative

### Risk Management in Pricing

- Reference risk in all pricing sections — show you understand the client's risk profile
- CBS CAPITAL framework: risk is allocated to the party best placed to manage it
- Avoid "risk transfer" language — use "risk allocation" (collaborative, not adversarial)

## Domain 4: Process Design

### Repeatability

- Document what works and what doesn't after each tender (post-submission debrief)
- Use consistent processes tailored to specific clients and bid scale
- Document reviews serve as the structure for all bid quality reviews
- CBS mapping: Bronze (Pink Team), Silver (Red Team), Gold (Gold Team)

## Domain 5: Sales and Capture

### Proposal Strategy

- Plan to write a persuasive proposal, not just a compliant one
- Capture strategy: understand the client's evaluation process and what drives their decision
- Ghosting: position your strengths to implicitly highlight competitor weaknesses without naming them
- Value proposition: the single most compelling reason the client should choose CBS

### Relevant Experience and Past Performance

- Persuasive narrative of past success, not a list of projects
- Admission of weaknesses (where relevant) builds credibility
- Case studies should follow: challenge → approach → outcome (quantified)
- Query KB: `category: tender` for past CBS submissions to similar clients

### Question Response Proposals

Many tenders use a question-and-answer format:
- Allocate the strongest writer to the highest-weighted questions
- Use graphics even in Q&A responses
- Each answer should include a section summary that directly answers the question
- Critical question identification: which questions carry the most evaluation weight?
