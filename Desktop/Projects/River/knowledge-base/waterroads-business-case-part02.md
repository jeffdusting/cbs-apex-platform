0) What exists today (anchors for assumptions)

Barangaroo ferry hub is in place and already serves F3 (Parramatta River) and F4 (Pyrmont Bay) services, integrating directly into the CBD’s western edge. The wharf opened in June 2017 and is designed as a major public transport gateway. Transport for NSWBarangarooWikipedia

Wynyard Walk gives a ~6-minute level, weather-protected walk from Wynyard Station to Barangaroo with capacity for ~20,000 people per hour, which is crucial when comparing water vs. rail access times to Barangaroo. NSW Government

F3 Parramatta River already provides a Rhodes-catchment option via nearby wharves (e.g., Meadowbank / Sydney Olympic Park). Typical Meadowbank ↔ Barangaroo ferry time is ~40–41 min, with ~20-minute headways in many periods. Rome2Rio+1Transit

Rhodes Wharf: the NSW Government has a live proposal to deliver a new ferry wharf and interchange at Rhodes linked to the Rhodes Place Strategy; location and timing are being progressed with the City of Canada Bay and DPHI. This provides the physical anchor for a direct Rhodes–Barangaroo water metro. Transport for NSWPlanning NSW

Other relevant constraints: low-tide disruptions affect only the upper river (Parramatta–Rydalmere) and not Rhodes–Barangaroo, which improves reliability versus a full Parramatta–CBD run. Parramatta Female Factory Friends

1) Proposed operating concept (for modelling)

Headway & span: every 6 minutes for 16 hours/day (6am–10pm) ⇒ 160 one-way trips per direction/day (320 total/day). (960 min ÷ 6 = 160).

Stop pattern: high-frequency Rhodes ⇄ Barangaroo spine (express or very limited stops) to deliver a step-change in end-to-end time vs. today’s all-stops F3.

Indicative in-vehicle time: ~35 minutes one way (express or 1–2 intermediate stops). This is 5–10 minutes quicker than today’s Meadowbank–Barangaroo (~40–41 min) and materially quicker for many Rhodes residents because it removes the surface access to Meadowbank/SOP and avoids train interchange plus the Wynyard Walk. Rome2Rio

Fleet need (order-of-magnitude): Round-trip cycle ≈ 2 × 35 min + turnarounds (≈ 6 min total) ≈ 76 min. To hold a 6-minute headway requires ~76/6 ≈ 13 vessels in line service (12–15 depending on dwell, speed controls—e.g., 15-knot transit zone near the Harbour Bridge—and actual one-way time). NSW Government+1

Vessel capacity reference: Sydney’s Emerald-class carries ≈400 pax; the new Parramatta River Class carries ≈200 pax and is designed for future electrification; TfNSW publishes official fleet capacities. A water-metro could target the 200–400 pax range with dual-side, fast turnarounds. Wikipedia+1Transport for NSW Open Data

Why Barangaroo works as a CBD end-point: Barangaroo is already a major employment node and interchanges directly to rail/Metro via Wynyard Walk (≈6 minutes), so a ferry that lands in-precinct competes well on total door-to-door time

Barge / Pontoon size (per node)

To berth two vessels concurrently and keep them on charge with safe working space:

Pontoon length: ~30.0 m = 12 m (Berth 1) + 3.0 m separation + 12 m (Berth 2) + 2×1.5 m end clearances.

Pontoon width: ~4.0 m clear deck (≥1.5 m per AS 3962; 4.0 m gives a comfortable service corridor and space for posts, bollards and cable trays).

Freeboard: typical 0.5–0.65 m is available on heavy-duty pontoons; choose to suit site. Suppliers list standard modules in 3.0–4.0 m widths and 12–20 m lengths that can be joined to reach 30 m. TMM Marinasscafffloat.co.uk

Why put cabinets & transformer onshore (not on the barge)?
Tesla specifies up to 100 m cabinet-to-post reach; keeping the heavy, powered equipment (kiosk transformer + V3 cabinets) ashore reduces marine structural demand and simplifies maintenance. V4 posts themselves have 1 m flood tolerance, making them well-suited to pontoon mounting. Caltrans Brightidea

Implementation notes

Why 3 posts (Config A) vs 4 (Config B): With your 6-minute headway and two-at-a-time charging, three posts per node gives operational slack and a spare bay without crowding the berthing face—and keeps average site power well within the 2× V3 cabinet envelope. (V3 cabinet specs and V4 post 250 kW rating are in the Tesla datasheet.) Caltrans Brightidea

Cabinet → post cable length: The 100 m max distance is observed in the plan; keeping cabinets onshore also avoids placing rectifiers over water. Caltrans Brightidea

Pontoon modules: Commercial suppliers list heavy-duty pontoons in 4.0 m width and 12–20 m lengths; a 30 m barge can be assembled from 2×15 m or 3×10–12 m modules with standard gangways / piles. Freeboard options around 0.5–0.65 m are common. TMM Marinas

Standards to observe: AS 3962 (marina design), AS/NZS 3000 & 3004 (electrical), Ausgrid NS117 for kiosks, and Tesla installation instructions for cabinets/posts (IP ratings, clearances, earthing). hamiltonislandpropertyAusgrid
