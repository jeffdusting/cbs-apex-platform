# Shipley Tender Lifecycle Framework — CBS Group Application

## Overview

The Shipley business development lifecycle provides a structured approach to opportunity identification, qualification, capture, and proposal development. CBS Group applies this framework through its Paperclip agent system, integrating the CAPITAL framework methodology at the Propose stage.

## Phase 0: Market Engagement

Continuous monitoring of tender opportunities across five jurisdictions:
- Australian Federal Government (AusTender)
- NSW Government (Tenders.NSW) — primary: TfNSW, Sydney Metro, motorway programme
- Victorian Government (Buying for Victoria) — VicRoads, MRPV, Metro Tunnel
- New Zealand Government (GETS) — Waka Kotahi/NZTA, KiwiRail
- Inland Rail (ARTC)

CBS Group's target sectors: infrastructure asset management, systems engineering, transport advisory, tunnelling, tolling, lifecycle management, PPP advisory.

## Phase 1: Opportunity Qualification (Bid/No-Bid)

### Shipley Bid/No-Bid Criteria Mapped to CBS Scorecard

| Shipley Criterion | CBS Scorecard Dimension | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Can we do the work? | Sector Alignment | 25% |
| Can we offer a unique solution? | CAPITAL Applicability | 20% |
| Can we win? | Competitive Position | 10% |
| Is it worth winning? | Contract Value + Strategic Value | 20% |
| Do we have the right relationships? | Client Relationship History | 15% |
| Can we resource it? | Resource Availability | 10% |

### Qualification Decision Thresholds

| Weighted Score | Decision | Shipley Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 4.0–5.0 | Go — pursue actively | Bid |
| 3.0–3.9 | Watch — monitor, reassess on new information | Conditional Bid |
| 1.0–2.9 | Pass — do not pursue | No Bid |

### Must-Have Criteria (automatic Pass if any fail)

1. Tender close date allows minimum 5 working days for response preparation
2. CBS Group meets the mandatory participation criteria (or can consortium to meet them)
3. No conflict of interest with existing client engagements
4. Estimated value exceeds $100,000 (below this, cost of pursuit outweighs return)

## Phase 2: Capture Planning

After a Go decision, capture planning focuses on:

### Client Intelligence

- Who is the decision maker? What do they value?
- What is the evaluation methodology? (weighted criteria, best value, lowest price)
- Is there an incumbent? What is their performance record?
- Have we worked with this client before? What was our performance?

### Solution Development

- How does CAPITAL framework apply to this opportunity?
- Which CBS personnel have relevant experience?
- What past projects demonstrate our capability?
- Can we quantify the value we will deliver (e.g. "$X savings over Y years")?

### Win Themes

3-5 key differentiators that will be threaded through every section of the response:

1. Each win theme must be specific to this opportunity (not generic)
2. Each must be evidence-based (traceable to KB content)
3. Each must counter a competitor's position
4. Each must be measurable where possible

## Phase 3: Propose — Bronze/Silver/Gold

### Bronze (Pink Team Equivalent)

- Response structure mapped to evaluation criteria
- Section assignments to specialist agents
- Compliance gap analysis — every mandatory criterion mapped
- Win themes identified and allocated to sections
- Pricing approach established (conforming + alternative)

**Gate:** CBS Executive reviews response plan. Approves structure and themes.

### Silver (Red Team Equivalent)

- Full draft of all sections with KB evidence
- Complete compliance responses
- Draft fee schedule
- Win themes threaded through narrative
- Competitor differentiation embedded

**Gate:** CBS Executive reviews complete draft. Provides specific section feedback.

### Gold (Gold Team / Final Review)

- All feedback incorporated
- Consistency review across sections
- Executive summary polished and aligned to win themes
- Formatting and presentation finalised
- Delivered to SharePoint for human review and submission

**Gate:** Jeff Dusting reviews and submits to tender portal.

## Phase 4: Post-Submission

- Track outcome in tender register
- If won: capture lessons learned, update client relationship profile
- If lost: request debrief from client, update competitor intelligence
- Update win/loss statistics for future qualification scoring

## Proposal Structure (Standard for CBS Tenders)

| Section | Content | Shipley Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Cover letter | Personalised, references client priorities, states commitment | Lead with client benefit, not CBS capability |
| Executive summary | Win themes, value proposition, key differentiators | Write last, distil the best content from below |
| Understanding of requirements | Demonstrate comprehension of scope and client needs | Mirror client language, show you've read the brief |
| Technical methodology | CAPITAL framework application, approach, tools, standards | Specific to this project, not generic methodology |
| Experience and past performance | Relevant case studies with quantified outcomes | Choose 3-5 most relevant, quantify everything |
| Key personnel | Named team with role-specific experience | Match personnel to evaluation criteria requirements |
| Management approach | Governance, reporting, risk management, quality assurance | Show how you'll manage the engagement day-to-day |
| Commercial offer | Conforming fee schedule + value-based alternative | Demonstrate value, not just price |
| Compliance | Mandatory criteria responses, conditions of participation | Complete, no gaps, evidence for every criterion |
