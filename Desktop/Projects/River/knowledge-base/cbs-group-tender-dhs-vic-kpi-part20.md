Introduction

The Victorian Department of Health and Department of Families, Fairness and Housing, from here on referred to as the departments, engage third parties for the delivery of ICT services as well as other information / data-related services.  

The departments maintain confidential and sensitive information and are accountable for the safekeeping of all public sector information that is generated/created on the department’s behalf by third parties. As such it is imperative that the security of departmental records and information is maintained to preserve the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of that information when it is accessed, modified, or stored by third parties.

What is the ISSRA?

The Information Security Standards Requirements Assessment (ISSRA) is a tool that enables the department to evaluate third parties against the security controls outlined within the department’s Standard 8b - Third-Party Information Security Standard for Externals (the Standard).   

It provides the departments with preliminary evidence of how a third party can meet the requirements within the Standard and preserve the confidentiality, integrity, and accessibility of departmental information. All third parties that provide ICT services and other data/information services (including accessing, modifying, generating, recording, or storing public sector information) on behalf of the department must complete the ISSRA before they can be considered for selection or awarded a contract with the department.  

The department's staff member responsible for initiating the procurement will send a copy of the Third-Party Standard 8b for Externals and the Information Security Standards Security Review Assessment (this document) to all prospective third parties.

A third party that plans to offer ICT services or other data/information services to the departments is the intended recipient of the ISSRA Questionnaire.
