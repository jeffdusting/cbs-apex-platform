# Shipley Four Box Model

## Purpose

A document structuring framework that organises any written deliverable (tender sections, board papers, executive summaries, client correspondence) into four sequential boxes for maximum persuasive impact.

## The Four Boxes

### Box 1: SUMMARY

Signal your purpose in a clear heading. Summarise the essence of your message. State what you want the reader to do, know, or feel.

**Rules:**
- Lead with the conclusion, not the background
- One paragraph maximum for short documents; one page for long documents
- The reader should understand your key message from Box 1 alone
- Include the specific action you want (approve, fund, proceed, shortlist)

### Box 2: INTRODUCTION

Preview the organisation of the document. Organise around the points most likely to influence the reader.

**Rules:**
- List the subordinate topics that will be covered in Box 3
- Order topics by importance to the reader (not by your internal logic)
- Each topic should link to a client hot button or evaluation criterion
- Maximum 5 subordinate topics for readability

### Box 3: BODY

Provide convincing details for the reader. Maintain the same organisational scheme introduced in Box 2. Consider visuals to help convey your message.

**Rules:**
- Each subordinate topic from Box 2 becomes a section heading
- Within each section: evidence → analysis → benefit to client
- Use graphics to convey information and elicit persuasive impact
- Maintain the customer's perspective throughout
- Every claim must be supported by evidence

### Box 4: REVIEW

Summarise the reasons the reader should do, know, or feel what you want. End by clearly stating the next realistically achievable step.

**Rules:**
- Restate the key benefits (not features)
- Reference the strongest evidence from Box 3
- End with a specific, realistic next step
- Do not introduce new information in Box 4

## Application to CBS Agent Outputs

### Tender Response Sections

Every tender section produced by Technical Writing, Compliance, or Pricing agents should follow Four Box structure:
1. **Summary:** What CBS offers for this criterion and why it matters to the client
2. **Introduction:** Preview of the evidence and approach areas covered
3. **Body:** Detailed methodology, case studies, personnel, with CAPITAL framework positioning
4. **Review:** Why CBS is the best choice for this criterion, next step

### Board Papers

Governance agent board papers already follow a structured template, but each section within the board paper should internally follow Four Box:
1. **Summary:** Key finding or recommendation for this section
2. **Introduction:** Topics covered
3. **Body:** Data, analysis, evidence
4. **Review:** Recommended action for the directors

### Executive Summaries

Under Shipley, the executive summary is the most important series of pages within a bid document. It must:
- Be written LAST (after all other sections are complete)
- Distil the best content from the full document
- Thread all win themes into a coherent narrative
- Be structured as a standalone Four Box document
- Address the evaluator who only reads the executive summary
