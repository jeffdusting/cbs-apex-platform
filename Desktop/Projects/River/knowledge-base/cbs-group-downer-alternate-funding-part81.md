Dear Dave,

Re: Alternate Funding Mechanisms | Sydney Fleet HVAC Initiative | Next Steps

Context

As affirmed last week in our presentation to you, the NSW ESS Scheme, using the Analysis & Verification methodology, is most appropriate energy-based funding mechanism for the Sydney Fleet HVAC Initiative.

ESS may provide up to $7.5m in funding in advance of installation. This funding is an incentive to reduce energy consumption and is independent of project cost. 

This proposal outlines how CBS Group can support the follow-on actions required to apply for these funds.

Requirements

Broadly, the actions required to become eligible for ESS funds are:

Define and document the project and scope of HVAC modifications to optimize the application for ESS funds

Provision of an approved auditor to establish a suitable measurement and verification methodology for this project

Ensure the ESS project definition aligns with the requirements of the project implementation 

Prepare and deliver briefing materials for TfNSW on the proposed application

Upon Project establishment a further requirement is to

Manage the application and fulfilment process including the provision of mandated measurement and verification reporting. *

*This activity is not included in this proposal.

Approach

CBS Group propose with your team to project manage the ESS stream of work on your behalf. We anticipate the work effort to be approx. 2.5 days/per week for three months. This approach minimises your up-front commitment and allows an end of month review and check point that provides a go/no-go check point and a review of works outstanding.

The principal tasks are to: 

Define and document the ‘tranches’ and scope of modifications (HVAC and others TBC) to optimize the application for ESS funds. 

Ensure alignment with the requirements of the project implementation. 

Concurrently, identify and partner with an approved auditor to establish a suitable methodology that aligns with Program and Project requirements.

Prepare and deliver briefing materials for TfNSW on the proposed application.

Other tasks that may be required to ensure certification for ESS.

Nominated Persons and Terms of Engagement

CBS proposes a blended team, led by Jeff Dusting to complete this work. Jeff has prior experience with Fleet Enhancement Program and team, the applicable schemes, and considerable commercial acumen. A daily rate of $2000 plus GST is billable on a pro-rata basis using an 8-hour day. Our estimate is 23 - 35 Days work effort. Estimated cost range for CBS Services is $66,000 - $92,400.

The Independent Auditor will be engaged separately at cost, under terms and conditions approved by Downer prior to commencement.

The current CBS Group Services Agreement terms of engagement, including current 30 day payment terms will continue to apply to this proposal.

Any travel to/from Sydney is billed at cost, with a daily per diem in accordance with the applicable ATO schedule per diem payable.

We look forward to the opportunity to progress this exciting initiative and would welcome an opportunity to clarify anything outlined in this proposal.

## Downer Proposal_July 2020.docx

*File: `Downer Alt Funding Proposals/Downer Proposal_July 2020.docx`*

Dear

Following further discussions and correspondence with Chris Bowen, we submit the following detailed proposal to assist Downer in accessing @7.5m in benefits realised through the NSW Energy Savings Scheme. We have shaped this proposal to provide Downer with a whole-of-lifecycle service that ensures successful participation in the ESS.

Proposal Context

The NSW Energy Savings Scheme

The NSW Energy Savings Scheme (ESS) seeks to reduce energy consumption in NSW by creating financial incentives for organisations to invest in energy saving projects. Energy savings are achieved by installing, improving or replacing energy savings equipment.

The Energy Savings Scheme is governed by NSW legislation. It places a mandatory obligation on Scheme Participants to obtain and surrender energy savings certificates, which represent energy savings.  The policy framework is the responsibility of the Department of Planning, Industry and Environment (DPIE). The Energy Savings Scheme is legislated to run until 2025 or until there is an equivalent national energy efficiency scheme. 

The ESS Rule

The Energy Savings Scheme Rule of 2009 or the ESS Rule sets out how energy saving certificates can be created and prescribes:

•	Types of eligible activities

•	Types of ineligible activities

•	Eligible applicants

•	Detailed calculation methodologies.

The Rule is amended from time to time. Rule changes occur to keep the Scheme up to date and to manage changes in the market and the emergence of new technologies.

However, the broad requirement is that an activity must reduce energy consumption without reducing production levels or quality of service.  Specifically, eligibility can be defined as either:

•	Modifying End-User Equipment

•	Modifying usage of End-User Equipment

•	Replacing End-User Equipment

•	Installing new End-User Equipment

•	Removing End-User Equipment.

Key Roles within the ESS

Key Roles within the ESS include 

IPART as the Scheme administrator and regulator, 

Original or Nominated Energy Savers: the owner or controller of the energy savings

Accredited Certificate Providers (ACPs) who can create and register Energy Savings Certificates.  

Scheme Participants purchase ESCs and surrender them to the Scheme Regulator to meet obligations they have under the Scheme

the Audit Services Panel who perform some of the Scheme Administrators statutory functions under The Electricity Supply (General) Regulation 2014 (s55 and s56) to ensure the ongoing compliance of Scheme Participants and Accredited Certificate Providers. 

Energy savings certificates (ESC) and Pricing

An energy savings certificate (ESC) is a tradeable certificate created under Division 7 of Part 9 of the Electricity Supply Act 1995. Each ESC represents one notional megawatt hour (MWh) of energy and acts as a tradeable certificate created under the Energy Savings Scheme. 

The ESC price varies depending on market conditions. There is no maximum price for an ESC. However, the penalty price acts as a practical maximum price. If a Scheme Participant does not surrender the required number of certificates in a given year (excluding any shortfall it is allowed to carry forward to the next compliance year), it must pay a penalty.

The ESS Registry 

The Registry is an online database which records the registration, transfer and ultimate surrender of energy savings certificates.  The functions of the Registry include:

Facilitating the registration and transfer of energy savings certificates

Listing details and tracking ownership of energy savings certificates

Allowing Scheme Participants to surrender energy savings certificates to meet mandatory obligations or company-specific offset schemes.

When a certiﬁcate is created, the Registry records information about that certiﬁcate including the entity, activity and activity type associated with it, and the vintage and creation date.  The Registry also tracks the certiﬁcate status (live, surrendered, or forfeited) and ownership history.  One certiﬁcate represents one notional megawatt hour attributable to energy savings arising from a recognised energy savings activity. 

ESS Audits

Audits are regularly conducted to protect the integrity of the scheme by verifying that certificates represent real energy savings.

Role of CBS

CBS Group propose to act as Downer’s Nominated Energy Saver and to partner with

Accredited Certificate Providers (ACPs) prior to the commencement of any HVAC upgrade works/trials,  to create energy savings certificates that can be sold to meet energy savings obligations. 

Noting:

Energy savings certificates can only be created for projects that were implemented in association with an ACP. 

A nominated energy saver must be nominated as at the implementation date

ACPs can only create certificates from recognised energy savings activities for which they are accredited for;

CBS Group will implement your energy savings activity in accordance with the Act, Regulation and the ESS Rule and comply with any accreditation notices specified.  CBS will take on the responsibility of complying with the requirements of the ESS. 

This will include:

•	Maintaining and storing records of the energy savings projects 

•	Reporting to the Energy Savings Scheme administrator on a regular basis

•	Having the energy savings projects audited by an auditor approved by IPART.

Scope of CBS Works

Specifically, these obligations will include:

Program Establishment

Establish and complete ACP project requirements and complete Energy Saver nomination of CBS Group.  

Define the Program ‘Tranches’ Working with the Fleet Engineering team to optimise the HVAC modification program and ESS framework to maximise net revenue (cost) of the modification for Downer. This may include assessment of additional modifications for inclusion in the scheme submission. 

Appoint the ACP upon agreement of the proposed measurement and verification methodology for this project

Documentation and Records Management

Define and document the project, scope of HVAC modifications, and proposed Analysis and Verification approach. Establishing defined record keeping arrangements.  The specific records kept depend on the energy savings activity, but must include the:

•	Location in which the energy savings activity occurred

•	Energy savings (calculated in accordance with the ESS Rule) arising from that activity

•	Methodology, data and assumptions used to calculate those energy savings.

Our record keeping arrangements will:

•	Facilitate the collection of the required records

•	Allow accurate records to be provided to the Scheme Administrator or to an auditor at any time

•	Retain the records for at least 6 years after ESC creation. To fulfil the requirements, CBS will provide:

•	A document register that lists the records that must be collected and retained to support their ESC creation

•	Processes and/or procedures for keeping records including step-by-step details of how records will be obtained, processed, maintained and controlled and the identity and the position(s) of the person(s) responsible for each of these steps in a process/procedure document

•	Quality assurance controls to ensure records are accurate.

ESC Application and ESC Audit Management

Manage the application and fulfilment process including the provision of mandated measurement and verification reporting. Maintaining defined record keeping arrangements, arranging for audits of our energy savings projects and advising IPART if there are any changes to the energy savings projects.

Stakeholder Management

Prepare and delivery briefings to Downer and support for TfNSW briefings on the proposed application

Proposed Program

July 2020 – July 2021

Program Notes

Benefits are obtained by establishing works within the ESS Framework prior to commencement. 

Noting that circumstances, including the cost of out-of-sequence works, the requirement to establish data over four seasons, and the impact of Covid 19 on passenger loads, may require program flexibility,  we propose an engagement model that maximises your  current planned investments in eligible equipment.

Fixed Costs

Certificate registration fees

1 January 2020 to 31 December 2020 fee is $0.88 per certificate

The application to register Energy Savings Certiﬁcates must be accompanied by the application fee. The application fee will be adjusted for movement in the Consumer Price Index each year. 

Transfer fees

XXXX - TBA

Costs and Fees

A retainer of $100,000 payable in six instalments (July 2020 – July 2021) to cover the works required to create eligible Energy Savings Certificates (ESC’s). 

Upon successful registration a fee of $2.00 per certificate to cover registration fees, audits and records maintenance over the life of the certificate (six years)
