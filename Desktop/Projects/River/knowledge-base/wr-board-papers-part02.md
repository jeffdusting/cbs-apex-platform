On behalf of the Board of Water Roads Pty Ltd, I am writing to invite you to attend our board meeting on 15 January 2026 as an observer, representing CBS Group Pty Ltd.

The meeting will address a comprehensive due diligence review and governance remediation plan in preparation for our Series A capital raising. Key matters to be discussed include:

Presentation of the DD Risk Register identifying corporate and governance issues arising from the Company's formation and early transactions.

PREAMBLE

The undersigned, being all of the shareholders of WaterRoads Pty Ltd ACN 686 900 014 (the Company), pass the following resolutions in writing pursuant to section 249A of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) (the Act).

These resolutions are effective when signed by all shareholders entitled to vote on the resolutions and have the same effect as if passed at a duly convened general meeting of the Company.

CURRENT SHAREHOLDERS AND VOTING ENTITLEMENTS

As at the date of these resolutions (and prior to the Recapitalisation taking effect), the shareholders of the Company and their voting entitlements are:

Note: These resolutions are signed prior to the Recapitalisation taking effect. The share structure above reflects the pre-Recapitalisation position. Following completion of the Recap Steps (defined below), the share structure will be as set out in Background section F below.

BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

A. Purpose of Recapitalisation

The Board of the Company resolved on 15 January 2026 (Board Meeting WR-2026-002) to implement a recapitalisation (the Recapitalisation) to:

(a) correct Historic Defects in the Company's capital structure arising from administrative errors, incomplete documentation, and unintended consequences of prior share issuances (as documented in the Recapitalisation Deed);

(b) align the share register with the Agreed Ownership structure of 96.00% CobaltBlu and 4.00% CBS Group, as agreed between the shareholders and reflected in the economic substance of their respective contributions to and interests in the Company;

(c) adopt a new two-class share structure (F Shares and Common Shares) with Founder Protective Provisions and automatic conversion mechanisms to facilitate the Company's Series A equity financing and future growth; and

(d) establish a clear, auditable, and investor-ready capital structure suitable for institutional investment and future corporate transactions.

B. Recapitalisation Deed

The Recapitalisation is documented in a Recapitalisation Deed dated 15 January 2026 (the Recap Deed) among:

WaterRoads Pty Ltd (the Company)

CobaltBlu Pty Ltd ACN 103 595 071 ATF CobaltBlu Unit Trust (CobaltBlu)

CBS Group Pty Ltd ACN 164 870 080 (CBS Group or CBS)

The Recap Deed sets out:

the definition and acknowledgment of Historic Defects;

the Agreed Ownership of 96% CobaltBlu / 4% CBS;

the sequential Recap Steps (defined below);

the Effective Time for implementation (11:59pm Sydney time on the Completion Date, being the date these shareholder resolutions are passed or such other date as specified in the Recap Deed);

representations, warranties, and confirmations by each party;

a solvency declaration by the Company's directors; and

conditions precedent, including shareholder and director approvals.

C. Related Documents

In addition to the Recap Deed, the following documents form part of the Recapitalisation implementation framework:

(a) CBS Consent and Waiver Deed

A deed dated 15 January 2026 among CBS Group, the Company, and CobaltBlu (the CBS Consent Waiver Deed), under which CBS Group:

consents to the Recapitalisation and the Recap Steps;

waives any rights it may have under the existing constitution or prior agreements that would be inconsistent with or prevent the Recapitalisation;

acknowledges that the reduction of its shareholding from 9.09% to 4.00% reflects the correction of Historic Defects and the Agreed Ownership; and

provides certain confirmations and undertakings regarding its secured loan facility and security interests.

(b) Amended and Restated Constitution — Version 3.1

The new constitution of the Company (the New Constitution), adopted by Special Resolution 1 below, which:

establishes a two-class share structure (F Shares and Common Shares);

sets out Founder Protective Provisions (clause 20);

provides for automatic F Share conversion upon a Qualifying Financing (clause 4.3);

establishes Founder Director appointment rights (clause 16.2);

authorises a 10% ESOP pool (clause 26); and

repeals and replaces the existing constitution (as originally adopted 8 May 2025 and amended by Deed of Amendment dated 1 August 2025).

A copy of the New Constitution has been provided to each shareholder and is initialled by the Company Secretary for identification purposes.

(c) Shareholder and Director Resolutions

These WaterRoads shareholder resolutions (this document);

CobaltBlu shareholder and director resolutions dated 15 January 2026 approving CobaltBlu's participation in the Recapitalisation;

CBS Group board resolutions dated 15 January 2026 (Board Meeting CBS-2026-001) approving CBS's participation in the Recapitalisation (with Jeffrey Alan Dusting abstaining and Jim Ellwood as independent director approving).

D. Sequential Recapitalisation Steps

The Recapitalisation involves the following sequential steps (collectively, the Recap Steps), to occur at the Effective Time (11:59pm Sydney time on the Completion Date):

Order of Implementation: The Recap Steps occur in the order set out above. All steps are treated as occurring at the Effective Time for legal and regulatory purposes, but the sequential order is preserved for the purpose of determining the operation and effect of each step.

E. Correction of Historic Defects and Agreed Ownership

The Recapitalisation corrects the following Historic Defects (as acknowledged in the Recap Deed):

(a) CBS Subscription (September 2025): CBS Group subscribed for shares intended to represent 4.00% of the Company's issued capital, but due to administrative error and unclear documentation, CBS was issued 100 Class B shares representing 9.09% (based on a total of 1,100 shares rather than the intended total that would have resulted in 4.00%).

(b) Incomplete Prior Documentation: Certain share issuances and capital structure decisions were not fully documented or were based on incorrect assumptions about the total number of shares on issue.

(c) Inconsistent Cap Table: The pre-Recapitalisation share register (1,000 FOU shares held by CobaltBlu, 100 Class B shares held by CBS) does not reflect the economic substance of the parties' agreement and contributions, which was for CobaltBlu to hold 96% and CBS to hold 4%.

Agreed Ownership: The Agreed Ownership structure, as acknowledged and confirmed by both CobaltBlu and CBS Group in the Recap Deed and CBS Consent Deed, is:

CobaltBlu: 96.00% (reflecting CobaltBlu's status as founder shareholder and principal investor)

CBS Group: 4.00% (reflecting CBS's minority equity stake and secured loan facility)

The Recapitalisation realigns the share register to the Agreed Ownership.

F. Post-Recapitalisation Share Structure

Following completion of all Recap Steps, the Company's share structure will be:

All shares are fully paid.

Class Rights: F Shares and Common Shares rank equally for dividends and distributions, except:

Founder Protective Provisions (clause 20 of New Constitution) apply while F Share Majority holds ≥30% on a Fully Diluted Basis;

F Shares automatically convert to Common Shares 1:1 upon a Qualifying Financing (clause 4.3);

Founder Director appointment right (clause 16.2) available while F Share Majority holds ≥20% on a Fully Diluted Basis.

G. Series A Financing and Future Dilution

The Company intends to pursue a Series A equity financing following the Recapitalisation. Key implications for shareholders:

(a) Dilution: Both CobaltBlu and CBS Group will be diluted by the Series A issuance. Expected Series A terms (including amount, valuation, and investor rights) are to be determined by the Board acting in accordance with fiduciary duties and the governance framework (including the Conflict of Interest Policy V6.0 – Institutional and, once appointed, independent director oversight).

(b) F Share Conversion: Upon completion of a Qualifying Financing (as defined in clause 1.1 of the New Constitution), all F Shares (including CobaltBlu's 960,000 F Shares) will automatically convert into Common Shares on a 1:1 basis. Post-conversion, all shareholders will hold Common Shares ranking equally (subject to any preferential rights granted to Series A Investors via a new class of Preference Shares or Series A Shares).

(c) Shareholders Agreement: Series A Investors will require a Shareholders Agreement governing rights, restrictions, board composition, and exit mechanisms. The Shareholders Agreement will be negotiated and executed following Series A term sheet execution and will bind the Company, CobaltBlu, CBS Group, and potentially individual shareholders.

(d) Independent Director: The Company has committed (in the Conflict of Interest Policy V6.0 – Institutional, adopted 5 February 2026) to appoint an independent director at or within 60 days of Series A close, providing disinterested oversight of related-party transactions and governance decisions.

Special Resolution 1 — Adoption of New Constitution

RESOLVED AS A SPECIAL RESOLUTION that, pursuant to section 136(2) of the Act:

(a) Repeal of Existing Constitution

The Company's existing constitution (as originally adopted on incorporation on 8 May 2025, and as amended by the Deed of Amendment to Constitution dated 1 August 2025) be and is hereby repealed in its entirety with effect from the Effective Time.

The document titled "Amended and Restated Constitution of WaterRoads Pty Ltd — Version 3.1", a copy of which has been provided to each shareholder and is initialled by the Company Secretary for the purposes of identification, be and is hereby adopted as the constitution of the Company with effect from the Effective Time.

(c) Key Provisions of New Constitution

The shareholders note and approve the following key provisions of the New Constitution:

(i) Two-Class Share Structure (Clauses 3–4)

F Shares (Founder Shares) — initially issued to CobaltBlu; rank equally with Common Shares for dividends and distributions; automatically convert to Common Shares 1:1 upon a Qualifying Financing (clause 4.3).

Common Shares — initially issued to CBS Group; Series A Investors will subscribe for Common Shares (or a new class of Preference Shares with preferential rights).

(ii) Founder Protective Provisions (Clause 20)

While the F Share Majority (holders of >50% of F Shares on a Fully Diluted Basis) holds ≥30% of all shares on a Fully Diluted Basis, the Company must obtain the prior written consent of the F Share Majority before:

amending the Constitution in a manner that adversely affects F Share holders' rights;

issuing shares ranking senior to or pari passu with F Shares (except pursuant to a Qualifying Financing or the ESOP);

disposing of all or substantially all of the Company's assets;

merging, consolidating, or selling the Company;

voluntarily liquidating or winding up the Company;

changing the principal business of the Company; or

taking any other Reserved Matter action as specified in clause 20.1.

Threshold Rationale: The 30% threshold ensures Founder Protections remain in place through at least one dilutive financing round post-Recap, but will cease if CobaltBlu's ownership falls below 30% (for example, following a large Series B or later-stage financing).

(iii) Founder Director Appointment Rights (Clause 16.2)

While the F Share Majority holds ≥20% of all shares on a Fully Diluted Basis, the F Share Majority has the right to appoint one Founder Director to the Board.

Current Founder Director: Following the Recapitalisation, the F Share Majority (CobaltBlu) has nominated Sarah Margaret Taylor as the Founder Director.

(iv) Automatic F Share Conversion (Clause 4.3)

Upon completion of a Qualifying Financing (defined in clause 1.1 as a bona fide equity financing in which the Company issues shares for aggregate gross proceeds of at least A$1,000,000 on arms-length terms approved by the Board), all F Shares automatically convert into Common Shares on a 1:1 basis without further shareholder action.

Rationale: Automatic conversion simplifies the capital structure post-Series A and ensures all shareholders (Founders, CBS, and Series A Investors) hold the same class of shares (or, if Series A Investors subscribe for a new class of Preference Shares, the Founders and CBS hold the same class of ordinary/common equity).

(v) ESOP Pool (Clause 26)

The Board is authorised to establish and administer an employee share option plan (ESOP) for up to 10% of the Company's fully diluted share capital (clause 26).

Grants may be made to employees, directors, consultants, and advisers in accordance with the ESOP Rules (to be adopted by the Board) and individual grant determinations.

Dilution: ESOP grants will dilute all shareholders pro-rata. The 10% cap is typical for early-stage Australian companies and is intended to facilitate employee retention and incentive alignment.

(vi) General Provisions

All other provisions of the New Constitution, including provisions relating to:

Dividend and distribution rights (clauses 6–7);

Voting rights (one vote per share unless otherwise specified) (clause 10);

Transfer restrictions and pre-emptive rights (clauses 8–9);

Board composition, appointment, and removal (clauses 16–17);

General meetings and resolutions (clauses 11–15);

Winding up and distribution of surplus assets (clause 18);

Disinterested director provisions for related-party transactions (clause 48.1); and

Other standard proprietary company constitutional provisions.

(d) Effect

Upon adoption of the New Constitution:

The existing constitution (including the 1 August 2025 Deed of Amendment) is repealed and has no further effect.

The New Constitution governs all rights, obligations, and relationships among the Company, its shareholders, and its directors.

All references in the Recap Deed, CBS Consent Deed, and related documents to "the Constitution" or "Constitution V3.1" refer to the New Constitution adopted by this resolution.

Special Resolution 2 — Selective Reduction of Capital (Cancellation of Existing Shares)

RESOLVED AS A SPECIAL RESOLUTION that, pursuant to sections 256B and 256C of the Act:

(a) Reduction of Capital

The Company's share capital be reduced by the cancellation of all 1,100 existing shares in the Company, being:

1,000 FOU/Founder's shares held by CobaltBlu Pty Ltd; and

100 Class B shares held by CBS Group Pty Ltd.

(b) No Consideration

No consideration is payable to any shareholder in respect of the cancellation. The cancellation is effected on a share-for-share basis as part of the integrated Recapitalisation, with new shares to be issued immediately following cancellation pursuant to Ordinary Resolution 1.

(c) Fairness to Shareholders (s256B(1))

The reduction is fair and reasonable to the Company's shareholders as a whole because:

(i) Integrated Recapitalisation

The cancellation is part of an integrated Recapitalisation designed to correct Historic Defects and align the share register with the Agreed Ownership. It is not a standalone capital return or buyback, but rather a mechanical step in a broader restructure.

(ii) Share-for-Share Exchange

New shares are being issued to the same shareholders immediately following cancellation, on a share-for-share basis adjusted to reflect the Agreed Ownership:

CobaltBlu surrenders 1,000 FOU shares → receives 96,000 F Shares (960,000 post-split)

CBS surrenders 100 Class B shares → receives 4,000 Common Shares (40,000 post-split)

Each shareholder's percentage ownership moves to the Agreed Ownership (96% / 4%), which both shareholders have acknowledged and consented to as the correct economic allocation.

(iii) No Cash Payment

No shareholder is receiving any cash payment, distribution, or other consideration. The transaction is capital-neutral from the Company's perspective (no outflow of assets).

(iv) Correction of Historic Defects

The cancellation corrects unintended consequences of prior administrative errors and documentation gaps (Historic Defects), restoring the capital structure to the parties' original intent and agreement.

(v) Facilitates Series A Financing

The Recapitalisation (including the cancellation) is necessary to establish a clean, investor-ready capital structure suitable for the Company's Series A financing and future growth.

(vi) Both Shareholders Consent

Both CobaltBlu and CBS Group have independently reviewed and approved the Recapitalisation, including the cancellation and reissuance, through their respective governance processes:

CobaltBlu: shareholder and director resolutions of January 2026 with s194 reliance and full conflict disclosure.

CBS Group: disinterested director approval (Jim Ellwood) on 16 January 2026 with Jeff Dusting recused.

(vii) Legal Advice Obtained

The Company has obtained independent legal advice confirming that:

the Recapitalisation structure complies with ss256B and 256C;

the selective reduction is fair and reasonable to shareholders;

the cancellation does not materially prejudice creditors; and

all procedural requirements have been satisfied.

(d) No Material Prejudice to Creditors (s256C(1))

The cancellation does not materially prejudice the Company's ability to pay its creditors, having regard to:

(i) Solvency Declaration

The directors have made a solvency declaration in the Recapitalisation Deed (and will make a formal declaration under s256C(1)) that, in their opinion, the Company will be able to pay its debts as and when they become due and payable.

(ii) No Asset Outflow

No cash or other assets are being paid out to shareholders. The cancellation is a book entry that does not reduce the Company's net assets.

(iii) Financial Position

As at the date of these resolutions, the Company:

has sufficient cash reserves to meet current and projected liabilities;

has no material outstanding creditor obligations other than the CBS Secured Loan (A$200,000 drawn, to be repaid from Series A proceeds);

is a going concern with a viable business plan and revenue pipeline; and

will receive substantial capital from the Series A financing (expected Q1 2026).

Disclosure is required whenever a director has, or may reasonably be perceived to have, a material personal interest in a matter before the Board. This includes, without limitation:

(c) include explicit acknowledgment of the director's obligations under this Policy;

(f) be updated whenever the nature or extent of the interest materially changes.

Rationale: With both directors equally interested, recusal would leave no quorum. Section 194 participation with full disclosure is the only available mechanism until an independent director is appointed. Independent Advice provides external validation of commercial reasonableness.

6.2.3 Director Remuneration and Benefits

Decisions on director remuneration, equity grants, expense reimbursement, or other benefits to directors or their associates require:

(a) disclosure of the interest by the relevant director(s);

(b) where an independent director has been appointed, approval by the independent director(s);

(c) where no independent director is available, both directors vote under s194 with the decision benchmarked against market comparables (including comparable roles at similar-stage companies, industry surveys, or adviser recommendations) and documented in the minutes;

(d) consideration of whether member approval is required under Chapter 2E (s208); and

(e) for Material Transactions or non-standard arrangements, Independent Advice on market terms.

6.2.4 Materiality Guidance

To assist in applying the conflict management framework, the Board has adopted the following materiality guidance:

Minor conflicts (typically managed via monitoring):

Transactions under A$10,000 on arm's length terms

Standard commercial hospitality or gifts of trivial value (under A$250)

Matters where the director's interest is remote or purely theoretical

Material conflicts (require recusal, Independent Advice, or heightened safeguards):

Transactions exceeding A$50,000

Any transaction involving non-standard or preferential terms

Any transaction where the director has competing fiduciary duties

Director remuneration or equity grants

Enforcement, waiver, or variation of existing related party contracts

When in doubt, directors should err on the side of disclosure and seek guidance from the Chair or Company Secretary.

6.3 Interim Period Protocol — Pre-Independent Director

High-risk transaction deferral: For the period between Series A close and appointment of the independent director, the following transactions should be deferred unless failure to act would materially prejudice the Company's interests:

(a) new related-party contracts or arrangements with CobaltBlu or CBS Group (excluding ordinary-course transactions contemplated in existing agreements);

(b) material changes to the terms of the CBS Loan or any other existing related-party arrangement;

(c) significant amendments to director remuneration or equity incentive arrangements; and

(d) any transaction exceeding A$100,000 involving a related party.

Exception for urgent matters: If a high-risk transaction cannot be deferred, the Board must:

obtain Independent Advice;

document clearly why deferral would prejudice the Company;

obtain investor notification or consent if required under the shareholders' agreement; and

minute the decision comprehensively.

7. INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR OVERSIGHT

THIS SECTION ACTIVATES UPON APPOINTMENT OF THE FIRST INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR

The Company has committed to appointing an independent director at or within 60 days of the Series A close. Upon that appointment, the procedures in this section take effect and supplement (but do not replace) the procedures in Section 6.

7.1 Independent Director Selection

The Series A lead investor will be consulted in the nomination and approval process for the first independent director to ensure the appointee meets institutional investor expectations for independence and expertise.

7.2 Enhanced Conflict Procedures

Once an independent director is appointed:

(a) Related-party transactions must be approved by the independent director(s) before the Board proceeds. Where the independent director does not approve, the matter may only proceed with member approval under s208 or if it falls within a recognised Chapter 2E exception.

(b) CBS Group matters: Jeff recuses. The independent director and Sarah (with spousal interest disclosed) form the decision-making quorum. The independent director's concurrence is required for any resolution to proceed.

(c) CobaltBlu matters: Both founding directors disclose and participate under s194. The independent director provides a disinterested vote. The independent director's concurrence is required for the resolution to be treated as having disinterested approval under Constitution Clause 48.1.

(d) Director remuneration: Approval by independent director required. Founding directors may not vote on their own remuneration packages once an independent director is available to provide disinterested oversight.

(e) Quarterly conflict review: The independent director will lead a quarterly review of all Standing Notices, any new conflicts, and the adequacy of conflict management procedures (see Section 9).

7.3 Committee Structures

These committees, if established, will have specific responsibilities for reviewing related-party transactions, director remuneration, and conflict management processes.

8. CHAPTER 2E — RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS

The Company must comply with Chapter 2E of the Act when giving a financial benefit to a related party. The Board must consider, for each proposed related-party transaction:

(a) whether the transaction constitutes a "financial benefit" to a "related party" within the meaning of sections 228–229 (noting that each director, CobaltBlu, CBS Group, and the spouse of each director are related parties);

(b) whether an exception under sections 210–216 applies — in particular:

s210: arm's length exception (terms no more favourable than reasonably expected if dealing at arm's length)

s211: reasonable remuneration exception

s212: small amounts exception (less than A$5,000 and reasonable in the circumstances)

(c) if no exception applies, whether member approval has been or can be obtained under section 208;

(d) the documentation required to evidence the exception relied upon (for example, Independent Advice, market comparables, valuation report, or legal opinion); and

(e) for Material Transactions (over A$50,000), mandatory Independent Advice to confirm arm's length terms or commercial reasonableness.

(c)	the Independent Director may be removed by ordinary resolution of the Board with the approval of both the F Share Majority and the Investor Majority (if applicable).

(a)	all interested Directors must disclose their interests in accordance with clause 18.1;

(b)	the interested Directors may be present and vote on the resolution in reliance on section 194 of the Corporations Act (the Company being a proprietary company);

(c)	the Board must record in the minutes: the nature and extent of each Director’s interest, the disclosure made, the reliance on section 194, and the commercial rationale for the transaction; and

(d)	the Board must consider whether the transaction is on arm’s length terms and, if not, whether it is reasonable and in the best interests of the Company.

(p)	enter into any agreement that restricts the Company’s ability to conduct its business in any material respect; and

(q)	approve any transaction or series of related transactions outside the ordinary course of business with a value exceeding A$500,000.

(a)	the Founders retain their Founder Director appointment rights under clause 16.2 (subject to the 20% threshold in that clause);

(b)	the Founders retain all class rights attaching to F Shares under clause 4, including the right under clause 4.6 to consent to any variation or cancellation of F Share class rights;

(c)	the Founders retain the right under clause 22.3 to withhold consent to any SHA provision that would derogate from the Founder Protective Provisions; and

(d)	any amendment to this clause 20 (including to the 30% threshold) continues to require the consent of the F Share Majority under clause 30.4, regardless of the Founders’ percentage holding at the time.

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes the framework by which directors and officers of WaterRoads Pty Ltd (the
Company) identify, disclose, and manage conflicts of interest and material personal interests in
connection with the affairs of the Company. It is designed to:

(d) meet the governance standards expected by institutional investors, including private equity
and venture capital funds undertaking due diligence; and
(e) establish practical procedures that reflect the Company's current board composition and
transition to independent board oversight following the Series A investment.

5. DISCLOSURE OBLIGATIONS
5.1 Statutory Disclosure (s191/s192)
A director who has a material personal interest in a matter relating to the affairs of the Company
must, as soon as practicable after becoming aware of the interest, disclose the nature and
extent of that interest to the other directors. Disclosure may be given:
(a) orally at a board meeting, to be recorded in the minutes of that meeting (s191); or
(b) by Standing Notice in writing, tabled at the next board meeting (s192).
Penalty for non-disclosure: Section 191(1) creates a strict liability offence carrying a penalty of
up to 50 penalty units (currently A$22,200) per director per undisclosed matter.

5.2 When Disclosure Is Required
Disclosure is required whenever a director has, or may reasonably be perceived to have, a
material personal interest in a matter before the Board. This includes, without limitation:
(a) any transaction, arrangement, or dealing between the Company and CobaltBlu, CBS Group,
or any entity in which a director holds an interest;
(b) any matter that confers or may confer a financial benefit (as defined in s229) on a related
party;
(c) any matter in which a director's spouse, de facto partner, or close family member has a
material interest;
(d) any matter in which a director has a conflicting duty to another entity (including a directorship
obligation to CobaltBlu or CBS Group);
(e) approval of the Company's remuneration, equity grants, or benefits to directors or their
associates;
(f) any contract or proposed contract in which a director has a direct or indirect pecuniary
interest; and
(g) any matter where the director's judgment may reasonably be perceived as influenced by
interests other than the Company's interests.

5.3 Standing Notices
Where a director's interest in a matter is of an ongoing nature (for example, a directorship of
another entity, a shareholding, or a spousal relationship), the director should give a Standing
Notice under section 192. A Standing Notice must:
(a) be in writing;
(b) set out the nature and extent of the interest;
(c) include explicit acknowledgment of the director's obligations under this Policy;
(d) include a statement that the notice extends to any matter in which the interests may
reasonably be perceived to influence the director's judgment;
(e) be tabled at the next board meeting after it is given; and
(f) be updated whenever the nature or extent of the interest materially changes.
A Standing Notice has ongoing effect and covers all matters within its stated scope. It does not
need to be re-given at each meeting, but the Chair should note at the commencement of each
meeting whether any Standing Notices are in effect and whether any updates are required.

5.4 Timing and Form
On appointment: Each new director must complete a disclosure form and provide a Standing
Notice (if applicable) before attending their first board meeting.
Annually: Each director must confirm annually that their Standing Notice remains current or
provide an updated notice.
As conflicts arise: Directors must disclose new conflicts immediately upon becoming aware of
them.
At meetings: The Chair will ask for conflict declarations at the start of each meeting for matters
on the agenda.
