     Client: Transport for NSW (TfNSW)
     Period: 2020-2021
     Role: Lead Transaction Advisors
     Context:
     Our team was engaged by Transport for NSW to lead a comprehensive Market Interactive Process
     (MIP) for the Western Harbour Tunnel project, a major toll road infrastructure initiative in Sydney.
     TfNSW needed to explore the viability and structure of potential concession arrangements while
     balancing public value and private sector interest.

     Scope:
     We designed and executed a structured market sounding process that engaged over 30 market
     participants including major concessionaires (Transurban, Fulton Hogan/Egis), financiers, toll road
     operators, and maintenance contractors. Our engagement tested various concession models, risk
     allocations, payment mechanisms, and commercial structures.

     Approach:

     Strategic Planning Phase & Engagement
     Developed an MIP strategy, information memorandum, and tailored questionnaires, leveraging CBS
     Group’s tolling expertise.
     • Developed a detailed market sounding methodology and communication strategy
     • Created a comprehensive information memorandum outlining the project scope, objectives, and
         potential concession opportunities
     • Designed bespoke questionnaires addressing key commercial and technical considerations
     • Established a robust probity framework to ensure transparency and fairness
     Market Engagement Phase:
     Facilitated workshops in Sydney, synthesizing feedback from diverse stakeholders into a cohesive
     framework.
     • Coordinated and facilitated three rounds of interactive sessions with participants
     • Conducted one-on-one confidential discussions focused on commercial structures, risk appetite,
         and deal parameters
     • Maintained detailed records of all interactions while preserving commercial confidentiality
     • Managed data room containing critical project information for market assessment
     Analysis and Recommendation Phase:

The opportunity
This RFP is issued by the Ministry of Transport, referred to below as “the Buyer” or “we” or “us”.

The Ministry of Transport is leading work to explore the value and viability of concessions on New Zealand
toll roads. Concessions involve engaging a private firm (called a concessionaire) to operate and maintain a
toll road in exchange for an up-front payment to the Crown.

The New Zealand Government is interested in exploring the ability of concessions to enhance the efficiency
of New Zealand toll roads and to generate up-front capital for reinvestment in other transport priorities.

Market sounding
Cabinet has agreed to undertake market soundings with potential toll road concessionaires to understand
the viability of concessions in New Zealand and how they could be best structured, phased or packaged to be
value accretive to the Crown while still achieving public sector objectives. This approach is intended to
reduce procurement risk for any potential subsequent transaction by identifying and addressing concerns
early, leading to a smoother and more competitive bidding process.

Our objectives for market sounding are to:

     •   Confirm whether concessions are a valuable proposition to the Government

     •   Understand investor appetite for New Zealand opportunities

     •   Test different ways to structure concessions.

Work on toll road concessions was announced at the NZ Infrastructure Investment Summit on 14 March
2025. We would like market sounding to occur between May and late-June 2025.

The Ministry of Transport will co-lead the market sounding process alongside National Infrastructure Funding
and Financing Limited (NIFFCo). Treasury and the New Zealand Transport Agency (NZTA) are also supporting
this work. Governance arrangements for this programme are currently being confirmed.


What we need
The Ministry of Transport is seeking experienced advisors to support market sounding on toll road
concessions with toll road concessionaires, operators and financiers.

We are seeking specialist support to:

     •   Scope the market sounding phase – including process, information requirements and timelines

     •   Advise on managing probity for the market sounding phase (and subsequent phases)

     •   Prepare material to support market sounding discussions (see ‘Key deliverables for Phase 1’ below)

     •   Identify key market participants (potential toll road concessionaires, operators and financiers) to
         engage in market sounding, and help the Ministry organise and undertake discussions with these
         participants

     •   Support discussions with market participants to achieve market sounding objectives

     •   Develop the work programme beyond market sounding including framing up any potential future
         stages in the transaction process (as appropriate)

 2.1     Background
The Ministry of Transport is seeking advisors to support market sounding on toll road concessions with

     •   Advise on managing probity for the market sounding phase (and subsequent phases)

     •   Support discussions with market participants to achieve market sounding objectives

     •   Develop the work programme beyond market sounding including framing up any potential future

     •   Based on insights from the market sounding process, identify the best structures, phasing,
         packaging, and approach to market viable and value-adding concessions on New Zealand toll roads.

Work on toll road concessions will be progressed in phases:

Phase 1: Market sounding
In this phase, we will undertake market sounding with potential concessionaires, operators and financiers.
Then, based on insights from that process, identify the best structures, phasing, and approach to market
viable and value-adding concessions on New Zealand toll roads. This work will lead to advice to the Ministers
of Transport and Infrastructure about the viability of concessions and the best ways to design and progress
them.

We are expecting to begin market sounding in May 2025.

At this stage, we are seeking advisory support for this first phase only.



Phase 2: Preparation for transactions phase
Subject to the outcomes of market sounding, a second phase of work would involve preparing for
transactions on any viable concessions. The scope of this phase is to be confirmed but could include formal
scoping studies and preparing data rooms, information memorandums and other material for the
transaction process.

Support for this phase will be contingent upon the results of Phase 1 above and subject to Ministerial /
Cabinet approval.
