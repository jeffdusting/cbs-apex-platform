Our Understanding

Sydney Trains is planning to implement and commission a range of Energy Conservation Measures (ECM) in the form of HVAC upgrades across the fleet of trains operating across the Greater Sydney Metropolitan Area. Downer will be performing these upgrades over a period of 4-5 years.  CBS Group is looking to access funding from the NSW Energy Savings Scheme (ESS) with minimal risk and effort, to provide additional capital and reduce the net cost for Downer throughout the upgrade cycle.  

The Energy Saving Certificates (ESCs) under the ESS provide incentive funding for large scale energy efficiency projects in NSW. The funding may be accessed yearly in arrears after the energy savings are realized using a Metered Baseline Method (MBM) or in a lump sum using forward creation of up to 10 years using the Project Impact Assessment with Measurement and Verification (PIAM&V) method.   The Sampling Method is a sub-method of PIAM&V that allows for multiple site ESC creation using a sampling method where there is a population of similar ECMs.  This method is the recommended option for Sydney Trains upgrades.

The exact value of ESCs to the project is uncertain as the ESC price at time of creation following the project, and the final quantity to be created are not fully known until the measurements and modelling has been performed. Nevertheless, for this project the value is substantial. 

Wattly holds the accreditations under the NSW ESS to create ESCs and has a range of forward-contracting options to hedge the ESC price that can help to firm up this value, or we can simply trade ESCs on the wholesale market at the time of creation. We have extensive experience in the measurement and verification techniques required for this process, and creation of ESCs in NSW.

NSW Energy Saving Scheme - ESS

The NSW ESS reduces electricity consumption by creating financial incentives for busineese to invest in energy savings projects. Energy savings are achieved by Accredited Certificate Providers (ACPs such as Wattly) assisting businesses to upgrading or installing more efficient equipment.  Wattly registers a number of Energy Savings Certificates proportional to the energy savings achieved in the upgrade.  Wattly sells the certificates to Scheme Participants (Energy Retailers such as AGL, Origin, EA, etc) who have an obligation under the Scheme to meet energy savings targets.

Businesses performing the energy saving upgrades (the original energy savers), must legally nominate Wattly as the energy saver to give Wattly the right to register the Energy Saving Certificates.  

Energy Savings from the upgrade are "brought forward" for up to 10 years of operation and the energy savings for this period are registered as Energy Saving Certificates (ESC).  An ESC represents a nominal MWh (Megawatt hour) of energy saved.

Replaced equipment cannot be reused and must be permanently decommissioned.  This is to ensure that the old inefficient equipment is not used elsewhere after it is removed.

The Independent Pricing and Regulatory Tribunal of NSW (IPART) is both the Scheme Administrator and Scheme Regulator.

The business paying for the energy saving upgrade much nominate (sign the Nomination Form) BEFORE the energy saving upgrade is fully installed (commissioned)

The current legislated end date of the NSW ESS is the end of 2025.  The ESS has strong bipartisan support and the current government is expanding the program and rebrand it as the NSW Energy Security Safeguard.  Wattly expects that the program will be rolled forward to at least 2030.

ESC creation method recommended

Wattly is accredited for a range of methods of ESC creation in NSW, and our role is to optimize the process by selecting the best method(s) for your situation. 

The PIAM&V Sampling Method will avoid performing Measurement & Verification (M&V) on every train set upgraded.  Note that HVAC has seasonal variation 12 months of data (before and after the upgrade) is the required measurement period to determine the energy savings.

The options for using this upgrade.

Perform Sampling Method M&V on a tranche of trains set to and claiming the energy savings for the population of trains in that tranche.  This method will allow shorter claiming periods and maintain claims under the 50,000 ESC limit

Perform Sampling Method M&V across the entire fleet and claim energy savings for train sets upgraded on a periodic basis (eg quarterly).  This method is desirable in that it may reduce the total amount of M&V, but will hit limitations on total ESC creation under the current guidelines.

Metered Baseline Method.  This method involves measuring the energy savings at the boundary of the train network and claiming the actual measure energy savings each year in arrears with a regression model accounting for production variations.  This method is likely to be difficult to implement due to an aggregated view of the entire network and the potential for unexplained variations in the network. 

At this stage option 1 above is the preferred option at this stage to ensure fastest time to funds whilst keeping M&V work to a minimum.  As further exploratory work is performed the other options will remain under consideration to ensure the option that is most efficient and provides the highest level of funding for Downer will be pursed.
