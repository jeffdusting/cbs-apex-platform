On behalf of CBS Group, I am pleased to submit our alternate offer for the provision of Asset Management

products and is designed to operate in parallel with either our conforming base offer or Alternate 1 offer,

The offer provides TfNSW with access to a suite of CBS Group specialist products developed through our
extensive experience delivering asset management outcomes for major infrastructure. These products
represent proven methodologies, analytical tools and frameworks that have contributed to more than $1
billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years.
Product Licensing

deploy the product independently across the organisation. This pathway suits products where TfNSW seeks
long-term capability uplift and internal ownership.

usage charges. This pathway provides flexibility for time-limited initiatives, pilot programs or where TfNSW
prefers operational expenditure over capital investment.

amendments to the contract terms. These amendments address intellectual property provisions, licensing

Acceptance of these amendments enables the product framework to operate effectively alongside the
primary engagement.
This framework provides TfNSW with the flexibility to select the most appropriate commercial approach for
each product, enabling rapid deployment of proven tools to support asset management objectives.
We have enclosed a detailed proposal outlining a selection of current products, licensing options, proposed
contract amendments, and the value each product is designed to create. Thank you for the opportunity to
present this alternate offer. We look forward to discussing it with you further.

Executive summary
Situation: Delivering effective asset management for complex infrastructure requires sophisticated tools,
methodologies and analytical frameworks. Developing these capabilities internally demands significant
investment in time, expertise and iterative refinement. Traditional consultant engagements provide access to
such tools on a project-by-project basis, but the intellectual property remains with the consultant—requiring
repeated engagement to access the same capabilities.
Complication: TfNSW faces growing pressure to deliver asset management outcomes efficiently while
building sustainable internal capability. Reliance on consultant-held tools creates dependency and limits
knowledge transfer. Procuring or developing equivalent products independently would require substantial
investment with uncertain outcomes. Standard contract terms do not contemplate product licensing or
service-based access models, creating a structural barrier to accessing proven tools through mechanisms
suited to their nature.
Response: CBS Group proposes an alternate engagement model designed to operate in parallel with either
the conforming base offer or Alternate 1 offer. This model provides access to our suite of specialist products
through a flexible dual-pathway commercial framework with three complementary mechanisms.
•   Product Licensing provides perpetual access to CBS Group products, enabling TfNSW to deploy proven
    tools independently across the organisation. This pathway supports long-term capability uplift and
    internal ownership of critical asset management methodologies.
•   Product as a Service provides access through an implementation fee and monthly usage charges. This
    pathway offers flexibility for time-limited initiatives, pilot programs or where operational expenditure is
    preferred over capital investment. It includes ongoing support and updates for the duration of the service
    arrangement.
•   Contract Amendments for Product Access details a set of proposed amendments to accommodate the
    licensing and usage mechanisms required. These amendments address intellectual property provisions,
    licensing arrangements and service-based commercial structures not contemplated in the standard
    terms, enabling the product framework to operate effectively alongside the primary engagement.
We bring a suite of proven asset management products, methodologies and analytical tools that have
delivered more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years. It is with this track
record that we offer these products with confidence in the value they will deliver.

1.2.         Initiative Selection and Prioritisation
The initiatives listed above represent opportunities identified through CBS Group's existing engagement with
TfNSW and analysis of strategic priorities. Not all initiatives need proceed simultaneously—the value-based
model allows for selective engagement based on TfNSW priorities and resource availability.
We propose the following approach to initiative selection:
     1. Joint prioritisation workshop: CBS Group and TfNSW jointly review the seed list and agree priority
        initiatives
     2. Value assessment: For each priority initiative, both parties review the value hypothesis and agree
        target outcomes
     3. Commercial agreement: Value target, duration, and value share percentage are agreed for each
        initiative
     4. Phased commencement: Initiatives commence in agreed sequence, allowing learnings to inform
        subsequent engagements
