PIAM&V Method 

The Project Impact Assessment with Measurement and Veriﬁcation (PIAM&V) method is based on internationally recognised measurement and veriﬁcation principals to calculate energy savings. It allows “equipment level” energy savings to be deemed for up to ten years, using persistence and conﬁdence factors to discount initial certiﬁcate creation. In this method, the term ‘energy’ may refer to electricity, gas, or both.

The PIAM&V method uses regression analysis to model the energy consumption of a system against an independent variable or variables before and after the project is implemented, and then applies this to a “normal year” of operation to calculate a statistically valid difference in energy consumption.

As we discussed last week, the following is a proposal for CBS Group to work with you to further qualify the potential for a range of alternate funding mechanisms to be used to support current operations and initiatives – initially focused upon the TfNSW Fleet Enhancements.

Context

Situation

Downer has a range of innovations and improvements that they propose to their clients for mutual benefit.   A recent example of this is the development of the suite of enhancements for the TfNSW Rolling Stock fleet that included improvements leading to reduced maintenance, improved passenger experience & safety, and reductions in energy usage.

Complication

As is often the case, these innovation and improvement initiatives struggle to progress from concept to project.   Often this is due to challenges securing the up-front capital to fund the project, typically because;

the benefits are accrued by others – other departments, customers, the public, etc

the benefits in operation expense cannot be accrued to create the required capital

Question

Is there a mechanism available where we can secure the upfront capital and support a greater proportion of Downer’s innovations to progress from concept to reality.

Answer

The focus upon CO2 emissions reductions has lead to the establishment of a number of funding mechanisms designed to provide capex support for energy reduction initiatives.   It is proposed that Downer enlist the support of CBS Group to investigate the feasibility of these funding sources – making use of the Fleet Enhancement Program as the focus for the initial works, providing the basis for subsequent application of these mechanisms in other contexts.

Approach

We propose a 3 stage approach to the engagement as shown in the table below.

Nominated Persons and Terms of Engagement

CBS proposes that Jeff Dusting lead this work.   Jeff has prior experience with Fleet Enhancement Program and team, the applicable schemes, and considerable commercial acumen.  As this is a short-term, part-time engagement, a daily rate of $2200 plus GST applies, billable on a pro-rata basis using an 8 hour day. For ease of budgeting, we propose a capped fee for Stages 1 and 2 of $33,000 plus GST – based upon 15 days work effort.

All travel to/from Sydney wil be billed at cost, with a daily per diem in accordance with the applicable ATO schedule payable to cover accommodation, meals and incidental expenses.

The current CBS Group Services Agreement terms of engagement, including current 30 day payment terms will continue to apply to this proposal.

We look forward to the opportunity to progress this exciting initiative, and would welcome an opportunity to meet and clarify anything outlined in this proposal.
