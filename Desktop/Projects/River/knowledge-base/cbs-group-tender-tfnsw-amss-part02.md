Executive summary
Situation: Traditional models of consultant engagement leave all value risk with the client. Consultants are
paid for their time and deliverables regardless of whether their recommendations generate tangible benefits.
This creates a fundamental misalignment of incentives—the consultant's commercial success is
disconnected from the client's actual outcomes. Similarly, standard contract terms allocate risks to the
consultant that come at a cost yet provide no corresponding benefit to the client when those risks do not
materialise.
Complication: Value in complex infrastructure engagements is inherently uncertain. Even well-conceived
initiatives may not deliver anticipated benefits due to implementation challenges, changing circumstances or
unforeseen constraints. Under traditional arrangements, TfNSW bears this uncertainty entirely while
consultants face no consequence for recommendations that fail to materialise as value. Meanwhile, contract
terms that transfer risk to consultants simply result in higher fees to cover contingencies that may never
eventuate—cost without benefit.
Response: CBS Group proposes an alternate engagement model built on two complementary mechanisms.
1. Traditional Engagement on Collaborative Contract Terms: This mechanism provides for engagement of
   our core and supplementary teams on amended commercial terms. TfNSW acceptance of these terms
   allows a reduction in CBS Group costs and risk premium - enabling reduced fees. This creates a direct
   exchange: TfNSW accepts manageable risks in return for tangible cost savings
2. Value Based Price (VBP) Method: Value Based Pricing structures our compensation around shared risk
   and aligned returns, operating on three foundational principles: shared conviction (both parties agree
   there is genuine possibility of value before work commences), co-investment (CBS Group assigns
   appropriate specialists on a fixed retainer basis, with value share agreed initiative-by-initiative), and
   aligned returns (if value is delivered, we share in it; if not, our returns are limited accordingly).
We bring a suite of proven asset management products, methodologies and analytical tools that have
delivered more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years. It is with this
capability and track record that we can accept shared risk with confidence.
Mechanism 1 is detailed at section 5 of this Alternate Proposal. The remainder of this document has been
drafted to explain the value and operation of Mechanism 2 (VBP).
We look forward to working through the our seed list of initiatives and the necessary commercial
amendments to rapidly enable this model and start driving real value to support the Asset Financial
Sustainability (AFS) targets set for the organisation.

1.          Dual engagement pathway framework
1.1.        Framework structure
This alternative framework is built on two distinct and complementary engagement pathways, allowing
TfNSW to select the most suitable approach for any given initiative.
Method 1: Traditional, Time-Based Engagement
This pathway follows a conventional consultancy model. Services are provided at market rates for a defined
scope of work. This option is suitable for projects where the scope and deliverables are clearly defined from
the outset and where a traditional risk allocation is preferred. Services are provided at market rates for a
defined scope of work. Work orders do not include value share provisions.
Method 2: Shared-Risk, Value-Based Engagement
This pathway is founded on a partnership model. CBSE accepts a significantly reduced monthly retainer,
representing a substantial investment from our side. In return, we participate in the value we help create
through a pre-agreed sharing mechanism on validated benefits. This model ensures that both organisations
share in the risks and rewards, creating a strong alignment of interests.

1.2.        Selection Criteria
The choice between Option A and Option B would be guided by the nature of the project.
•    Method 1 is the preferred choice for more straightforward engagements. This includes defined-scope
     studies, technical investigations, or when seeking short-term expert advice. It is also appropriate for
     engagements where a more conservative, traditional risk allocation is desired
•    Method 2 is particularly well-suited for initiatives with the potential for significant value creation, such as
     long-term transformation programs or projects that require innovative solutions. It is most effective in
     situations where a deep, collaborative partnership and fully aligned incentives are critical to success.

1.3.        Flexibility and Combination
The framework is designed for flexibility, allowing TfNSW to:
•    Employ different options for different projects concurrently
•    Transition between the two options with an agreed-upon notice period
•    Combine both options within a single large-scale program
•    Initiate with a pilot of Option A before committing to a broader rollout

3.         The Value-Based Engagement Model
3.1.       Core Principles
The value-based engagement model fundamentally restructures the consultant-client relationship around
three principles that address the limitations of traditional arrangements:
Principle 1: Shared Conviction
Before any initiative commences, both TfNSW and CBS Group must agree that a genuine possibility of value
exists. This is not a formality—it requires both parties to review the proposed intervention, examine the
evidence base, and commit resources only where there is mutual confidence in the potential for measurable
improvement.
Principle 2: Co-Investment
CBS Group assigns a team of appropriate specialists to agreed initiatives on a retainer basis. The value target,
task duration and value share percentage are agreed on an initiative-by-initiative basis. CBS Group's initial
fees are set at 40-50% of market rates, representing our investment in the partnership. This reduced fee is not
a discount—it reflects our willingness to accept a lower certain return in exchange for participation in value
upside.
Principle 3: Aligned Returns
If value is delivered and independently validated, CBS Group shares in that value through benefit-sharing
payments. If value does not materialise, our returns are limited to the reduced retainer. This structure
ensures that CBS Group's commercial success depends directly on TfNSW's outcomes.

3.2.      Process Overview
The value-based pricing model operates as illustrated in the diagram and incorporating the following steps:
•   Client or CBS identifies a value-generating opportunity
•   CBS and TfNSW agree on scope, baseline costs, method of measurement (considering CATALYST 7™),
    start and end date, number of teams, and value share (with cap if required)
•   A Project Schedule is completed and executed by both parties
•   Project commences with monthly retainers for the agreed number of teams paid by TfNSW
•   Value share is paid once measurement outcomes are confirmed as documented in the Project Schedule




3.2.1.    Identification & Scoping
Either party may identify a potential value-generating opportunity. CBS Group will maintain an ongoing
pipeline of opportunities through its embedded team and strategic analysis.
For each identified opportunity, CBS Group and TfNSW will jointly:
•   Define the problem statement and anticipated outcome
•   Establish baseline costs or performance metrics
•   Agree the measurement methodology aligned with CATALYST 7™
•   Determine the number of teams and resource commitment required
•   Agree the start date, end date, and value measurement period
•   Negotiate the value share percentage and any applicable cap

3.2.2.    Agree Terms and Execute Schedule
The agreed terms will be documented in a Project Schedule, which forms a binding appendix to the Master
Agreement. Work on the initiative will not commence until both parties have executed the Project Schedule.

3.2.3.    Delivery
Upon execution of the Project Schedule:
•   Monthly retainers for the agreed number of teams commence
•   Baseline data is formally documented and certified

3.5.           Value Share Mechanism
Value share is calculated and paid on an initiative-by-initiative basis. For each value-generating opportunity,
CBS Group and TfNSW will execute a Project Schedule that documents the specific commercial terms
applicable to that initiative.
The value share payable for each initiative is calculated as follows:
Value Share = (Validated Benefits - Cumulative Retainer Paid) × Agreed Value Share Percentage
The value share is payable only after the measurement outcomes are confirmed through the validation
process documented in the Project Schedule.

3.7.1.           Validation Principles
The validation process is governed by the following principles to ensure both rigour and efficiency:
•     Transparency: All benefit calculations and supporting evidence are shared with TfNSW

•     Attribution: Only benefits demonstrably linked to CBS Group interventions are included
•     Timing: Benefits are recognised when realised, not when projected
•     Independence: ST Engineering provides objective assessment at all tiers
