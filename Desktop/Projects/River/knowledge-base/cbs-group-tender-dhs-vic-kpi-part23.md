INTRODUCTION

Department of Health

The Department of Health (the department) plays a critical role in the Victorian health system and is responsible for shaping it to meet the health needs of Victorians into the future. 

For further information refer to the department’s website https://www.health.vic.gov.au/about   

System Planning Division 

The System Planning Division provides an integrated and joined-up approach to Victorian health system planning, reform, and leadership to support delivery of strategic reform priorities across the Victorian health system. The division integrates the department’s approach to strategic and systemic long-term reform and includes integrated approaches to system and service planning as well as asset management. The division is focused on health system structural reform, intergovernmental relations, and action to address climate threats, and responses to priority challenges and projects as they emerge. 

Health System and Asset Planning Branch 

The Health System and Asset Planning branch within the System Planning Division brings together infrastructure Investment Strategy, Asset Planning, and the Health Infrastructure Client Control Office to support an integrated approach to asset management, system, service, and clinical planning. Together the branch is responsible for managing system infrastructure risks and undertaking capital investment planning on a portfolio level. Climate and land policy functions, ensure the branch integrates broader government directions and objectives into infrastructure planning. 

The function of the branch clearly delineates the role of the department as an infrastructure client and steward of the health infrastructure asset management system. Under this model: 

The Victorian Infrastructure Delivery Authority (VIDA) is responsible for delivering health infrastructure at the direction of the department to meet its current and future service needs, and 

Health service providers are the day-to-day operators and maintainers of assets and infrastructure. They are operational experts who are accountable for the asset’s reliability and availability to support the delivery of high-quality health outcomes.

Asset Planning Unit

The Asset Planning Unit is responsible for the asset management function within the department under a stewardship model, establishing and implementing strategic asset management capabilities across Victoria’s health sector. The unit is responsible for monitoring and facilitating compliance of the department and health agencies with the Asset Management Accountability Framework (AMAF). The Asset Planning unit provides guidance of sector practice and the setting of asset management standards. This includes policy and reform initiatives that have a strong focus on health infrastructure planning, data capability, risk management, and performance monitoring. The unit aims to equip Victorian health services with the skills and knowledge to optimise their asset lifecycle and enable delivery of quality care.

BACKGROUND

The Department of Health, as the steward of Victoria’s public health infrastructure, is committed to ensuring that all health facilities operate at optimal standards of safety, reliability, and efficiency. Across the state, 77 public health services manage a diverse portfolio of assets, each employing a range of engineering maintenance models—from fully in-house teams to fully outsourced service arrangements. This diversity, while reflecting local needs and capacities, presents challenges in achieving consistent asset performance and maintenance outcomes.

To address these challenges and drive sector-wide improvement, the Department has developed comprehensive Engineering Standard Maintenance Guidance. Central to this initiative is the implementation of 12 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), which have been carefully designed to:

Establish clear, measurable standards for asset maintenance across all Victorian public health services.

Enable consistent and transparent monitoring of maintenance performance, supporting benchmarking and continuous improvement.

Provide an evidence-based foundation for strategic asset planning and investment decisions ensuring resources (particularly capital funds) are directed to areas of the greatest impact.

Support compliance with best practice maintenance standards and inform actions that reduce risk and sustain the reliability and availability of critical health infrastructure., reducing risk and enhancing the reliability and availability of critical health infrastructure.

Tailor technical support and guidance to the unique needs of individual health services, fostering capability development and knowledge sharing across the sector.

By supporting health services to implement these KPIs, the Department enables informed, risk-based decision-making that strengthens operational and financial planning. This approach provides a consistent evidence base for prioritising maintenance and renewal, ensuring investment decisions deliver maximum value and align with government objectives for sustainability, resilience, and accountability.

PURPOSE

The purpose of this project is to engage a qualified and experienced contractor who will collaborate closely with relevant stakeholders. The contractor will work alongside stakeholders, utilising their existing systems and data capturing processes, to implement the defined Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) through a phased approach. This collaborative effort will ensure that the implementation is tailored to the operational realities of each stakeholder group, supports effective data-driven decision-making, and enables continuous improvement in engineering maintenance standards across the sector.

REQUIREMENTS AND DELVERABLES

The contractor will deliver a phased engagement, structured as follows:

Phase 1: Discovery & Assessment

Comprehensive Review: Undertake a systematic assessment of maintenance models, systems, and processes across pilot health services.

Stakeholder Mapping: The Department of Health (DH) will systematically identify and provide comprehensive details of all key stakeholders, including operational leads and asset managers, to enable targeted and effective engagement throughout the program lifecycle.

Readiness Evaluation: Assess organizational and operational readiness for KPI implementation, including data capability and resource availability.

Phase 2: Data Analysis & Benchmarking

Data Collection: Gather and validate maintenance data from participating health services.

Benchmarking: Analyse data to identify sector-wide strengths, weaknesses, and gaps; compare against best practice standards.

Maturity Segmentation: In collaboration with the Department of Health, health services will be systematically classified into distinct cohorts according to their asset management maturity and operational capability. Based on the data analysis, we will define the appropriate size of the pilot health services (HS) group and identify the targeted health services for participation in the pilot phase.

Phase 3: Engagement & Co-Design

Stakeholder Consultation: Facilitate workshops, interviews, and surveys to capture insights, challenges, and local innovations.

Collaborative Design: Co-develop tailored support options and action plans with stakeholders, ensuring alignment with operational realities.

Phase 4: Pilot Implementation

Pilot Planning: Design and execute a pilot phase with selected health services, including direct consultation and workshops.

Monitoring & Feedback: Collect pilot data, evaluate effectiveness, and document lessons learned.

Phase 5: Full Implementation & Continuous Improvement

Refinement: Incorporate pilot feedback to finalize implementation and action plans.

Change Management: Develop and deliver communication and engagement strategies to support sector-wide adoption.

Sustainability: Establish mechanisms for ongoing monitoring, evaluation, and continuous improvement.
