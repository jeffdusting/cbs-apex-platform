# WaterRoads Financial Model Summary

> Financial modelling parameters for the Rhodes to Barangaroo hydrofoil water transport service, extracted from WaterRoads business case and investor materials.

**Export date:** April 2026
**Source:** WaterRoads folder in CBS_RAG_Documents (Water-Roads.pdf, Deep Power Review, SJT presentations, Economic Impact Documentation)

---

## Key Financial Parameters

The following financial parameters have been extracted from the WaterRoads business case and investor materials.

### Return Metrics

The financial model demonstrates the following return on invested capital (ROIC) scenarios:

- **Without government support:** approximately 17% ROIC
- **With 30% government financial support:** approximately 27% ROIC
- Multiple exit paths identified including strategic buyouts, IPOs, and secondary sales for investor liquidity

### Capital Investment

- **Total investment:** approximately EUR 34M (compared to $8B Beaches Link motorway deferral)
- **Government funding leverage:** 30% support (EUR 3.2M to EUR 4.2M) yields 27% ROIC
- Three-phase rollout minimises risk while validating pilot

### Operating Economics

- **Operating cost advantage:** Electric Hydrofoil Vessels (EHVs) design delivers 85% lower operating costs than traditional ferries
- Supports frequent, unscheduled services, lowering fares and boosting margins
- Smaller vessel size enables easier docking and maintenance, reducing operational costs

### Revenue and Economic Impact

- **Job creation:** 30-50 direct jobs by 2026 (vessel operations, maintenance) in Sydney
- **Economic stimulus:** Creates 50-75 direct jobs and $10M-$20M annual contribution by 2028
- **Carbon credits:** $7K-$50K carbon credit revenue from 508-2,540 tonnes CO2 reduction by 2028
- **User time-savings benefits:** $3.1M-$8.3M per year across demand scenarios (3,000-8,000 daily boardings)
- **Carbon benefit:** $0.11M-$0.31M per year from car-to-ferry modal shift

### Demand Scenarios

Three daily ridership scenarios modelled (boardings per day, both directions):

| Scenario | Daily Boardings | Annual Time-Savings Benefit | Carbon Benefit |
|----------|----------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| Low | 3,000 | $3.1M | $0.11M |
| Medium | 5,000 | ~$5.5M | ~$0.20M |
| High | 8,000 | $8.3M | $0.31M |

Assumed previous mode split for new service users:

- 50% ex-train/bus
- 20% ex-F3 ferry (upgraded by frequency)
- 25% ex-car
- 5% induced demand

### Operating Concept

- **Headway and span:** Every 6 minutes for 16 hours/day (6am-10pm) = 160 one-way trips per direction/day (320 total)
- **In-vehicle time:** approximately 35 minutes one way (express or 1-2 intermediate stops)
- **Fleet requirement:** approximately 13 vessels in line service (12-15 depending on dwell, speed controls)
- **Vessel capacity:** 200-400 passenger range with dual-side, fast turnarounds

### ESG and Investor Positioning

- Favourable policies and zero emissions position WaterRoads to capture growing demand from ESG-focused investors
- Scalable business model with potential to package and scale into new international geographies
- Public-private partnership approach reduces perceived risk and enhances funding feasibility
