# WaterRoads PPP Structure Summary and Investor Materials

> Public-Private Partnership structure for the WaterRoads Rhodes to Barangaroo hydrofoil water transport service.

**Export date:** April 2026
**Source:** WaterRoads folder in CBS_RAG_Documents

---

## PPP Structure Overview

WaterRoads operates as a public-private partnership model delivering public benefits with private sector efficiency. The structure maintains public oversight while leveraging private sector expertise in electric hydrofoil vessel technology and operations.

### Partnership Model

The WaterRoads PPP is structured around the following principles:

- **Innovation partnership:** Delivering public benefits with private efficiency
- **Public oversight maintained:** Government retains regulatory and safety oversight
- **Private sector expertise leveraged:** Vessel technology, operations, and commercial management
- **Risk sharing:** Three-phase rollout minimises risk while validating the pilot service

### Government Engagement Framework

The government engagement strategy positions WaterRoads as addressing the following policy priorities:

- **Cost-effective alternative:** EUR 34M investment versus $8B Beaches Link motorway deferral, delivering immediate capacity without land acquisition costs
- **Funding leverage:** 30% government support (EUR 3.2M to EUR 4.2M) yields 27% ROIC — a high-return public-private partnership
- **Policy alignment:** Metro-style services enhance transport resilience and waterfront access, aligning with TfNSW goals
- **Sustainability targets:** Reduces pressure on roads and rail while advancing sustainability, justifying government backing

### Implementation Phases

The PPP implementation follows a three-phase approach aligned with political and investment priorities:

**Phase 1: Immediate Health and Environment Benefits (2025-2026)**

- Pilot service launch — Rhodes to Barangaroo
- Air quality monitoring demonstrating immediate improvements
- Health impact measurement quantifying community benefits

**Phase 2: Manufacturing and Jobs Focus (2026-2027)**

- Local manufacturing facility establishment in Western Sydney
- Job creation announcements supporting electoral commitments
- Skills development programmes in partnership with TAFE NSW

**Phase 3: Network Expansion (2027-2028)**

- Additional routes serving key electorates
- Tourist service development supporting Sydney's economy
- Export market development positioning NSW as innovation leader

### Investor Value Proposition

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Favourable market position** | Zero emissions positioning captures growing ESG-focused investor demand |
| **Attractive financial returns** | ~17% ROIC without government support; ~27% ROIC with 30% government financial support |
| **Flexible liquidity** | Multiple exit paths including strategic buyouts, IPOs, and secondary sales |
| **Scalable business model** | Potential to package and scale offering into new international geographies |

### Key Technology Partners

- **Candela P-12:** 30-passenger electric hydrofoil ferry — zero-emission technology
- **Artemis EF-12 Escape:** 12-passenger luxury water taxi — pursuing Class 1C certification for offshore routes

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| "Privatisation by stealth" concern | Public-private partnership model with maintained public oversight |
| "Expensive experiment" concern | Proven technology with quantified health and environmental benefits |
| "Limited capacity" concern | Scalable model — vessels added, removed, or rerouted as demand evolves |
| Technology risk | Three-phase rollout validates pilot before full commitment |
| Demand risk | Multiple demand scenarios modelled (3,000 to 8,000 daily boardings) |
