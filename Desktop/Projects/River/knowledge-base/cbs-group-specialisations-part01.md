ITS Australia Smart Transport Infrastructure Award Submission

CBS Group [and Transport for NSW] - Next-Generation Video-Based Tolling as a Service

Snapshot Summary

CBS Group [partnering with Transport for NSW] pioneered Australia's first video-based "Tolling as a Service" model for the Western Harbour Tunnel, eliminating physical toll infrastructure through AI-powered computer vision technology. This innovation delivers seamless customer experience, reduces government capital risk, and creates a sustainable foundation for next-generation road usage charging across Australia.

Core Submission Responses

Impact, Efficient & Safe: Measurable Benefits

CBS Group's innovation [delivered in partnership with Transport for NSW] achieves transformational efficiency and safety benefits through intelligent system design. The video-based approach eliminates traditional tolling complexity by removing physical tags and associated exception handling, achieving 100% automated transaction processing without roadside-backoffice rule conflicts. The dual-use of existing CCTV infrastructure for both tolling and tunnel safety creates targeted evacuation capabilities, enabling real-time identification of tunnel occupants rather than generic broadcast warnings. Revenue assurance is guaranteed through the performance-based commercial model where [Transport for NSW] pays only for successful vehicle identification and billing transactions, eliminating traditional revenue leakage from system failures or non-compliance.

Durable: Lasting Impact and Benefits

The solution's durability stems from its embedded continuous improvement mechanisms and simplified technical architecture. Under the fixed-fee Tolling as a Service model, suppliers retain cost savings from automation improvements, creating perpetual incentives for technological advancement while providing 30-year cost certainty for [Transport for NSW] through the CAPITAL framework. The elimination of complex roadside-backoffice integration points reduces long-term maintenance requirements and system obsolescence risk. This approach establishes a resilient foundation for Australia's transport funding evolution, moving beyond project-specific solutions to scalable infrastructure that adapts and improves over time while maintaining operational simplicity.

Transferable: Scalability and Adaptation Potential

[Transport for NSW] is actively pursuing opportunities to implement this model across all current and future toll roads, demonstrating immediate transferability within Australia's largest road network. The innovation's technical foundation using existing CCTV infrastructure makes it readily adaptable to any transport corridor with video monitoring capabilities, eliminating barriers to adoption. The commercial model's reduced capital requirements enable smaller jurisdictions to implement advanced tolling without traditional infrastructure investment constraints. Beyond tolling, this approach provides a scalable foundation for comprehensive road usage charging regimes, positioning Australia as a global leader in sustainable transport funding mechanisms through technology that can be deployed across bridges, urban congestion zones, and freight corridors.

Innovative: Clear Point of Difference

CBS Group [in collaboration with Transport for NSW] has delivered Australia's first video-only tolling system combined with a performance-based Tolling as a Service commercial model, representing dual innovation in both technology and business model design. The breakthrough lies in "designing out exceptions" - eliminating the complex tag-video matching processes and backoffice rule engines that plague traditional systems, creating unprecedented operational simplicity. The revolutionary integration repurposes existing ITS CCTV infrastructure traditionally used for traffic surveillance into a comprehensive tolling and safety solution. Advanced video analytics generate a real-time rolling list of all vehicles in the tunnel at any moment, and by linking this with customer account information, enables direct personalised communication with vehicle owners during emergencies. This represents a paradigm shift from legacy Public Address and Radio Rebroadcast systems to personalised device-based safety and evacuation systems, positioning Australia as a global leader in intelligent tunnel safety management through innovative technology convergence.

Sustainable: Measurable Environmental and Economic Benefits

The innovation delivers significant environmental benefits through infrastructure reduction, eliminating tolling-specific gantries by utilising existing structures and removing the electronic tag supply chain that generates substantial e-waste and ongoing logistics costs. The simplified system architecture reduces long-term material usage, maintenance requirements, and operational energy consumption compared to traditional tolling infrastructure. Economic sustainability is achieved through the CAPITAL framework's 30-year cost certainty model, which transforms [Transport for NSW's] tolling from capital-intensive infrastructure deployment to predictable operational expenditure. This approach creates a circular improvement economy where technological advancement benefits both supplier profitability and government cost reduction, establishing a self-sustaining model for continuous innovation in transport technology.
