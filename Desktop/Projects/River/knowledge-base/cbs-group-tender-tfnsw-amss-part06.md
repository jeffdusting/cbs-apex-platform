Executive summary
Situation: Traditional models of consultant engagement leave all value risk with the client. Consultants are
paid for their time and deliverables regardless of whether their recommendations generate tangible benefits.
This creates a fundamental misalignment of incentives—the consultant's commercial success is
disconnected from the client's actual outcomes. Similarly, standard contract terms allocate risks to the
consultant that come at a cost, yet provide no corresponding benefit to the client when those risks do not
materialise.
Complication: Value in complex infrastructure engagements is inherently uncertain. Even well-conceived
initiatives may not deliver anticipated benefits due to implementation challenges, changing circumstances or
unforeseen constraints. Under traditional arrangements, TfNSW bears this uncertainty entirely while
consultants face no consequence for recommendations that fail to materialise as value. Meanwhile, contract
terms that transfer risk to consultants simply result in higher fees to cover contingencies that may never
eventuate—cost without benefit.
Response: CBSE proposes an alternate engagement model comprising a flexible dual-pathway commercial
framework with three complementary mechanisms.
1. Method 1 - Traditional Time-Based Engagement provides access to the full depth of expertise available
   from CBS Group and Egis through conventional fee arrangements for engagements with clearly defined
   scope on Collaborative Contract Terms. It provides for engagement of our core and supplementary
   teams on amended commercial terms. TfNSW acceptance of these terms allows a reduction in CBSE
   costs and risk premium, enabling reduced fees. This creates a direct exchange: TfNSW accepts
   manageable risks in return for tangible cost savings.
2. Method 2 - Value Based Pricing structures our compensation around shared risk and aligned returns,
   operating on three foundational principles: shared conviction (both parties agree there is genuine
   possibility of value before work commences), co-investment (CBSE assigns appropriate specialists on a
   fixed retainer basis, with value share agreed initiative-by-initiative), and aligned returns (if value is
   delivered, we share in it; if not, our returns are limited accordingly).
We bring a suite of proven asset management products, methodologies and analytical tools that have
delivered more than $1 billion in validated savings for TfNSW over the past three years. Combined with the
global capabilities of Egis, it is with this capability and track record that we are able to accept shared risk with
confidence.
Mechanism 1 (Traditional Time-Based Engagement) and Mechanism 2 (Collaborative Contract Terms) are
detailed at Section 4 of this Alternate Proposal. The remainder of this document explains the value and
operation of Mechanism 3 (Value Based Pricing).

1.          Dual engagement pathway framework
1.1.        Framework structure
This alternative framework is built on two distinct and complementary engagement pathways, allowing
TfNSW to select the most suitable approach for any given initiative.
Method 1: Traditional, Time-Based Engagement
This pathway follows a conventional consultancy model. Services are provided at market rates for a defined
scope of work. This option is suitable for projects where the scope and deliverables are clearly defined from
the outset and where a traditional risk allocation is preferred. Services are provided at market rates for a
defined scope of work. Work orders do not include value share provisions.
Method 2: Shared-Risk, Value-Based Engagement
This pathway is founded on a partnership model. CBSE accepts a significantly reduced monthly retainer,
representing a substantial investment from our side. In return, we participate in the value we help create
through a pre-agreed sharing mechanism on validated benefits. This model ensures that both organisations
share in the risks and rewards, creating a strong alignment of interests.

1.2.        Selection Criteria
The choice between Method 1 and Method 2 would be guided by the nature of the project.
    Method 1 is the preferred choice for more straightforward engagements. This includes defined-scope
     studies, technical investigations, or when seeking short-term expert advice. It is also appropriate for
     engagements where a more conservative, traditional risk allocation is desired
    Method 2 is particularly well-suited for initiatives with the potential for significant value creation, such as
     long-term transformation programs or projects that require innovative solutions. It is most effective in
     situations where a deep, collaborative partnership and fully aligned incentives are critical to success.

1.3.        Flexibility and Combination
The framework is designed for flexibility, allowing TfNSW to:
    Employ different options for different projects concurrently
    Transition between the two options with an agreed-upon notice period
    Combine both options within a single large-scale program
    Initiate with a pilot of Method 1 before committing to a broader rollout

agencies. Under traditional fee-for-service arrangements, consultants are compensated for effort expended
rather than value delivered.

Infrastructure asset management initiatives involve inherent uncertainty. Even expertly designed
interventions may not achieve intended outcomes due to organisational readiness, competing priorities,
market conditions, or technical constraints that emerge during implementation. This uncertainty is not a
failure of planning—it is a reality of complex systems.
The question is not whether to accept this uncertainty, but how to allocate it fairly between client and
consultant. Under traditional models, TfNSW accepts all of this uncertainty while consultants face no
consequence for recommendations that fail to materialise as value.

2.2.         A Different Approach: Shared Risk, Aligned Returns
CBS Group proposes an alternative model structured around three foundational principles:

        value, duration and value share agreed initiative-by-initiative.
     3. Aligned returns: If value is delivered, we share in that value. If it is not, our returns are limited
        accordingly.

3.         The Value-Based Engagement Model
3.1.       Core principles
The value-based engagement model fundamentally restructures the consultant-client relationship around
three principles that address the limitations of traditional arrangements:

3.1.1.     Principle 1: Shared Conviction
Before any initiative commences, both TfNSW and CBSE JV must agree that a genuine possibility of value
exists. This is not a formality—it requires both parties to review the proposed intervention, examine the
evidence base, and commit resources only where there is mutual confidence in the potential for measurable
improvement.

3.1.2.     Principle 2: Co-Investment
 CBSE JV assigns a team of appropriate specialists to agreed initiatives on a retainer basis. The value target,
task duration and value share percentage are agreed on an initiative-by-initiative basis. CBSE JV's initial fees
are set at 40-50% of market rates, representing our investment in the partnership. This reduced fee is not a
discount—it reflects our willingness to accept a lower certain return in exchange for participation in value
upside.

3.1.3.     Principle 3: Aligned Returns
If value is delivered and independently validated, CBSE JV shares in that value through benefit-sharing
payments. If value does not materialise, our returns are limited to the reduced retainer. This structure
ensures that CBSE JV's commercial success depends directly on TfNSW's outcomes.

3.2.       Process overview
The value-based pricing model operates as illustrated in the diagram and incorporating the following steps:
    Client or CBSE identifies a value-generating opportunity
    CBSE and TfNSW agree on scope, baseline costs, method of measurement (considering CATALYST 7™),
     start and end date, number of teams, and value share (with cap if required)
    A Project Schedule is completed and executed by both parties
    Project commences with monthly retainers for the agreed number of teams paid by TfNSW
    Value share is paid once measurement outcomes are confirmed as documented in the Project Schedule
3.2.1.         Identification & Scoping
Either party may identify a potential value-generating opportunity. CBSE JV will maintain an ongoing pipeline
of opportunities through its embedded team and strategic analysis.
For each identified opportunity, CBSE JV and TfNSW will jointly:
      Define the problem statement and anticipated outcome
      Establish baseline costs or performance metrics
      Agree the measurement methodology aligned with CATALYST 7™1
      Determine the number of teams and resource commitment required
      Agree the start date, end date, and value measurement period
      Negotiate the value share percentage and any applicable cap

3.2.2.         Agree Terms and Execute Schedule
The agreed terms will be documented in a Project Schedule, which forms a binding appendix to the Master
Agreement. Work on the initiative will not commence until both parties have executed the Project Schedule.

3.2.3.         Delivery
Upon execution of the Project Schedule:
      Monthly retainers for the agreed number of teams commence
      Baseline data is formally documented and certified
      The project team is mobilised in accordance with the agreed composition

3.2.4.         Realise Value
Upon completion of the initiative:
      Value is measured in accordance with the agreed methodology
      Benefits are validated through the applicable validation tier
      Value share is calculated and paid as documented in the Project Schedule

3.3.           Project Schedule Requirements
Before work commences on any value-based initiative, both parties will agree and document the following in
a Project Schedule (Appendix A):
       1. Scope: The specific activities, deliverables and outcomes for the initiative
       2. Baseline: The current state costs or performance metrics against which value will be measured
       3. Measurement Method: The methodology for calculating value, aligned with the CATALYST 7™
          framework
       4. Duration: The start date, end date, and value measurement period
       5. Team Composition: The number and composition of teams assigned to the initiative
       6. Value Share: The percentage payable to CBSE JV, including any applicable cap

3.4.           Retainer Framework
The value-based model operates through initiative-specific retainers, with the number of teams determined
for each Project Schedule based on the scope and complexity of the work.

3.5.           Value Share Mechanism
Value share is calculated and paid on an initiative-by-initiative basis. For each value-generating opportunity,
CBSE JV and TfNSW will execute a Project Schedule that documents the specific commercial terms
applicable to that initiative.
The value share payable for each initiative is calculated as follows:
Value Share = (Validated Benefits - Cumulative Retainer Paid) × Agreed Value Share Percentage
The value share is payable only after the measurement outcomes are confirmed through the validation
process documented in the Project Schedule.

The commercial structure operationalises these principles through an integrated pricing arrangement:

    Payment frequency              Quarterly reconciliation based on validated achievements

This structure creates a partnership dynamic fundamentally different from traditional consulting. CBSE JV
has a direct financial interest in ensuring that initiatives succeed—not merely that deliverables are
completed.
3.7.             Validation Framework
3.7.1.           Validation Principles
The validation process is governed by the following principles to ensure both rigour and efficiency:
     Transparency: All benefit calculations and supporting evidence are shared with TfNSW
     Conservatism: Where uncertainty exists, benefit estimates err on the conservative side
     Attribution: Only benefits demonstrably linked to CBSE JV interventions are included
     Timing: Benefits are recognised when realised, not when projected
     Independence: ST Engineering provides objective assessment at all tiers

A robust validation framework is essential to the integrity of the value-based model. Both parties must have
confidence that claimed benefits are real, measurable and attributable to the engagement. We propose a

Appendix E: Catalyst7TM Measurement Framework
CBSE JV Principles for Using CATALYST 7™ and Applying Value-Based Pricing

Overview
CATALYST 7™ is CBS Group's proprietary framework for delivering and measuring value across infrastructure
advisory engagements. Developed through over a decade of application and more than $1 billion in
documented savings for Transport for NSW and other major infrastructure clients, CATALYST 7™ provides a
comprehensive methodology that moves beyond traditional time-and-materials consulting to create
strategic partnerships where compensation is directly tied to measurable value creation.
The framework recognises that infrastructure value extends far beyond financial returns to encompass seven
interconnected capital dimensions, enabling CBS Group to identify, quantify, and deliver holistic value that
benefits all stakeholders.

The Seven Capitals Framework
CATALYST 7™ measures and optimises value across seven interconnected capital dimensions:

 Capital                  Description
 Financial                Direct financial returns, economic impact, cost savings, revenue generation, and asset
                          value appreciation
 Manufactured             Physical assets, infrastructure systems, asset condition and performance, and lifecycle
                          optimisation
 Intellectual             Innovations, methodologies, proprietary IP, process improvements, and
                          organisational learning
 Human                    Workforce knowledge and skills, safety culture, organisational capabilities, and talent
                          development
 Social                   Relationships and networks, community benefit, stakeholder satisfaction, social
                          licence to operate, and accessibility
 Natural                  Environmental resources, sustainability performance, carbon emissions, biodiversity,
                          and circular economy principles
 Digital                  Data assets and insights, information systems, digital transformation capabilities, and
                          AI/advanced analytics

Value Multiplication Principles
CATALYST 7™ recognises that the seven capitals form an interconnected system where interventions in one
dimension create ripple effects across others. CBS Group actively designs interventions to maximise
synergistic effects through three mechanisms:
    1. Cross-Capital Synergies: Interventions in one capital dimension enhance value creation in another
       (e.g., Digital Capital investments enhance Manufactured Capital performance while improving
       Natural Capital outcomes).
    2. Cascading Value Effects: Value created in one dimension triggers sequential value creation in others
       through mapped pathways.
    3. Reinforcing Feedback Loops: Value creation across multiple dimensions establishes self-
       reinforcing cycles that compound over time.
Typical value multipliers range from 1.8x–2.7x for two-dimensional interactions, 2.5x–3.8x for three-
dimensional synergies, and 3.2x–4.7x for comprehensive multi-dimensional integration.
Value-Based Pricing Commercial Model
7.1.1.     Dual-Component Structure
CBS Group's VBP model comprises two components designed to balance budget predictability with strong
performance incentives:
   •     Strategic Partnership Retainer: Fixed monthly fee covering strategic advisory, methodology
         development, knowledge transfer, governance support, and continuous improvement activities.
   •     Value-Based Success Fees: Calculated as a percentage of independently validated value creation
         across the seven capitals, typically representing 60–80% of total CBS compensation in successful
         engagements.

7.1.2.     Measurement and Validation
CATALYST 7™ applies rigorous measurement protocols to ensure credible value creation claims:
   •     Baseline Rigor: Comprehensive baseline assessment incorporating 3–5 years of historical
         performance data, industry benchmarks, and explicit counterfactual analysis.
   •     Conservative Estimation: Lower-bound estimates, time-value discounting, and exclusion of weakly
         attributed value categories.
   •     Independent Validation: All material value claims (>$1M) undergo third-party validation by qualified
         assessors, with full methodology and data transparency.

7.1.3.     Performance Guarantees
CBS Group typically guarantees minimum value creation of 3x–5x total retainer fees over the engagement
period, demonstrating confidence in the CATALYST 7™ methodology. Remediation mechanisms include
success fee waivers, partial retainer refunds, and extended engagement commitments if guaranteed
minimums are not achieved.

7.2.       Perfect Incentive Alignment
CATALYST 7™ VBP fundamentally restructures the consultant-client relationship:
   1. Outcome-Based Compensation: CBS Group's compensation is calculated as a percentage of
      documented value creation, ensuring CBS earns more when clients achieve more value.
   2. Risk-Sharing: CBS Group bears significant performance risk—if interventions fail to create value,
      CBS receives only the base retainer without success fees.
   3. Long-Term Partnership: Engagements typically span 3–5 years with phased success fee payments
      tied to sustained value creation.
   4. Capability Transfer: Structured knowledge transfer builds client capabilities over time, enabling
      increasingly independent value creation.

7.3.       Summary
CATALYST 7™ represents a fundamental shift from traditional consulting to genuine strategic partnership. By
measuring and optimising value across seven interconnected capitals, applying rigorous baseline and
validation protocols, and structuring commercial arrangements around shared risk and reward, CBS Group
delivers transformative outcomes where consultant success depends entirely on delivering measurable,
sustainable client value.
