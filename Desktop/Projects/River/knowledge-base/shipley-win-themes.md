# Shipley Win Theme Development

## Purpose

Win themes are the 3-5 key messages that differentiate your offer and are threaded consistently throughout the entire proposal. They are the answer to "why should the client choose us?"

## Win Theme Strategy — Four Quadrants

Every competitive strategy falls into one of four quadrants:

### 1. EMPHASISE Our Strength

"We will **emphasise** our perceived **strength** in [issue] as a result of [discriminator] by..."

Use when CBS has a clear, evidenced advantage the client values.

**Example:** "We will emphasise our strength in whole-of-life cost modelling as a result of the CAPITAL framework's $180M validated savings on WHT by quantifying projected savings for the client's asset portfolio."

### 2. MITIGATE Our Weakness

"We will **mitigate** our perceived **weakness** in [issue] as a result of [discriminator] by..."

Use when CBS has a gap the client may notice. Address it proactively.

**Example:** "We will mitigate our perceived weakness in large-scale rail delivery as a result of our specialist advisory focus by partnering with [rail delivery partner] and emphasising our systems engineering assurance capability."

### 3. HIGHLIGHT Competitor Weakness

"We will **highlight** our competitor's perceived **weakness** in [issue] as a result of [discriminator] by..."

Use to draw attention to a competitor gap without naming them directly.

**Example:** "We will highlight competitors' weakness in commercial outcome delivery by contrasting generic asset management advisory with CAPITAL's quantified outcome guarantees and performance-linked fee structure."

### 4. DOWNPLAY Competitor Strength

"We will **downplay** our competitor's perceived **strength** in [issue] as a result of [discriminator] by..."

Use when a competitor has an advantage you cannot match. Reframe the discussion.

**Example:** "We will downplay competitors' strength in scale and resource depth by emphasising that specialist, named personnel with direct project experience deliver better outcomes than rotating generalist teams."

## Win Theme Development Process

### Step 1: Identify Client Hot Buttons

Client hot buttons are the issues that will most influence the procurement decision. Sources:
- Evaluation criteria and weightings from the RFP
- Client engagement history (from KB)
- Industry context and current challenges
- Conversations and correspondence (verified at kick-off)

### Step 2: Map Solutions to Hot Buttons

For each hot button, define how CBS's solution addresses it. State as **benefit first, then features**.

Wrong: "CBS uses the CAPITAL framework which has 7 core capabilities..."
Right: "CBS delivers a minimum 3-5× return on advisory fees through the CAPITAL framework, proven across a $10B asset portfolio."

### Step 3: Distil Win Themes

Each win theme should be a **6-8 word phrase or sentence** that encapsulates the competitive message.

| Client Hot Button | Our Solution / Benefits | Win Theme |
|---|---|---|
| Reduce lifecycle costs | CAPITAL delivers $180M+ validated savings | Proven outcomes, not just advisory |
| Named expert team | David Harper, Jim Ellwood with direct project experience | Your named team from day one |
| Value for money | Performance-linked fees aligned to client outcomes | We only succeed when you succeed |
| Innovation | CAPITAL integrates commercial + technical advisory | Beyond engineering — commercial outcomes |

### Step 4: Thread Through Document

Every major section of the tender response must reference at least one win theme. The Tender Coordination agent should verify win theme threading during Silver and Gold quality reviews.

| Section | Primary Win Theme | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Executive Summary | All themes (summary form) | — |
| Methodology | Proven outcomes | Beyond engineering |
| Past Experience | Your named team | Proven outcomes |
| Personnel | Your named team | — |
| Commercial | We only succeed when you succeed | Proven outcomes |
| Compliance | [Maps to specific criteria] | — |

## Application to CBS Agents

- **Research CBS:** Develops win themes during Bronze phase competitor assessment
- **Technical Writing:** Threads win themes through methodology and experience sections
- **Pricing and Commercial:** Aligns commercial positioning to win themes
- **Tender Coordination:** Verifies consistent threading at Silver and Gold reviews
- **CBS Executive:** Reviews win theme strategy at Bronze gate
