CBS Group | Personnel Capability Statement


Nicole Caranguian
Change Manager, Digital Systems & Analytics lead, and Health FM Advisor
Qualifications: University of New England (AU), 2017–2020

Professional Profile
Nicole is a senior technology executive and digital transformation leader with over 20 years of experience in
Defence, national security and government ICT. She brings extensive expertise in digital and business
transformation, data governance, cyber security assurance, AI strategy and large-scale program delivery
across Commonwealth and state government agencies.
Nicole’s career spans a decade of distinguished service in the Royal Australian Navy as a Communications
Information Systems specialist, followed by progressive leadership roles at Fujitsu, the Department of
Defence (CIOG), Downer Group and xDNA, culminating in her current dual role as Technology Consultant
with CBS Group and Director of Applications and Integration at Defence Housing Australia. She also
founded the Caranguian Group, a specialist technology consultancy.
Nicole’s strength lies in her ability to translate complex technology and data challenges into structured
roadmaps that deliver measurable business outcomes. Her experience developing technology strategies,
governance frameworks, business cases and operating models for government agencies is directly relevant
to the data collection, validation, benchmarking and digital platform requirements of this engagement. She
brings a rigorous, security-conscious approach to data handling, with direct experience in PSPF, SOCI and
government information security frameworks, and has published on the NSW AI Assessment Framework
covering privacy, security, transparency and accountability in government operations.

Role on This Engagement
As a Change Manager, Digital Systems & Analytics lead, and Health FM Advisor, Nicole will be responsible
for the data collection, validation, cleansing and analysis activities across Phases 1 and 2, and will provide
ongoing data governance and quality assurance throughout the engagement. She will also advise on digital
platforms and tools to support KPI monitoring, reporting and AI-enabled analytics.
Key responsibilities on this project include:
   • Designing and managing the data collection framework, including self-assessment questionnaires
       and data extraction processes across health services
   • Leading the Phase 2 data validation, cleansing and analysis activities to ensure accuracy and
       comparability across the 77 public health services
   • Developing data governance protocols to ensure the integrity and security of departmental
       information throughout the engagement, aligned with the ISSRA and Standard 8b requirements
   • Advising on digital platforms and tools for KPI dashboards, performance monitoring and reporting,
       including assessment of AI-enabled analytics opportunities
   • Supporting the maturity assessment process with data analytics and benchmarking insights to inform
       health service segmentation
   • Ensuring compliance with the Department’s information security requirements, drawing on direct
       experience with PSPF, SOCI and government cyber security frameworks
