Our understanding of Services

The State Tolling Corporation (STC) has proposed to develop an end-to-end solution for the Toll Collection Capability (TCC) that incorporates the delivery and operation of a Roadside System (RSS) and the delivery, support, and maintenance of a Back-Office system (BOS).

Program Services

Egis will submit an Independent Verifier (IV) Plan within 20 business days of commencement. This plan will be agreed with STC and will detail:

Skills, expertise and experience of the resources and the estimated resource levels required for each component of the Services,

Progress reports to be provided to the Project Control Group (PCG), and

A verification audit program to monitor performance of the Contractors’ obligations under the TCC.

Requirements Phase Services

Egis will provide STC with an independent, expert opinion on whether the specified technical and business requirements— including the Scope of Works and Technical Requirements (SWTRs)— have been accurately translated into robust, verifiable requirements.  

We will ensure that TCC Contractors implement strong processes to capture specialist engineering requirements, such as safety, human factors, Reliability, Availability and Maintainability (RAM), and cyber security. These requirements will complement the SWTRs and are typically gathered through workshops with subject matter experts from the operational community.  

All technical requirements must be captured in a centralised repository that serves as the single source of truth. If the project uses a Requirements Management Database (RMDB) such as IBM DOORS, we can provide specialist resources to set up the RMDB and develop templates and tools for tracking and managing requirements. Additionally, we can offer training for both STC and TCC Contractor staff on how to use these tools effectively.  

We will review the Requirements Verification Traceability Matrices (RVTMs) to ensure that verification evidence is captured at each stage of the project lifecycle. This will provide early assurance that all requirements have been adequately verified and highlight any gaps or 'orphans' that need to be addressed.

Design Phase Services

We will conduct document reviews for each of the TCC Contract design deliverables in accordance with the Project Delivery Schedule and will provide structured feedback for items that do not meet the specified requirements. 

Egis will also advise STC when each deliverable is suitable for endorsement or acceptance under the terms of the TCC Contracts.

Test Phase Services

We will provide specialist staff to witness specified tests and demonstrations, ensuring that the requirements needing verification through these methods are satisfactorily met.  

Additionally, we will provide ongoing updates on the maturity of the RVTMs and offer advice on when the Project RVTM is complete, accurate, and contains the necessary evidence to confirm that the requirements within the TCC Contracts have been fulfilled.

Implementation and Operational Readiness Phase Services

Operational readiness is typically the responsibility of the Project Integrator, working with the Operator. Egis will tap into our experience in previous projects to support the operational readiness planning and ensure that the transition from Project to Operations happens seamlessly.

Our approach to test witnessing 

Egis conducts all test witnessing activities with a clearly defined set of objectives, which include; 

the verification of test results, 

assurance of compliance, 

ensuring documentation traceability, and 

identification & mitigation of risks. 

We conduct thorough technical reviews of test plans, schedules, and performance or functional requirements. These reviews help identify critical test cases, potential compliance issues, and operational deficiencies, enabling us to provide constructive feedback. We will develop a test witness schedule focused on these critical test cases to ensure that suitably experienced technical witnesses are present as needed.

Test witnessing observations are systematically collected and categorised according to the established objectives, then communicated concisely to the project. Responses, resulting controls, and any changes to test plans will be tracked to ensure risk mitigation and closure of observations.

Egis coordinates closely with the project team to ensure comments and observations are directed to the relevant forums and escalation levels. Our approach is adaptable, and we will tailor the process to best support the project by adding or removing scope based on stakeholder requirements, or adjusting outputs to align with existing project systems

Approach to GORC and its application 

Egis will implement a structured and proactive approach to General Overview and Reasonable Checking (GORC) throughout all stages of the project, ensuring full compliance with the technical, assurance, and contractual requirements set out in the TCC Contracts. The IV team will be led by a dedicated management team, with experience applying GORC practices on major Victorian transport projects such as the High Capacity Metro Trains (HCMT) and X’Trapolis 2 (XT2) Technical advisor roles, ensuring that personnel have the requisite expertise and accreditation to fulfil obligations. Egis’ approach is to provide continuous oversight, identify potential issues early, and resolve them promptly, minimising risks and ensuring the successful deployment of the tolling system. 

The IV Plan, along with supporting procedures, will be developed and submitted within 20 days of commencement to outline methodologies for meeting key performance and traceability requirements. Project risks will be organised by discipline, with risk ratings providing guidance for resourcing efforts. This approach will help prioritize key aspects of the project and determine the necessary actions as outlined for the QTMP IC project in Section 8, "Risk Management," of this proposal. These ratings will be reviewed and adjusted as the project evolves by the IC management team in consultation with STC representatives. A Completion Plan will be developed in line with the contractor’s documents which outlines the information required for the IV to make progressive assessments of each Critical Interface Milestone. 

Regular progress reports, governance meetings and audits will inform decision-making and enable prompt resolution of issues. Close communication with the STC and all stakeholders along with updates to the Services Delivery Management Plan on a 6 monthly basis will ensure alignment and facilitate early intervention when necessary.

Management Plans, Requirements, and Design Documents

During the requirements and design review stage of the project, Egis will review the TCC Contractors’ management plans, including technical specifications, quality assurance strategies, risk management plans and schedules. This will ensure that these plans align with the TCC Contracts and provide a realistic foundation for successful project delivery. 

We will assess the consistency of these documents with broader NEL tollway objectives, ensuring that they comply with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements. Specialist design reviewers will evaluate compliance with the project requirements and relevant reference documents and tap into Egis’ international expertise as required.

Testing 

As the project progresses to the testing phase, Egis will ensure that test plans comprehensively cover all technical and functional requirements outlined in the TCC Contracts. Our testing and commissioning experts will witness tests and evaluate results to confirm the system performs as expected and meets relevant standards, ensuring the tolling system meets performance criteria before operational deployment.  

IV Testing Reviewers will assess the implementation of these plans, review the Contractors' records for completeness, and ensure the Project RVTM is complete, accurate, and contains the necessary evidence to verify that the requirements within the TCC Contracts have been met.

Operational Readiness 

During the operational readiness phase, Egis will ensure that systems, processes, and personnel are fully prepared for tolling operations by assessing the compliance of the TCC Contractors to their management plans and the project’s contractual and operational requirements and ensuring that activities such as user training, support systems and maintenance protocols are in place.

Risk Management

Please provide sufficient evidence and experience from similar projects to demonstrate an understanding of the risks, issues and opportunities that commonly arise and how the identified risks were managed:

Egis Oceania has extensive experience managing commercial and project risk for our public and private sector clients. Egis Oceania's capabilities include the identification and quantification of infrastructure project related risks including design, construction and commissioning risk; operational risk; market risk; network and interface risk; industrial relations risk; and asset ownership risk (including residual risk). We are also familiar with the mechanisms of risk transfer and the role of risk allocation and value-for-money in tender evaluations. The generic structure of our risk management processes are aligned with AS/NZS 31000 2009: Risk Management.  At appropriate stages in complex projects, we facilitate risk management workshops (using an established methodology, an agreed risk register template and an agreed scale of likelihoods, impacts and consequences). Risk Management workshops will focus on areas of risk relevant to the particular project including: commercial risk, service delivery risk, reputational risk, project timing, technical quality and stakeholder relationships. For each new engagement, following the project inception meeting Egis will facilitate a risk management workshop with the relevant Project Stakeholders to establish the initial risk register and rate the risks using the scale of likelihoods, impacts and consequences, and with risk owners identified and with defined risk mitigation strategies.  

Identified risks will be assessed as to their priority to be managed. 

Egis have successfully delivered similar services, such as QTMP IC, we did this with our tried-and-true Risk Based Approach (RBA). As we are on QTMP, Egis will undertake a high-level assessment to rank identified risks associated with each Project and Design requirement in terms of consequence and likelihood. This will determine the level and type of review needed for each requirement and support a robust and repeatable evidence-based assessment across all requirements. Requirements defined as medium and high risk (see figure x) will be subject to a more intensive level of compliance assessment. This rating process is critical to form the basis for the design reviews, certification of design packages and identification/resolution of Potential, Actual and Minor Non-Compliances. 

If the Key Stakeholders disagree with the level of risk associated with a design requirement and the level of compliance assessment effort to be applied, our IV team will work collaboratively with those organisations to align/agree the risk allocation. If the level of risk associated with any requirement is unclear, a more conservative view will be taken i.e. a higher risk ranking will be assigned, and our team will apply the commensurate level of effort in our review and assessment. 

Egis will assess and rate the risks associated with each design package to determine the level of rigour we will apply in the certification process for each package and in monitoring controls that are applied. This supports an agile and efficient approach to compliance assessment, targeting allocation of our resources to ensure the level of effort and type of assessment is proportionate to the risk associated with a particular design package or deliverable, in achieving the objectives of the Project.

We actively prioritise engaging with Indigenous and Torres Strait Islander businesses, particularly in indirect expenditure categories such as office supplies, catering, cleaning, training, and printing. This approach not only contributes to the economic success of Indigenous communities but also creates long-term social benefits.

We:

Prioritise purchasing from Indigenous enterprises where possible, including sourcing PPE from SupplyAus Holdings, stationary from Gulbari, and publishing/printing services from Iscariot Media.

Partner with organisations like Deadly Coders and offer Cultural Awareness Training delivered by First Nations trainers.

Work with Supply Nation to procure office supplies and IT equipment from verified Indigenous-owned businesses.

These initiatives align with our broader commitment to our Reconciliation Action Plan (RAP), fostering relationships with First Nations communities and businesses. By continuing to engage with Indigenous suppliers, we support their growth and provide meaningful opportunities for economic participation. Should we be successful in the North East Link project, we will further expand our engagement with local First Nations suppliers, further strengthening the Indigenous business community in Victoria.

Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Strategy and Gender Inclusive Policy

Egis is committed to fostering a diverse and inclusive workplace. Our DEI strategy, focused on First Nations, Disability, Gender, Rainbow, and Culture, aims to create an environment where all employees can thrive.

Our gender inclusivity initiatives include:

Aiming to increase female representation across the workforce from 31.3% to 33% and leadership from 25.2% to 27% by 2025.

Addressing the gender pay gap and promoting career development for women.

Supporting families through a strong parental leave policy that provides flexibility.

We actively implement DEI initiatives through our employee-led DEI Network, which drives events such as International Women’s Day and NAIDOC Week. Egis is also compliant with the Australian Workplace Gender Equality Act, and we regularly monitor our progress towards closing gender gaps and promoting diversity at all levels. Our efforts ensure an inclusive workplace that values equality, creating opportunities for all employees to succeed and contribute to a high-performance culture.
