6 COMPLIANCE WITH REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Our Understanding of Services
The State Tolling Corporation (STC) has proposed to develop an end-to-end solution for the Toll Collection
Capability (TCC) that incorporates the delivery and operation of a Roadside System (RSS) and the delivery,
support, and maintenance of a Back-Office system (BOS).




FIGURE 1 - PHASES

PROGRAM SERVICES
Egis will submit an Independent Verifier (IV) Plan (please see draft in Attachment 2) within 20 business days
of commencement. This plan will be agreed with STC and will detail:

▬ Skills, expertise and experience of the resources and the estimated resource levels required,
▬ Progress reports to be provided to the Project Control Group (PCG), and
▬ A verification audit program to monitor performance of the Contractors’ obligations under the TCC.

REQUIREMENTS PHASE SERVICES
We will provide STC with an independent, expert opinion on whether the specified technical and business
requirements— including the Scope of Works and Technical Requirements (SWTRs)— have been accurately
translated into robust, verifiable requirements.
We will ensure that TCC Contractors implement strong processes to capture specialist engineering
requirements, such as safety, human factors, Reliability, Availability and Maintainability (RAM), and cyber
security. These requirements will complement the SWTRs and are typically gathered through workshops with
subject matter experts from the operational community.
All technical requirements must be captured in a centralised repository that serves as the single source of
truth and align with the International Council on Systems Engineering (INCOSE) standards. If the project uses
a Requirements Management Database (RMDB) such as IBM DOORS, we can provide specialist resources to
set up the RMDB and develop templates and tools for tracking and managing requirements. Additionally, we
can offer training for both STC and TCC Contractor staff on how to use these tools effectively.
We will review the Requirements Verification Traceability Matrices (RVTMs) to ensure that verification
evidence is captured at each stage of the project lifecycle. This will provide early assurance that all
requirements have been adequately verified and highlight any gaps or 'orphans' that need to be addressed.

6.2 Our Approach to Test Witnessing
Egis conducts all test witnessing activities with a clearly defined set of objectives, which includes:

▬ The verification of test results,
▬ Assurance of compliance,
▬ Ensuring documentation traceability, and
▬ Identification & mitigation of risks.
We conduct thorough technical reviews of test plans, schedules, and performance or functional
requirements. These reviews help identify critical test cases, potential compliance issues, and operational
deficiencies, enabling us to provide constructive feedback. We will develop a test witness schedule focused
on these critical test cases to ensure that suitably experienced technical witnesses are present as needed.
Test witnessing observations are systematically collected and categorised according to the established
objectives, then communicated concisely to the project. Responses, resulting controls, and any changes to
test plans will be tracked to ensure risk mitigation (more on risk can be found in Section 8) and closure of
observations.
Egis will coordinate closely with the project team to ensure comments and observations are directed to the
relevant forums and escalation levels. Our approach is adaptable, and we will tailor the process to best
support the project by adding or removing scope based on stakeholder requirements, or adjusting outputs
to align with existing project systems


6.3 Approach to GORC and its Application
Egis will implement a structured and proactive approach to General Overview and Reasonable Checking
(GORC) throughout all stages of the project, ensuring full compliance with the technical, assurance, and
contractual requirements set out in the TCC Contracts. The IV team will be led by a dedicated management

continuous oversight, identify potential issues early, and resolve them promptly, minimizing risks and
ensuring the successful deployment of the tolling system.
The IV Plan, along with supporting procedures, will outline methodologies for meeting key performance and

A Completion Plan will be developed in line with the Contractor’s documents which outlines the information
required for the IV to make progressive assessments of each Critical Interface Milestone. Regular progress
reports, governance meetings and audits will inform decision-making and enable prompt resolution of
issues. Close communication with the STC and all stakeholders along with updates to the Services Delivery
Management Plan on a 6 monthly basis will ensure alignment and facilitate early intervention when
necessary.

MANAGEMENT PLANS, REQUIREMENTS, AND DESIGN DOCUMENTS
During the requirements and design review stage of the project, Egis will review the TCC Contractors’
management plans, including technical specifications, quality assurance strategies, risk management plans
and schedules. This will ensure that these plans align with the TCC Contracts and provide a realistic
foundation for successful project delivery.
We will assess the consistency of these documents with broader NEL tollway objectives, ensuring that they
comply with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements, tapping into Egis’ international expertise as
required.

TESTING
As the project progresses to the testing phase, Egis will ensure that test plans comprehensively cover all
technical and functional requirements outlined in the TCC Contracts. Our testing and commissioning experts
will witness tests and evaluate results to confirm the system performs as expected and meets relevant
standards, ensuring the tolling system meets performance criteria before operational deployment.
IV Testing Reviewers will assess the implementation of these plans, review the Contractors' records for
completeness, and ensure the Project RVTM is complete, accurate, and contains the necessary evidence to
verify that the requirements within the TCC Contracts have been met.

OPERATIONAL READINESS
During the operational readiness phase, Egis will ensure that systems, processes, and personnel are fully
prepared for tolling operations by assessing the compliance of the TCC Contractors to their management
plans and the project’s contractual and operational requirements and ensuring that activities such as user
training, support systems and maintenance protocols are in place.
