PREAMBLE

The undersigned, being all of the shareholders and directors of CobaltBlu Pty Ltd ACN 103 595 071 (CobaltBlu or the Company), pass the following resolutions in writing pursuant to:

Section 249A of the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) (the Act) — shareholder resolutions passed without a meeting; and

Section 248A of the Act — director resolutions passed without a meeting.

These resolutions are effective when signed by all shareholders and all directors, and have the same effect as if passed at duly convened meetings of shareholders and directors respectively.

BACKGROUND AND CONTEXT

A. Current Shareholding and Investment

CobaltBlu holds 1,000 FOU/Founder's shares in WaterRoads Pty Ltd ACN 686 900 014 (WaterRoads or Portfolio Company), representing 90.91% of WaterRoads' issued share capital as at the date of these resolutions.

CobaltBlu's investment in WaterRoads represents the principal asset of CobaltBlu. The two directors and shareholders of CobaltBlu (Sarah Margaret Taylor and Jeffrey Alan Dusting) also serve as directors of WaterRoads.

B. The Recapitalisation

WaterRoads proposes to implement a recapitalisation (the Recapitalisation) to correct historic defects in its capital structure and align the share register with the agreed ownership structure between CobaltBlu and CBS Group Pty Ltd ACN 164 870 080 (CBS Group).

The Recapitalisation is documented in:

(a) Recapitalisation Deed dated 15 January 2026 between WaterRoads, CobaltBlu, and CBS Group (the Recap Deed);

(b) CBS Consent and Waiver Deed dated [●] January 2026, to which CobaltBlu is or will become a party;

(c) CBS Loan Variation and Waiver Deed between WaterRoads and CBS Group (waiving loan covenants, subordinating the CBS Loan, and committing to full discharge at Series A close); and

(c) Amended and Restated Constitution of WaterRoads — Version 3.1 adopted by special resolution of WaterRoads shareholders effective 15 January 2026 (the New Constitution).

C. Recapitalisation Mechanics

The Recapitalisation involves the following sequential steps (collectively, the Recap Steps):

(i) the cancellation of all 1,100 Existing Shares (1,000 FOU/Founder’s shares held by CobaltBlu and 100 Class B shares held by CBS Group);

(ii) the issue of 100,000 new shares (96,000 F Shares to CobaltBlu and 4,000 Common Shares to CBS Group);

(iii) a 10-for-1 share split, increasing total share count to 1,000,000 (960,000 F Shares and 40,000 Common Shares); and

(iv) the adoption of the New Constitution (V3.1, effective 15 January 2026).

Agreed Ownership means the ownership structure agreed between CobaltBlu and CBS Group as documented in the Recap Deed, being 96.00% CobaltBlu and 4.00% CBS Group, and reflects the correction of Historic Defects (as defined in the Recap Deed).

D. Post-Recapitalisation Position

Following completion of the Recap Steps, CobaltBlu will hold:

960,000 F Shares in WaterRoads

Representing 96.00% of WaterRoads' issued share capital

Ranking in accordance with the New Constitution

E. Series A Financing and F Share Conversion

WaterRoads intends to pursue a Series A equity financing following the Recapitalisation. Key implications for CobaltBlu:

(a) Dilution: CobaltBlu's percentage shareholding will be diluted by the Series A issuance (expected quantum and terms to be determined by WaterRoads board in accordance with fiduciary duties and governance framework).

(b) F Share Conversion: Upon completion of a Qualifying Financing (as defined in clause 3.1 of the New Constitution — broadly, a bona fide equity financing of at least A$[●] on arms-length terms), CobaltBlu's F Shares will automatically convert into Common Shares on a 1:1 basis (clause 4.4 of the New Constitution).

(c) Founder Protections: While CobaltBlu (together with any other F Share holders, the F Share Majority) holds ≥30% of WaterRoads shares on a Fully Diluted Basis, certain Founder Protective Provisions apply (clause 20 of the New Constitution), requiring F Share Majority consent for specified major decisions.

(d) Board Representation: While the F Share Majority holds ≥20% on a Fully Diluted Basis, the F Share Majority has the right to appoint one Founder Director to the WaterRoads board (clause 16.2 of the New Constitution).

(e) Shareholders Agreement: Series A investors will require entry into a Shareholders Agreement, which may include further governance, consent, and exit provisions affecting CobaltBlu's rights as a shareholder.

F. CBS Consent and Waiver Deed

CBS Group (as WaterRoads' minority shareholder and secured lender) has consented to the Recapitalisation and waived certain rights. CobaltBlu is required to execute, or has been asked to be a party to, the CBS Consent and Waiver Deed to:

(a) acknowledge CBS Group's consent and the binding effect of the Recapitalisation;

(b) provide certain confirmations regarding the Agreed Ownership and Recap Steps; and

(c) undertake to comply with the governance framework set out in the New Constitution.

G. Governance Context

CobaltBlu is a proprietary company with two directors and two shareholders, each holding 50%:

Sarah Margaret Taylor — Director, Company Secretary, 50% shareholder

Jeffrey Alan Dusting — Director, 50% shareholder

Both are also directors of WaterRoads. Jeff is also a director of CBS Group. Jeffrey Alan Dusting abstained from all CBS votes on the Recapitalisation at the CBS board meeting of 16 January 2026, with Jim Ellwood (independent CBS director) authorised to execute all CBS-side documents.

CobaltBlu has adopted a Conflict of Interest — Board Policy (Version 6.0 – Institutional) which governs the management of conflicts arising from WaterRoads and CBS Group matters. The resolutions in this document are passed in accordance with that policy.

Nature of Conflict

Both directors have material personal interests in the resolutions below by virtue of:

(i) their roles as directors and 50% shareholders of CobaltBlu (the entity making the decisions);

(ii) their roles as directors of WaterRoads (the Portfolio Company subject to the Recapitalisation);

(iv) Jeff's additional role as a director of CBS Group (the counterparty to the Recap Deed and CBS Consent Deed); and

(v) their spousal relationship, which creates indirect interests in each other's positions.

Statutory Basis for Voting

CobaltBlu is a proprietary company with only two directors, both of whom are interested in these matters.

Section 194 of the Act permits directors of a proprietary company to vote on, and be present at a meeting to consider, a matter in which they have a material personal interest, provided:

the interest has been disclosed to the other directors in accordance with s191 or s192; and

the company is not a public company.

Reliance on s194: The directors resolve to vote on these resolutions in reliance on section 194, having made full disclosure as set out above.

Standing Notices

WaterRoads (directorship, shareholding via CobaltBlu)

CBS Group (Jeff's directorship)

Those Standing Notices remain in effect and cover all matters addressed in these resolutions.

Commercial Rationale

The directors are satisfied that, notwithstanding their conflicts:

(a) the Recapitalisation is in CobaltBlu's interests because it:

corrects Historic Defects in WaterRoads' capital structure;

aligns the share register with the Agreed Ownership of 96% CobaltBlu / 4% CBS;

facilitates WaterRoads' Series A financing, which is necessary for WaterRoads' business growth and the protection and enhancement of CobaltBlu's investment;

preserves CobaltBlu's majority position and Founder Protections under the New Constitution;

(b) the terms of the Recapitalisation (including the share-for-share exchange, nil cash consideration, and adoption of the New Constitution) are fair and reasonable to CobaltBlu;

(c) the Series A financing (while dilutive in percentage terms) is necessary for WaterRoads to execute its business plan and represents the most commercially viable path to value creation for CobaltBlu as a shareholder;

(d) CobaltBlu's participation in the CBS Consent Deed is necessary to implement the Recapitalisation and does not involve CobaltBlu giving up material rights or assuming inappropriate obligations; and

(e) the decision-making process (including reliance on s194 with full disclosure, documentation of rationale, and compliance with CobaltBlu's Conflict Policy) is appropriate given CobaltBlu's current board composition and the impracticability of obtaining a disinterested director approval.

Independent Director Appointment (Future)

The directors note that WaterRoads has committed to appoint an independent director at or within 60 days of Series A close. Once that appointment occurs, WaterRoads-related decisions will benefit from independent oversight, and CobaltBlu (as a WaterRoads shareholder) will have the benefit of that enhanced governance framework.

Resolution 5 — Approval of Series A Pursuit and Associated Implications

RESOLVED that the shareholders of CobaltBlu note, acknowledge, and approve WaterRoads' intention to pursue a Series A equity financing following the Recapitalisation, and the following implications for CobaltBlu as a shareholder:

(a) Dilution of Percentage Shareholding

CobaltBlu's percentage shareholding in WaterRoads will be diluted by the issuance of shares to Series A Investors. The quantum and terms of the Series A financing are to be determined by the WaterRoads board acting in accordance with its fiduciary duties, the New Constitution, and WaterRoads' Conflict of Interest Policy (Version 6.0 – Institutional).

The shareholders acknowledge that:

(i) dilution is a necessary and expected consequence of an equity financing;

(ii) the Series A financing is commercially necessary for WaterRoads to execute its business plan and grow the enterprise value of CobaltBlu's investment;

(iii) while CobaltBlu's percentage ownership will decrease, the absolute value of CobaltBlu's shareholding is expected to increase if the Series A financing is successful and WaterRoads executes its business plan; and

(iv) CobaltBlu will retain the protections afforded by the Founder Protective Provisions (while the F Share Majority holds ≥30%) and the Founder Director appointment right (while the F Share Majority holds ≥20%).

(b) Automatic Conversion of F Shares

Upon completion of a Qualifying Financing, CobaltBlu's 960,000 F Shares will automatically convert into 960,000 Common Shares on a 1:1 basis (clause 4.4 of the New Constitution).

Post-conversion, CobaltBlu will hold Common Shares ranking equally with all other Common Shares (including those held by CBS Group and Series A Investors), subject to:

(i) any preferential rights granted to Series A Investors pursuant to a new class of shares (e.g., Preference Shares) issued in the Series A; and

(ii) the rights and obligations set out in the Shareholders Agreement (see paragraph (c) below).

(c) Entry into Shareholders Agreement

Series A Investors will require WaterRoads, CobaltBlu, CBS Group, and potentially the individual shareholders of CobaltBlu (Sarah Margaret Taylor and Jeffrey Alan Dusting) to enter into a Shareholders Agreement governing:

(i) governance and board composition;

(ii) consent rights and reserved matters (likely including investor consent rights that supplement or override the Founder Protective Provisions);

(iii) information and inspection rights;

(iv) transfer restrictions, rights of first refusal, co-sale rights, and drag-along/tag-along provisions;

(v) anti-dilution protections;

(vi) exit mechanisms and liquidation preferences; and

(vii) other customary venture capital or growth equity investment terms.

The shareholders authorise the directors to negotiate and enter into a Shareholders Agreement on behalf of CobaltBlu, subject to:

(A) the terms being consistent with market practice for Series A financings of similar-stage Australian companies;

(B) the directors obtaining legal advice on the terms and implications for CobaltBlu;

(C) the agreement not imposing obligations on CobaltBlu that are materially prejudicial to CobaltBlu's interests as a shareholder or that would prevent CobaltBlu from complying with its own constitution or applicable law; and

(D) any approval requirements under CobaltBlu's constitution being satisfied (including, if applicable, further shareholder approval if the Shareholders Agreement involves a material commitment or disposition by CobaltBlu).

(d) Further Amendments to WaterRoads Constitution

Series A Investors may require further amendments to the WaterRoads Constitution as a condition of their investment, which may include:

(i) creation of a new class of Preference Shares or Series A Shares with preferential dividend, liquidation, or voting rights;

(ii) modifications to board composition requirements to include investor-nominated directors;

(iii) additional Reserved Matters requiring investor consent; and

(iv) changes to transfer restrictions, pre-emptive rights, and exit provisions.

The shareholders authorise the directors to consent (on behalf of CobaltBlu as a WaterRoads shareholder) to such constitutional amendments, subject to:

(A) the amendments being reasonably necessary to facilitate the Series A financing;

(B) the amendments not materially and adversely affecting CobaltBlu's existing rights under the New Constitution (including the Founder Protective Provisions and Founder Director appointment right) without CobaltBlu's express consent; and

(C) the directors obtaining legal advice if the amendments involve complex or unusual terms.

(e) Final Terms Subject to WaterRoads Board and Governance

The shareholders acknowledge that:

(i) the final terms of the Series A financing (including valuation, amount raised, investor identity, Shareholders Agreement terms, and constitutional amendments) are to be determined by the WaterRoads board acting in accordance with its fiduciary duties to WaterRoads and all its shareholders;

(ii) WaterRoads' Conflict of Interest Policy (Version 6.0 – Institutional) establishes procedures for managing the conflicts of Sarah Taylor and Jeff Dusting in their capacity as WaterRoads directors, including:

recusal where appropriate;

reliance on s194 with full disclosure;

Independent Advice for Material Transactions;

appointment of an independent director at or within 60 days of Series A close to provide disinterested oversight;

(iii) CobaltBlu, as a WaterRoads shareholder, will have rights under the New Constitution (including Founder Protective Provisions and consent rights) and the Shareholders Agreement (once executed) to protect its interests; and

(iv) this resolution provides a general authorisation and acknowledgment of the Series A process, and does not constitute approval of specific Series A terms (which may be subject to further CobaltBlu shareholder or director approval depending on their nature and impact on CobaltBlu).
