Re: Alternate Funding Mechanisms | Proposal for CBS Group Support

As we discussed last week, enclosed is an updated proposal for CBS Group to work with you to further qualify the potential for a range of alternate funding mechanisms to be used to support current operations and initiatives – initially focused upon the TfNSW Fleet Enhancement (HVAC Upgrade).  We have completed a review of the FE04 HVAC Energy Efficiency Report and updated the proposal accordingly.

Context

Situation

Downer has a number of innovations and improvements they want to propose to their clients for mutual benefit.   One recent example is the development of a suite of enhancements for the TfNSW Rolling Stock fleet that included improvements with measurable benefits in reduced maintenance, improved passenger experience & safety, and energy usage.  The HVAC Energy Efficiency initiative is a well-developed initiative with a great deal of pre-existing modelling already completed.

Complication

As is often the case, these innovation and improvement initiatives struggle to progress from concept to project execution.   Often this is due to challenges securing the up-front capital to fund the project, typically because;

the benefits are accrued by others – other departments, customers, the public, etc.

the benefits in operation expense cannot be accrued to create the required capital

Question

The focus upon CO2 emissions reductions has led to the establishment of a number of funding mechanisms designed to provide capex support for energy reduction initiatives.  Are we able to leverage these funding mechanisms to secure the upfront capital needed to mobilise the Sydney Fleet, HVAC Energy Efficiency inoitiative.   And if so, are we able to extend the learnings to determine what the boundaries are for other similar Downer initiatives.

Answer

We propose that Downer engage CBS Group to investigate the feasibility of these funding sources – using of the Fleet Enhancement HVAC Energy Efficiency Initiative (FE04) as the focus for the initial works, providing the basis for subsequent application of these mechanisms in other contexts.

Approach

We propose a 3 stage approach to the engagement as shown in the table below.

Nominated Persons and Terms of Engagement

CBS proposes that Jeff Dusting lead this work.   Jeff has prior experience with Fleet Enhancement Program and team, the applicable schemes, and considerable commercial acumen.  As this is a short-term, part-time engagement, a daily rate of $2200 plus GST applies, billable on a pro-rata basis using an 8 hour day. For ease of budgeting, we propose a capped fee for Stages 1 - 5 of $15,400 plus GST – based upon 7 days work effort.

All travel to/from Sydney is billed at cost, with a daily per diem in accordance with the applicable ATO schedule per diem payable. The current CBS Group Services Agreement terms of engagement, including current 30 day payment terms will continue to apply to this proposal.

We look forward to the opportunity to progress this exciting initiative and would welcome an opportunity to meet and clarify anything outlined in this proposal.

Dear Dave,

Re: Alternate Funding Mechanisms | Sydney Fleet HVAC Initiative | Stage 2

Context

As confirmed last week in our presentation to you, the NSW Energy Savings Scheme (ESS) is likely to provide $7.5m in funding to support the NSW Fleet HVAC Enhancement Proposal – regardless of the overall project funding mechanism.   

Our recommended funding method, the Analysis and Verification method, provides for funding prior to the modification implementation - this funding is an incentive to reduce energy consumption and is independent of project cost.  This mechanism will however require us to work with your team to divide the fleet or modification program into approximately 5 modification tranches.

To secure these funds as soon as possible, we recommend an immediate start to the ESS process for this project, and this proposal outlines the recommended approach.

Key Activities

Broadly, the key actions that must be completed are:

Document
Define and document the project, scope of HVAC modifications, and proposed Analysis and Verification approach.

Program ‘Tranche’ Definition
Work with the Fleet Engineering team to optimise the HVAC modification program and ESS framework to maximise net revenue (cost) of the modification for Downer

Certificate Issuer
Identify a suitable accredited ESS Certificate issuer to review, amend and approve the proposed measurement and verification methodology for this project 

Stakeholder Management
Prepare and support delivery of briefings to TfNSW on the proposed application

Application Management
Manage the application and fulfilment process including the provision of mandated measurement and verification reporting

Approach

As the level of effort and timeframe is uncertain for each of the activities described above, CBS Group proposes a weekly rate of effort engagement.   Under this model:

CBS Group partner and supporting staff will be assigned to these activities at not more than 3 days total per week, and 10 days in any month

We will invoice monthly, on a Time and Materials but not exceeding 10 days in any month without prior approval by Downer

The expected engagement timeframe is 3 months, after which time, the engagement may be extended by mutual agreement.

The following table provides an estimate of the work effort allocation to each key activity and a high level timeframe.

Nominated Persons 

CBS proposes that Jeff Dusting lead this work. Jeff has prior experience with Fleet Enhancement Program and team, the applicable schemes, and considerable commercial acumen. 

The Independent Auditor will be engaged separately at cost, under terms and conditions approved by Downer prior to commencement.

Pricing and Terms of Engagement

Based upon a blended rate of $1900 per day excluding GST, we expect a total engagement fee of $76000 excluding GST.

Any travel to/from Sydney is billed at cost, with a daily per diem in accordance with the applicable ATO schedule per diem payable.

The current CBS Group Services Agreement terms of engagement, including current 30 day payment terms will continue to apply to this proposal.

We look forward to the opportunity to progress this exciting initiative and would welcome an opportunity to clarify anything outlined in this proposal.

Re: Alternate Funding Mechanisms | Sydney Fleet HVAC Initiative | Next Steps

Context

As affirmed last week in our presentation to you, the NSW ESS Scheme, using the Analysis & Verification methodology, is most appropriate energy based funding mechanism for the Sydney Fleet HVAC Initiative.

ESS provides up to $7.5m in funding in advance of installation. This funding is an incentive to reduce energy consumption and is independent of project cost. 

This proposal outlines how CBS Group can support the follow-on actions required to access these funds.

Requirements

Broadly, the actions required to become eligible for ESS funds are:

Define and document the project and scope of HVAC modifications to optimize the application for ESS funds

Provision of an approved auditor to establish a suitable measurement and verification methodology for this project

Ensure the ESS project definition aligns with the requirements of the project implementation 

Prepare and deliver briefing materials for TfNSW on the proposed application

Manage the application and fulfilment process including the provision of mandated measurement and verification reporting*

Approach

CBS Group propose to undertake each of these actions as the second stage of work, noting the requirement to manage the application and fulfilment process including the provision of mandated measurement and verification reporting over time is currently outside the scope of this proposal and would form a third stage as the project goes live.

Nominated Persons and Terms of Engagement

CBS proposes that Jeff Dusting lead this work. Jeff has prior experience with Fleet Enhancement Program and team, the applicable schemes, and considerable commercial acumen. As this is a short-term, part-time engagement, a daily rate of $2200 plus GST applies, billable on a pro-rata basis using an 8-hour day. 

Our estimate is 23 - 35 Days work effort. Estimated cost range is $66,000 - $92,400.

The current CBS Group Services Agreement terms of engagement, including current 30 day payment terms will continue to apply to this proposal.

Any travel to/from Sydney is billed at cost, with a daily per diem in accordance with the applicable ATO schedule per diem payable.
