Recitals

The Discloser may disclose Confidential Information to the Recipient.

In consideration of the Discloser disclosing Confidential Information to the Recipient, the Recipient agrees that it will not use or disclose the Confidential Information except as provided in this Deed Poll.

Definitions

In this Deed Poll:

Confidential Information means:

the existence of this Deed Poll and the fact of discussions between the parties; and

all information, whether disclosed before or after the execution of this Deed Poll, in whatever form and however recorded or stored, relating to the Discloser or the Purpose.

Document includes any note, memorandum, record, report, financial information, summary, analysis, calculation, strategic assessment, market survey, business plan, computer program, computer record, drawing, specification, material or any other means by which information may be stored or reproduced.

Purpose means relating to current or future operations or aspects relating to Spotless and Downer’s commercial interests in the Melbourne Cricket Ground and surrounding Yarra Park Lands and related operations.

Confidentiality and permitted use

The Recipient must:

only use the Confidential Information for the Purpose;

hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose, or cause or permit the disclosure of, the Confidential Information (or the fact that Confidential Information has been received by the Recipient), except as permitted under this Deed Poll;

only create, or cause or permit to be created, a Document which reproduces, is based on, utilizes or relates to Confidential Information if that creation is solely for the Purpose;

keep the Confidential Information and any Documents created in accordance with clause 2(c) secure and

If requested by the Discloser, the Recipient must immediately return

to the Discloser, or destroy or delete as the Discloser directs, all original Documents and copies which:

are or contain Confidential Information; or

reproduce, are based on, utilize or relate to Confidential Information.

Exceptions

The Recipient’s obligations under this Deed Poll do not apply to any Confidential Information that:

the Recipient is required to disclose by any applicable law or legally binding order of any court; or

is in the public domain other than as a result of a breach of this Deed Poll.

If the Recipient must make a disclosure referred to in clause 4(a)(i), then:

the Recipient must disclose only the minimum Confidential Information required to comply with the applicable law, order or requirement; and

before making such disclosure, the Recipient must:

give the Discloser reasonable written notice of the full circumstances of the required disclosure and the Confidential Information which it proposes to disclose; and

consult with the Discloser as to the form of the disclosure.

Operation of this Deed Poll

The Recipient acknowledges that damages may be inadequate compensation for breach of this Deed Poll. Subject to the court’s discretion, the Discloser may seek specific performance, injunctive relief or similar as a remedy for any conduct or threatened conduct which is or would be a breach of this Deed Poll.

This Deed Poll commences on the date that it is executed, and continues for three years.

This Deed Poll is governed by the law of Victoria. Each party irrevocably submits to the non-exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Victoria.
