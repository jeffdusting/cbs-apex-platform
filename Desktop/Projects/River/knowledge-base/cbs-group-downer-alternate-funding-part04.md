Background
CBS Group submitted a proposal to Downer in February 2020 to review opportunities for funding of rolling stock fleet enhancements using the current energy savings schemes available in Australia. 
Downer accepted the proposal and CBS Group provided a report of findings in March 2020. The report considered the current energy savings schemes and provided a recommendation on the scheme considered to be the most suitable to the proposed rolling stock fleet enhancements for the Waratah train fleet in NSW.  The following schemes were evaluated in this report:
Australian Government Emissions Reduction Fund
Australian Government Clean Energy Initiative Fund
Energy Services Company – Energy Performance Contracting
NSW Government Energy Savings Scheme 
CBS Group has subsequently further investigated the details of the recommended scheme and provided a proposal for management and implementation of the processes involved in realizing a revenue stream from the sale of Energy Savings Certificates (ESCs) issued under the NSW Government Energy Savings Scheme working in conjunction with Industry partners.
We have proposed a value share model whereby Downer provides the data and pays CBS Group a recoupable retainer through project development and implementation

The NSW Energy Savings Scheme (ESS) has the potential to deliver funding back to Downer based on energy savings derived from some of the software and hardware upgrades to the Sydney train fleets.
It requires application of a framework, developed for fixed infrastructure, to mobile rolling stock, a new application of the ESS.
The application requires:
an approach that successfully models an increased number of complex variables 
satisfying ESS auditors and successfully managing the disposition of certificates on a live ESS trading platform
To take advantage of the software upgrade, CBS requires a commencement date of no later than 30 November 2020 to be certain Waratah / Sydney Growth Train fleet software modifications are compliant for ESCs.
CBS Group has:
identified a path to manage these factors, and retained a qualified ESC (Energy Saving Certificates) issuer as a partner

### Slide 9
Program Description
CBS’ Partner, Certificate Issuer and Auditors have confirmed:

ApproachThe best approach is a ‘Measurement and Verification’ method treating the fleets as a series of tranches, defined by the number of sets that may be modified in each window prior to Winter or Summer
Brings forward the ESC payments (early as possible)
Allows for software and hardware modifications to be ‘assessed’ as tranches
Downer are the  ‘nominated energy saver’.  The application is independent of TfNSW

Reference ModelCertificate issue requires the development of a baseline energy reference model for each tranche
Data from each set in the tranche post modification needs to be collected, and energy savings determined over both Summer and Winter
Normalisation for passenger loads is permitted

Pilot SetFor the NSW Energy Savings Scheme, the planned pilot set does not provide any useful results or contribution to the verification method.
