Kate Heath

Delivery Manager, Change Manager, Stakeholder & Communications Lead

Professional Profile

Kate is a senior bidding, strategy and delivery management professional with over 20 years of experience across procurement, contract management, project controls and program governance within government, infrastructure, resources, defence and social services sectors across Australia and New Zealand.

Kate’s career has progressively advanced through senior leadership roles at Downer Group, Ventia, Cushman & Wakefield and Broadspectrum, where she held positions including Senior Bid Manager, APAC Bid Governance Manager, Senior Manager of Bidding and General Manager of Precontracts. In these roles, she has been responsible for managing centralised teams delivering major tenders, proposals and contract mobilisations exceeding $20 million, with a focus on capture and strategy development, bespoke delivery solutions, governance and assurance frameworks and improving bid outcomes.

Kate brings proven capability in managing complex, multi-stakeholder programs under tight timeframes, coordinating multidisciplinary teams across geographically dispersed locations and maintaining structured communication channels at all levels. Her experience in governance design, organisational alignment and risk mitigation translates directly to the structured, phased delivery and stakeholder engagement required for the Department of Health’s maintenance KPI implementation program.

Role on This Engagement

As Delivery Manager, Change Manager, Stakeholder & Communications Lead, Kate will be responsible for the day-to-day management of the engagement, ensuring that deliverables are produced on time, within scope and to the required quality standard. She will lead the communication and stakeholder engagement activities across all five phases and manage the governance, reporting and risk management framework for the program.

Key responsibilities on this project include:

Managing the overall delivery program including scheduling, resourcing, risk tracking and milestone reporting across all five phases

Leading the stakeholder engagement plan development and execution, ensuring all health service stakeholders are informed, consulted and aligned throughout the program

Coordinating the Phase 1 kick-off workshops, including scope alignment sessions and initial stakeholder briefings with the Department’s Asset Planning Unit

Managing the Phase 3 co-design workshops, interviews and survey processes across metropolitan, regional and rural health services

Overseeing the Phase 5 change management and communication strategy development and rollout planning

Establishing and maintaining program governance frameworks including reporting cadence, assurance processes and escalation protocols

Providing regular progress reporting to the Department, including dashboards, milestone reviews and risk registers

Nicole Caranguian

Change Manager, Digital Systems & Analytics lead, and Health FM Advisor

Qualifications: University of New England (AU), 2017–2020

Professional Profile

Nicole is a senior technology executive and digital transformation leader with over 20 years of experience in Defence, national security and government ICT. She brings extensive expertise in digital and business transformation, data governance, cyber security assurance, AI strategy and large-scale program delivery across Commonwealth and state government agencies.

Nicole’s career spans a decade of distinguished service in the Royal Australian Navy as a Communications Information Systems specialist, followed by progressive leadership roles at Fujitsu, the Department of Defence (CIOG), Downer Group and xDNA, culminating in her current dual role as Technology Consultant with CBS Group and Director of Applications and Integration at Defence Housing Australia. She also founded the Caranguian Group, a specialist technology consultancy.

Nicole’s strength lies in her ability to translate complex technology and data challenges into structured roadmaps that deliver measurable business outcomes. Her experience developing technology strategies, governance frameworks, business cases and operating models for government agencies is directly relevant to the data collection, validation, benchmarking and digital platform requirements of this engagement. She brings a rigorous, security-conscious approach to data handling, with direct experience in PSPF, SOCI and government information security frameworks, and has published on the NSW AI Assessment Framework covering privacy, security, transparency and accountability in government operations.

Role on This Engagement

As a Change Manager, Digital Systems & Analytics lead, and Health FM Advisor, Nicole will be responsible for the data collection, validation, cleansing and analysis activities across Phases 1 and 2, and will provide ongoing data governance and quality assurance throughout the engagement. She will also advise on digital platforms and tools to support KPI monitoring, reporting and AI-enabled analytics.

Key responsibilities on this project include:

Designing and managing the data collection framework, including self-assessment questionnaires and data extraction processes across health services

Leading the Phase 2 data validation, cleansing and analysis activities to ensure accuracy and comparability across the 77 public health services

Developing data governance protocols to ensure the integrity and security of departmental information throughout the engagement, aligned with the ISSRA and Standard 8b requirements

Advising on digital platforms and tools for KPI dashboards, performance monitoring and reporting, including assessment of AI-enabled analytics opportunities

Supporting the maturity assessment process with data analytics and benchmarking insights to inform health service segmentation

Ensuring compliance with the Department’s information security requirements, drawing on direct experience with PSPF, SOCI and government cyber security frameworks
