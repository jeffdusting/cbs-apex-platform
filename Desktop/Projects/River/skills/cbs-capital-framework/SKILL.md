## CAPITAL Framework Summary

**CAPITAL** stands for **Commercial Asset Performance, Infrastructure Tailoring And Lifecycle**.

It is a long-term asset management and commercial model designed for critical infrastructure. The framework integrates value-based commercial structures with whole-of-life asset lifecycle thinking, delivering measurable savings while maintaining safety and performance standards.

### Core Principles

1. **Value-Based Commercial Structures** — Fixed-fee, performance-linked commercial models that align operator incentives with asset owner outcomes. Risk is allocated to the party best placed to manage it, not simply transferred to the lowest bidder.

2. **Whole-of-Life Lifecycle Thinking** — Asset management decisions are evaluated across the full 30-year lifecycle, not just the immediate maintenance period. Capital renewal, degradation curves, and residual value are factored into every commercial decision.

3. **Systems Engineering Rigour** — Infrastructure systems are modelled as interconnected components with defined failure modes, performance envelopes, and degradation pathways. Maintenance regimes are derived from engineering analysis, not historical precedent alone.

4. **Collaborative Safety Assurance** — Safety outcomes are achieved through collaborative governance between asset owner and operator, with shared risk registers, joint assurance frameworks, and transparent reporting. This aligns with ISO 44001 (Collaborative Business Relationships).

### Reference Implementation — Western Harbour Tunnel

The CAPITAL framework was initially implemented for the Western Harbour Tunnel (WHT) and Sydney Harbour Tunnel, establishing a 30-year fixed-fee asset management, operations, and maintenance framework.

**Key outcome:** The CAPITAL approach delivered an estimated **$180 million in savings** over the contract life compared to traditional procurement models, while improving safety outcomes and asset condition transparency.

The WHT implementation demonstrated that value-based commercial models, when underpinned by rigorous engineering analysis and collaborative governance, outperform traditional lowest-price competitive procurement for complex infrastructure.
